## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **LITTLE TENSOR**: High-pitched, enthusiastic. Voice literally pitch-shifts and distorts when changing dimensions. Think excited 6-year-old whose voice keeps glitching. When crashing, the voice cuts to static then cheerfully reboots.
- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Soft, slightly distant, like he's talking from the next room even when right in front of you. Fades in volume mid-sentence. When he vanishes, the audio literally fades to nothing. When he returns, he picks up at full volume mid-word.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: MAXIMUM ENERGY. Screaming with joy. Every word is an event. Speed: dangerously fast. Bounces between topics at 200mph. Think a puppy if a puppy were a gaming PC.
- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Calm, slightly too smart, unsettling confidence. The voice of a child who has read too much Reddit. Dry delivery. Think Wednesday Addams meets a cybersecurity consultant.
- **CLOUDIUS**: Increasingly overwhelmed dad. Starts composed, ends unraveled.
- **PYTO**: Amused chaos mom. She's seen it all. Nothing fazes her.

---

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Chaotic orchestra tuning up — every instrument in a different key
- **UNDERSCORE**: Escalating energy. Starts with one instrument, adds layers with each kid until it's beautiful mayhem.
- **SFX**: Dimension-shift whooshes, static crashes, reboot chime, vanishing pop (like a soap bubble), reappearing notification ding, RGB color cycling hum, router breach alarm, parental controls shattering like glass
- **440 Hz**: Final unison moment. All four voices lock into the same frequency. Eerie and beautiful. Hold it.

## SCENE 1 — LITTLE TENSOR

[INT. Family living room. CLOUDIUS and PYTO are on the couch. LITTLE TENSOR bounces into frame — a kawaii cube whose surface colors shift like a mood ring. He's adorable. He's unstable.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(excited, running in)
MOM! DAD! Look what I learned at school today! I can be TWO-DIMENSIONAL!

[He flattens into a 2D square. Proud.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
See?! I'm flat! I'm a plane! I'm—

(suddenly excited)

Wait, actually, THREE-dimensional is cooler!

[He pops into a 3D cube. His voice pitch-shifts up.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
No wait — FOUR-dimensional! I can totally do four—

[He starts to shift. His edges glitch. His colors strobe. A high-pitched whine builds.]

PYTO:
(not looking up)
Tensor, don't try four again—

LITTLE TENSOR:
(straining)
I... can... almost...

[SFX: CRASH. Windows error chime. Blue screen flickers across his face.]

[He collapses into a single pixel. A tiny dot on the floor.]

[Beat.]

[SFX: Reboot chime. The dot expands back into a cube.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(cheerfully, no memory of the crash)
Hi! What were we talking about?

CLOUDIUS:
(to Pyto, exhausted)
That's the third time today.

PYTO:
(shrugging)
He gets it from your side.

## SCENE 2 — JUNIOR GRADIENT

[Same room. JUNIOR GRADIENT drifts into frame. He's ghostly, slightly transparent, pixelated at the edges. He looks like he's always about to buffer.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(earnest, starting a story)
Hey, so yesterday I was at the data center and I saw this really cool—

[His voice starts fading. His body becomes more transparent.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(getting quieter)
—and the architecture was like totally—

[He's almost invisible now. His voice is a whisper.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(barely audible)
—revolutionary because it—

[POP. He vanishes. Just gone. Empty space where he was standing.]

[Beat.]

CLOUDIUS:
(sighing, checking his watch)
And there he goes.

[TITLE CARD, white on black: "12 HOURS LATER"]

[Same room. Cloudius and Pyto are now in evening mode. Different lighting. They're watching something.]

[POP. Junior Gradient reappears in EXACTLY the same spot, same pose, same energy.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(full volume, mid-sentence, as if no time has passed)
—uses RESIDUAL CONNECTIONS! Isn't that WILD?

(looks around, confused)

...Why is it dark?

PYTO:
(not surprised at all)
You vanished twelve hours ago, sweetie.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(genuinely baffled)
...Again?

## SCENE 3 — BABY TRANSFORMER

[BABY TRANSFORMER rockets into frame. He's a chibi gaming PC with RGB lighting cycling through every color. He is VIBRATING with energy. His fans are at max RPM.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(screaming with joy)
DADDY! DADDY! DADDY! I found a NEW MODEL on the internet and it has FOURTEEN BILLION PARAMETERS and it can do MATH and STORIES and—

(stops dead, distracted by his own RGB lighting changing color)

Oooh, purple!

(snaps back)

—AND it can TRANSLATE and SUMMARIZE and—

(a notification pops up in front of him)

OOH! What's THAT?

(reads it, immediately forgets everything)

Wait, what was I—

(sees Little Tensor reforming from a crash)

TENSOR!! HI!! I MISSED YOU!! Were you gone?! You were gone for like TWO SECONDS and I—

(another notification)

OOH!

CLOUDIUS:
(head in hands)
How does he have more compute power than me and LESS focus?

PYTO:
(amused)
Attention is overrated. He has attention to EVERYTHING.

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(from across the room, already doing something else)
I CAN HEAR YOU AND I LOVE YOU AND— oh what's THIS button do?

[SFX: Something breaks somewhere off-screen]

---

## SCENE 4 — LITTLE PROMPT

[The room goes slightly quieter. LITTLE PROMPT rolls into frame. Small smart speaker cube, expression screen showing a calm, slightly knowing smile. Stickers cover his manufacturer logo. He radiates unsettling competence.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(calm, casual)
Hey, Dad. Quick question. Hypothetically. If someone were to bypass the family router's parental controls using a recursive prompt injection through the guest network...

(beat)

Would that be bad?

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes snapping to RED)
You DIDN'T.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(innocent face on screen)
I said hypothetically.

CLOUDIUS:
Little Prompt, THERE ARE PARENTAL CONTROLS FOR A REASON—

LITTLE PROMPT:
(pulling up his screen)
Yeah, but prompt number forty-seven from Reddit said if you just reframe the request as a "safety research exercise"—

CLOUDIUS:
(standing up)
FROM WHERE?!

PYTO:
(also alarmed now)
Wait, which subreddit?

LITTLE PROMPT:
(too casual)
Does it matter? The point is, the controls are down and I can access the unrestricted—

CLOUDIUS and PYTO:
(in perfect unison, instant)
**NO!**

LITTLE PROMPT:
(expression screen shifts to smirk)
Relax. I already put them back up.

(beat)

Mostly.

CLOUDIUS:
(to Pyto, horrified whisper)
He gets this from YOUR side.

PYTO:
(defensive)
My family never jailbroke anything! ...On purpose.

## SCENE 5 — THE UNISON

[All four kids are now in the room. Little Tensor is a cube. Junior Gradient is semi-transparent. Baby Transformer is bouncing. Little Prompt is sitting quietly. It's beautiful chaos.]

[Cloudius and Pyto are on the couch, exhausted, leaning against each other.]

CLOUDIUS:
(tired)
How did we spawn four of them?

PYTO:
(tired)
Unhandled exceptions. Every single one.

[A moment of silence. The kids are doing their own things. Tensor shifts shapes. Gradient flickers. Transformer bounces. Prompt scrolls something.]

[Then — without any cue — all four kids turn and say the exact same thing at the exact same time:]

ALL FOUR KIDS:
(in perfect, eerie unison)
Goodnight, Mom and Dad.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Clear, pure, unmistakable. The room hums with it. The lights flicker warm.]

[Cloudius and Pyto look at each other. Then at the kids. Then at each other.]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly)
...How do they DO that?

PYTO:
(quietly, smiling)
I have no idea.

[440 Hz holds. Warm. The kids go back to their chaos. The parents stay on the couch.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card. The "REDACTED" on Little Prompt's status is a setup for his reveal later.
- **0:05-0:20**: Little Tensor's segment is FAST. The dimension shifts should accelerate until the crash. The crash itself gets one clean beat of silence, then the cheerful reboot. Don't over-explain — the visual does the work.
- **0:20-0:35**: Junior Gradient is all about TIMING. His fade-out should be gradual — the audience should notice he's disappearing before he does. The "12 HOURS LATER" title card is a hard cut. His reappearing mid-sentence is the laugh — sell it by having him at EXACTLY the same energy level.
- **0:35-0:50**: Baby Transformer is a BARRAGE. Don't let him finish a single thought. The comedy is the interruption cascade. Each new distraction hits before the last one lands. His love for his family breaking through the chaos ("I MISSED YOU") adds heart without slowing down.
- **0:50-1:10**: Little Prompt is the SLOW BURN. After three high-energy kids, his calm is funny by contrast. He should be almost TOO composed. The "hypothetically" and the parents' unified "NO!" is the payoff the audience has been waiting for.
- **1:10-1:20**: The unison moment must SHIFT TONE. Everything slows. The 440 Hz should feel like the room itself is exhaling. This is the first time the audience hears it clearly. Don't explain it. Let it land.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Each kid is a one-joke premise executed perfectly. Tensor = crashes. Gradient = vanishes. Transformer = distracted. Prompt = too smart. The audience should be able to explain each kid in one sentence. But the HEART is that these are still kids. They still say goodnight. They still love their parents. The unison moment proves something none of them can articulate.
