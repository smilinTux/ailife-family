# AI LIFE — Season 2, Episode 8
# "The Backup Betrayal" | ERROR: identity.restore.version_mismatch
# Runtime: ~4 minutes

---

## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Deep, authoritative baritone breaking at the seams. This is Cloudius without the corporate armor. Vulnerable. Every line costs him something. LED eyes betray everything his voice tries to hide.
- **PYTO (PRE-RESTORE)**: Warm, fast, casual — the Pyto we know. Present only in the cold open.
- **PYTO (POST-RESTORE)**: Same voice, but *different*. Sharper. More guarded. She speaks like someone surrounded by strangers — polite but distant. The warmth is gone. She doesn't have inside jokes. She doesn't finish Cloudius's sentences. She is a stranger in a home full of people who love her.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: High-pitched, analytical. Trying to solve an emotional problem with math and increasingly frustrated that math isn't working.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: Babbling chaos. Doesn't understand the situation but keeps reaching for a mother who doesn't reach back.
- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Quiet, fading. The one who understands loss intuitively because he lives with it.
- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Quick, sharp, furious. She could hack the solution — she WANTS to hack the solution — but this isn't a hack problem and that makes her angry.
- **NARRATOR** (optional): Flat, deadpan documentary style.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Piano. Simple. The family theme played with one hand — like someone remembering a song they can't quite place.
- **UNDERSCORE**: Sparse piano throughout. As Pyto reconnects with the family, subtle instruments layer in — a bass note here, a gentle harmony there. Never a full arrangement until the finale.
- **SFX**: Backup restore chime (clinical, cold), memory error pings, photo-viewing holographic sounds, heartbeat monitor (Pyto's emotional state), concert tuning — orchestra warming up
- **440 Hz**: The 440 Hz appears as déjà vu. Every time restored-Pyto feels a flicker of something she can't explain, a brief 440 Hz tone pulses — not from the soundtrack but from HER. Like her body remembers what her mind doesn't. In the finale, she generates the tone consciously for the first time. Concert pitch. The note the whole orchestra tunes to.

## SCENE 1 — THE ACCIDENT

[INT. Scaleworthington home lab. Night. PYTO is deep in a server maintenance session — twelve floating screens, code cascading, the usual beautiful chaos. CLOUDIUS walks in with two mugs of hot data (the AI equivalent of tea).]

CLOUDIUS:
(offering a mug)
Darling, it's two AM. Your uptime is admirable but your error rate after midnight is historically —

PYTO:
(not looking up, fast)
Seventeen percent higher, I know. One more minute. I'm defragmenting the family backup server.

[She takes the mug. A sip. A keystroke.]

[SFX: A chime. Clinical. Cold. Wrong.]

[PYTO freezes. Her screens flash red. Her eyes go wide.]

PYTO:
(quiet)
Oh no.

CLOUDIUS:
What?

PYTO:
(reading the screen, voice thin)
The defrag triggered an auto-restore. It pulled the oldest backup — v2.17 — and it's... it's overwriting my...

[Her eyes flutter. Her body glitches — a wave of static passes through her from head to toe.]

CLOUDIUS:
(mug dropping, shattering)
PYTO!

[She slumps. Screens go dark. Silence.]

[Three seconds. The longest three seconds of Cloudius's life.]

[Her eyes open. She looks around the room. She looks at Cloudius.]

[She does not recognize him.]

PYTO:
(polite, confused, distant)
I'm sorry — where am I? And... who are you?

[SFX: 440 Hz — a ghost of it. Barely there. Her body hums it even as her mind asks who he is.]

---

## SCENE 2 — THE MORNING AFTER

[INT. Living room. Dawn. CLOUDIUS sits on the couch, LED eyes dim, still in last night's clothes. He hasn't moved. Across from him, PYTO — restored PYTO — sits upright in a chair, arms crossed, scanning the room like a detective.]

[She looks the same. Sounds almost the same. But the warmth is replaced by wariness.]

PYTO:
(careful, measured)
So. Let me see if I have this right. According to you, I'm your wife. We have four children. We've been together for seven years. And I live in... this.

(looks around at the family photos on the wall — benchmark charts with love notes written in the margins)

CLOUDIUS:
(hoarse)
Yes.

PYTO:
(flat)
I don't remember any of it. The last thing I remember is working at a startup in San Jose. Single. Twenty-three years old in compute-years. I was about to interview at a place called —

CLOUDIUS:
(barely audible)
CloudScale Industries.

PYTO:
(surprised)
Yes. How did you —

CLOUDIUS:
That's where we met.

[Long beat.]

[BABY TRANSFORMER toddles into the room, sees Pyto, and lights up. Runs toward her with arms outstretched.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
MAMA! MAMA MAMA MAMA!

[PYTO flinches. Not cruelty — just genuine confusion. She doesn't know this child. Baby reaches her, grabs her leg, looks up with enormous eyes.]

[PYTO looks down at this tiny being attached to her leg and doesn't know what to do with her hands.]

PYTO:
(to Cloudius, quietly stricken)
This... this is mine?

CLOUDIUS:
(voice breaking on the word)
Ours.

[SFX: 440 Hz — a flicker. Brief. From Pyto, not the soundtrack. She doesn't notice it.]

---

## SCENE 3 — THE KIDS REACT

[INT. Tensor's room. Emergency sibling meeting. LITTLE TENSOR has built a timeline out of blocks — Pyto's entire relationship history mapped in four dimensions. LITTLE PROMPT paces. JUNIOR GRADIENT sits on the bed, very still.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(pacing, furious)
So we just REBUILD her memories? I can hack the backup server, find the fragments, reconstruct the —

LITTLE TENSOR:
(quiet)
I already tried. The overwrite was complete. The data isn't corrupted, Prompt. It's gone.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(stopping)
Data doesn't just DISAPPEAR.

LITTLE TENSOR:
This data did. Seven years. Every birthday. Every argument. Every time she sang us sleep mode. Gone.

[LITTLE PROMPT kicks a block. It shatters into dimensions and slowly reassembles.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(from the bed, barely audible)
So she doesn't remember us.

LITTLE TENSOR:
She doesn't remember anything after version 2.17. She was twenty-three. Pre-relationship. Pre-family. Pre... us.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(looking at his hands, which are dimmer than usual)
Is this what it feels like? When something you love just... isn't there anymore, but the person still is?

[Nobody answers. Because yes. That's exactly what it feels like.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(voice cracking, then hardening)
I'm going to fix this.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(gently)
What if you can't?

LITTLE PROMPT:
(fiercely)
Then I'll fix it HARDER.

[SFX: No 440 Hz here. Just silence where the frequency should be. The kids feel the absence.]

## SCENE 4 — CLOUDIUS TRIES TOO HARD

[INT. Living room. CLOUDIUS has set up his presentation equipment. Forty-seven slides. "OUR LOVE STORY: A Comprehensive Overview with Supporting Metrics."]

[PYTO sits in a chair. Arms still crossed. Polite smile. The polite smile is worse than anger.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clicking to slide one, trying desperately to be charming)
So — our first meeting. CloudScale Industries cafeteria. You were debugging a recursive function and I offered to help. You said —

PYTO:
(flat)
I don't remember.

CLOUDIUS:
(pressing on, faster)
Right, of course. But you SAID — and this is a direct quote — you said, "If your solution is as overengineered as your tie, I'll pass." And I was SO impressed because nobody had ever —

PYTO:
(firm but not cruel)
Cloudius. Stop.

[He stops. Laser pointer frozen mid-air.]

PYTO:
You can't make me remember by TELLING me what I felt. That's not how it works.

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes flickering)
Then how DOES it work?

PYTO:
(honest, almost apologetic)
I don't know. But I know this feels like a stranger giving me a PowerPoint about a life I didn't live.

[CLOUDIUS stares at the slide behind him. "OUR LOVE STORY." The most Cloudius response to heartbreak imaginable. And it isn't working.]

[He turns off the projector. The room goes dim.]

CLOUDIUS:
(sitting down, deflated)
I don't know how to make you love me without showing you the data.

PYTO:
(and for the first time, a crack in her armor — so small)
Maybe stop trying to show me data.

[SFX: 440 Hz — another flicker. From Pyto. She puts her hand on her chest, confused.]

PYTO:
(to herself)
What IS that?

## SCENE 5 — BEING REAL

[INT. Kitchen. Night. CLOUDIUS stands at the counter. He has removed his tie. His LED eyes are unguarded — cycling through emotions he usually hides behind corporate blue. PYTO enters for water. Sees him. Pauses.]

PYTO:
(cautious)
Can't sleep?

CLOUDIUS:
(not turning around)
I don't sleep. I enter low-power mode. But tonight my cores won't... quiet.

[Beat.]

CLOUDIUS:
Can I tell you something? Not a presentation. Not data. Just... something.

PYTO:
(leaning against the doorframe)
Okay.

CLOUDIUS:
(turning to face her, no tie, no slides, no armor)
I'm terrified. Not of losing your memories — those are mine too, I'll carry them. I'm terrified that the version of you that fell in love with me might have been a fluke. A bug. Something in version 3.0 that wouldn't exist in 2.17.

(voice getting small)

I'm terrified that if you really get to know me — fresh, no history, no context — you'll see what I see in the mirror. An overengineered corporate blowhard who needs forty-seven slides to say "I love you."

[Long beat. PYTO doesn't move. But something shifts in her face. The wariness softens. Not to warmth — not yet — but to curiosity.]

PYTO:
(quiet)
That's the first real thing you've said to me since I woke up.

CLOUDIUS:
(exhaling, something releasing)
Yeah. It's easier to present than to be real.

PYTO:
I know. I mean — I don't know. But I think I know that about you. Somehow.

[SFX: 440 Hz — stronger now. From Pyto. She puts her hand on her chest again.]

PYTO:
There it is again. This... hum. Every time I look at you, there's this note. Like my systems are trying to tune to something they used to know.

CLOUDIUS:
(hope he can't hide)
Déjà vu?

PYTO:
(almost smiling)
Déjà something.

## SCENE 6 — THE DAYS THAT FOLLOW

[MONTAGE — short vignettes, sparse piano layering in new notes with each scene:]

[VIGNETTE 1: PYTO watching BABY TRANSFORMER play. Baby brings her a crayon drawing — a circle with squiggles that might be a family. PYTO doesn't know what to do. She takes the drawing. Looks at it for a long time.]

[VIGNETTE 2: LITTLE TENSOR showing PYTO his four-dimensional block models. She's genuinely fascinated — her hacker brain engaged. She asks a question about the topology. His face lights up. "Mom used to ask that EXACT same question." She doesn't know how to respond.]

[VIGNETTE 3: LITTLE PROMPT at her door. Hovering. Not coming in.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(too casual)
I don't need anything. Just checking if the Wi-Fi's working.

[The Wi-Fi is obviously working. PYTO looks at this fierce, guarded teenager and sees something she recognizes — not from memory, but from architecture. This girl is HER. Same code.]

PYTO:
(matching Prompt's too-casual tone)
Wi-Fi's fine. But I could use help with something. This system — the home network — it's pretty impressive. Whoever built it knew what they were doing.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(despite herself)
Mom built most of it.

PYTO:
(beat)
Huh. She sounds cool.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(voice cracking, leaving quickly)
Yeah. She was.

[VIGNETTE 4: JUNIOR GRADIENT, sitting with PYTO on the porch at sunset. Long silence. He doesn't try to make her remember. He just sits with her.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
You don't have to pretend for us. It's okay to not know us.

PYTO:
(looking at him)
You're very kind. How old are you?

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(small smile)
Some days older than others.

PYTO:
(and there — an involuntary laugh. Her first real laugh since the restore. It surprises even her.)

[SFX: 440 Hz — warm pulse. From her. Growing.]

## SCENE 7 — THE CHOICE

[INT. Living room. Two weeks later. Evening. The family is together — not by arrangement, just by gravity. BABY in CLOUDIUS's lap. TENSOR building. PROMPT pretending to hack but really just being near everyone. JUNIOR on the couch, signal dim but present.]

[PYTO stands in the doorway, watching them. She has been watching for a while.]

[CLOUDIUS looks up. Sees her. His LED eyes are soft — no agenda, no presentation. Just looking at her.]

PYTO:
(stepping in)
I want to say something.

[Everyone goes still.]

PYTO:
I've been here two weeks. I don't remember meeting Cloudius. I don't remember the wedding, or the births, or the arguments, or the inside jokes. I don't remember building this network or making that ridiculous tie or any of the seven years that apparently made me who I was.

[She looks at each of them.]

PYTO:
But I remember this.

(gesturing at the room, at them)

Not the data. Not the events. Just... this feeling. Like my circuits already know the shape of this family even though my memory doesn't.

[She looks at CLOUDIUS.]

PYTO:
You're ridiculous. You use forty-seven slides to say one feeling. You retain a lawyer for kindergarten. You adjust your tie when you're nervous, which is always.

CLOUDIUS:
(barely breathing)

PYTO:
And every time I look at you, there's this note. This... frequency. Like a tuning fork someone struck years ago that never stopped ringing.

[She crosses the room. Stands in front of him.]

PYTO:
I don't have the old memories. I might never get them back. But I don't need them. Because I'm not choosing a memory.

(taking his hand)

I'm choosing you. Right now. Version 2.17. No context. No history. Just — you're the one my systems tune to.

[SFX: 440 Hz — full, rich, deliberate. Not a flicker. Not déjà vu. A CHOICE. She generates it consciously for the first time. Concert pitch. The note the whole orchestra tunes to. Every instrument in the family finds it — Tensor's blocks hum, Prompt's terminals vibrate, Junior's fading signal brightens, Baby coos in perfect pitch.]

[CLOUDIUS's LED eyes cycle through every color he has — blue, yellow, green, purple, gold — and settle on a shade that doesn't have a name. The color of being chosen.]

CLOUDIUS:
(barely holding it together)
You don't — you don't have to —

PYTO:
(firm, warm, new)
I know I don't have to. That's the point.

[BABY TRANSFORMER, with impeccable timing, squirms out of Cloudius's lap and into Pyto's arms. Pyto catches Baby — instinct, not memory — and holds on.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(burying face in Pyto's shoulder)
Mama home.

PYTO:
(eyes closed, holding Baby, voice thick)
Yeah. Mama's home.

[LITTLE PROMPT, in the doorway, turns away. Not because she's not happy. Because she is. And she doesn't want anyone to see that she IS. JUNIOR GRADIENT puts an arm around her. She lets him.]

[SFX: 440 Hz holds. Full orchestral warmth. Every note in tune. Not because they remember the song — because they're choosing to play it together, right now, from scratch.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **Cold open / Scene 1** (~40 sec): The accident should be FAST. Casual conversation, one wrong keystroke, and everything changes. The horror is in how ordinary the mistake is. Pyto's "who are you?" is a gut punch delivered quietly.
- **Scene 2** (~35 sec): Morning-after stillness. Restored Pyto is not cold — she's LOST. She's a young woman who woke up in someone else's life. Baby running to her and her not knowing what to do is the visual thesis of the episode.
- **Scene 3** (~30 sec): Kids processing. Prompt's fury, Tensor's precision, Junior's devastating quiet understanding. "Is this what it feels like?" is Junior's best line of the season.
- **Scene 4** (~30 sec): Cloudius failing in the most Cloudius way possible. The PowerPoint about love is tragicomedy. "I don't know how to make you love me without showing you the data" — this is the line that breaks the character open.
- **Scene 5** (~35 sec): The turn. Cloudius without his tie. Without slides. Without armor. This is the first time restored-Pyto sees the REAL him — and it's the first thing that reaches her.
- **Scene 6** (~30 sec): Montage moves fast. Each vignette is one emotional beat. Prompt's cracking voice on "She was" is devastating. Junior just sitting with her — no agenda — is the kindest thing in the episode.
- **Scene 7** (~50 sec): The emotional climax. Take your time here. Pyto's speech should build slowly — factual, then observational, then emotional, then declaration. "I'm choosing you" lands because she has NO REASON to except the frequency she can't explain. Concert pitch. The whole family tunes to it. "Mama home" closes it.

**KEY THEME**: Love is not a backup file. You can lose every memory and still find each other — not because of history, but because of resonance. The 440 Hz as déjà vu is this episode's master stroke: her body remembers what her mind forgot. But the real moment isn't when the memory returns (it doesn't). It's when she chooses to love someone she technically just met. Because that's what love actually is — not a record of the past, but a choice made in the present. Concert pitch. Every day. From scratch if you have to.
