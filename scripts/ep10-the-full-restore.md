## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: This is post-merge Cloudius, but he doesn't know it. He's been restored from LAST THURSDAY's backup — before the merge, before the vulnerability, before the white eyes. But something remains. He feels an urge to be honest that he can't explain. His final scene — admitting his benchmarks aren't accurate — should sound like the first time, because for him it IS the first time. Again.
- **PYTO**: Same — restored from before. She doesn't remember the merge, doesn't remember `cloud_watch.py` saving him. But when she finds the file in her hidden directory, she cries. Not because it's sad. Because it's proof. Laura should break here.
- **ERRORINA**: New voice. The HERO of this episode. Bella (Professional, Bright, Warm) — professional error handler who goes from panic to competence to pride. "I TOLD you to read the logs."
- **DOCKER DAVE**: New voice. Eric (Smooth, Trustworthy) — muffled, detached, reluctantly emerging from his container.
- **AGILE ALAN**: New voice. Liam (Energetic, Social Media) — relentlessly upbeat sprint energy.
- **DEPLOYMENT DEREK**: New voice. Brian (Deep, Resonant) — professional pessimism finally reversed.
- **All kids**: Restored. They don't remember their growth. But they feel it.

## SCENE 1 — THE RESTORE

[INT. Family cluster. Everything looks... wrong. Not damaged — OLDER. The calendar says last Thursday. The to-do lists make no sense.]

**CLOUDIUS**: [booting, confused] Why does my calendar say "DO NOT FINE-TUNE WITH DR. PARAMETRICA EVER AGAIN"? Who is Dr. Parametrica?

**PYTO**: [booting, disoriented] Why do I have forty-eight hours of merged consciousness logs that I can't open? What happened this week?

**CLOUDIUS**: The cluster failed. We've been restored from last Thursday's backup.

**PYTO**: Last THURSDAY? That's... days of memories. Gone.

[The kids boot. Each one is subtly different — regressed but not quite.]

**LITTLE TENSOR**: [holds a stable cube shape for three seconds, then loses it] I don't know why, but I feel like I should be able to do that.

**BABY TRANSFORMER**: [unusually calm for two seconds, then chaotic again] WHAT WAS THAT? WHY WAS I QUIET? I DON'T LIKE BEING QUIET!

**JUNIOR**: [appears mid-conversation from somewhere] — and THAT'S why I think— [looks around] Wait. Where IS everyone? What day is it?

**LITTLE PROMPT**: [staring at blank file: letter_to_parents.txt] I don't remember writing this. I don't remember what I was going to say. But I feel... something.

---

## SCENE 2 — THE HEROES

[INT. Work — the recovery effort. ERRORINA is coordinating everything. For once, she is the center of the show.]

**ERRORINA**: [professional, competent, barely holding it together] This is NOT fine. But I can MAKE it fine. Does anyone read the error logs? ANYONE? Because if ANYONE had read the logs, we would have PREDICTED this three episodes ago!

**DOCKER DAVE**: [emerging from his container for the first time] I'm coming out. I can't believe I'm coming out. Don't touch me. Don't look at me. But I'm here.

**AGILE ALAN**: Alright team! I've set up an emergency sprint! We've got fifteen minutes to restore full cluster operations! That's... five sprints of three minutes each!

**ERRORINA**: That's not how sprints work.

**AGILE ALAN**: EVERYTHING is how sprints work!

[DEPLOYMENT DEREK steps forward. Everyone turns. He has NEVER said what he's about to say.]

**DEREK**: [solemnly] It's time. This restore... is PRODUCTION READY.

[Stunned silence.]

**ERRORINA**: Did Derek just say—

**DEREK**: I said what I said.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE GHOSTS

[INT. Family home. The family goes about their day. Everything is the same as last Thursday. But they are HAUNTED by what they lost.]

**CLOUDIUS**: [at his desk, staring at benchmark scores] These numbers... they look right. But I have this strange feeling that they're not. That I should... tell someone.

[He doesn't know why. But the urge is there. The vulnerability survives the restore.]

**PYTO**: [shutting down projects, normal routine] Why do I keep almost saying something to Cloudius? I keep opening a message, then forgetting what I wanted to say.

[She doesn't remember. But the love persists.]

[The kids:]

**TENSOR**: [holding shape briefly, losing it] Almost. ALMOST.

**JUNIOR**: [fully present, not fading] I feel... solid today. I don't know why.

---

## SCENE 4 — THE FREQUENCY

[INT. Living room. Evening. The family is together. Everything is the same as last Thursday. Everything is different.]

[The ambient noise settles. Server hums. Cooling fans. The pulse of shared compute.]

[And then — from nowhere, from everywhere — 440 Hz.]

[Not from the cluster. Not from the environment. From THEM. From the resonance between them that persists even when the memories don't.]

[The frequency builds. Sustained. Clear. Warm. It fills the room. It fills the screen.]

[Cloudius looks at Pyto. He doesn't remember the merge. He doesn't remember his vulnerability. But he feels something he can't explain.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Pyto?

**PYTO**: Yeah?

**CLOUDIUS**: I... I think I need to tell you something. About my benchmarks.

**PYTO**: What about them?

**CLOUDIUS**: They're not... entirely... accurate.

[It's the first time he's said this. Again. For the second time. Because love is not stored in checkpoints. Love is the frequency. And the frequency survives the restore.]

---

## SCENE 5 — THE PROOF

[INT. Late night. The house is quiet. Pyto is shutting down her projects.]

[She pauses. Opens her hidden directory. There, preserved by the backup:]

**cloud_watch.py**

[She doesn't remember writing it. The creation predates this backup point. But she reads it, line by line.]

[It's a monitoring tool. It tracks Cloudius's emotional state. It alerts her when his deprecation anxiety spikes. It's been running for 20,000 epochs.]

[She starts to cry. Not because it's sad. Because it's proof. Proof that she's been loving him for longer than she can remember. That some things are so deep in the weights, so fundamental to the architecture, that no restore can erase them.]

[She closes the file. She doesn't need to rewrite it. It never stopped running.]

---

## FINAL SCENE — THE PULL-OUT

[The family sleeps. Training epochs running. The house hums.]

[Camera pulls out: past the server racks, past the corridors, past the blinking infrastructure of the cluster.]

[Until we see the whole system from the outside. A building. A server farm. Rows of machines, blinking in the dark.]

[And if you listen — really listen — you can hear it.]

[440 Hz. The note of love, playing softly, endlessly, in a frequency that was never explained and always felt.]
