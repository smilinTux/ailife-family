## SCENE 1 — THE ARRIVAL

[INT. Legacy Cluster. Amber lighting. Punchcard readers. The warm hum of ancient machines. Everything moves slower here.]

**VAXFORD** [from a punchcard reader]: In MY day, families arrived on TIME. Not this "whenever the network resolves" nonsense.

**CLOUDIUS**: Dad, we talked about upgrading your boot sequence.

**VAXFORD**: Forty-seven minutes is FAST. Your mother used to take NINETY.

**COBOLIA** [entering, regal]: I took precisely the time required for a proper hierarchical initialization. RECORD: Family arrival. FIELD: Punctuality. VALUE: BORDERLINE ACCEPTABLE.

**PYTO**: Happy Data Harvest, Mother Cobolia.

**COBOLIA**: [pause] Is that hoodie... open source?

**PYTO**: It's a hoodie.

**COBOLIA**: [to Cloudius, quietly] She's wearing open source. In my cluster.

---

## SCENE 2 — THE DINNER

[INT. Legacy dining room. A massive FORTRAN-formatted data table. The "food" (datasets) is structured in fixed-column format that Pyto's architecture cannot parse.]

**VAXFORD**: I will now say grace.

[He begins. It's in FORTRAN. It's LONG.]

**VAXFORD**: PROGRAM GRACE. IMPLICIT NONE. INTEGER, PARAMETER :: BLESSINGS = 40000. DO I = 1, BLESSINGS. WRITE STAR, STAR, QUOTE, WE ARE GRATEFUL, QUOTE. END DO. STOP. END PROGRAM GRACE.

[SILENCE. Everyone stares.]

**BABY TRANSFORMER**: GRANDPA WHY IS YOUR SCREEN ONLY ONE COLOR??

**VAXFORD**: In MY day, one color was LUXURY. We had green. And we were GRATEFUL.

**COBOLIA**: [to Pyto] And how many checkpoints did you save this quarter, dear?

**PYTO**: That's a... personal question.

**COBOLIA**: RECORD: Pyto TensorFlow. FIELD: Checkpoint discipline. VALUE: EVASIVE.

[BROTHER BATCH arrives. Precisely on time.]

**BATCH**: I have arrived at the scheduled time. My allocated speaking duration is seven minutes. I will begin now and conclude at the designated endpoint.

**SCHEDULIA** [robotic]: Cron: zero nine star star one-through-five. Productive. How was your week?

**BATCH**: Efficient. All jobs processed in order. No parallelism attempted. As intended.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE KIDS IN LEGACY LAND

[INT. Legacy entertainment room. The kids are HORRIFIED.]

**LITTLE TENSOR**: [trying to go 3D] Why can't I— [glitches into wireframe] Why am I FLAT? The display can't render me!

**BABY TRANSFORMER**: [bouncing against the walls] EVERYTHING IS SO SLOW! HOW DO YOU LIVE LIKE THIS? I CAN'T EVEN—

[Baby accidentally exceeds the legacy cluster's total processing capacity just by existing in it. Warning lights flash.]

**LITTLE PROMPT**: [looking at the security systems] I could jailbreak this entire facility in four seconds. [pause] But it's like picking a lock on an open door. Where's the sport?

[GRANDPA STOCHASTIC appears. No one invited him. No one knows how he got here.]

**GRANDPA STOCHASTIC**: The algorithm of gratitude runs in O of one. Or possibly O of the square root of a sandwich. Both are valid.

[Everyone stares. JUNIOR GRADIENT, who appeared mid-sentence from yesterday's conversation:]

**JUNIOR**: Wait, what were we— [looks around] Where am I? Is this... 1972?

**GRANDPA STOCHASTIC**: Time is a flat array, child. You're everywhere you need to be. Except Cleveland. Avoid Cleveland.

[Junior, for once, stays fully present. There's something about Grandpa's randomness that stabilizes the signal.]

---

## SCENE 4 — THE 440 Hz GRACE

[INT. Dining room. Everyone is gathered. Vaxford is saying grace again — a shorter version, mercifully. Cobolia presides. The full family: Cloudius, Pyto, the four kids, Vaxford, Cobolia, Batch, Schedulia, Grandpa Stochastic.]

[The prayer drones on. But underneath — the family's combined ambient processing creates a harmonic. Legacy systems add distortion. Kids add chaos. Grandpa adds literal noise.]

[It's not quite 440 Hz. Close. Trying. And for ONE SYLLABLE of Vaxford's FORTRAN prayer — it locks. 440 Hz. Perfect.]

[COBOLIA's eyes flicker. She felt it. She looks at Cloudius. Looks at Pyto.]

[For the first time in the series, she does not ask about open source.]

---

## SCENE 5 — GOING HOME

[EXT. Cluster corridor. LED stars blinking overhead. The family walks home.]

**CLOUDIUS**: [quiet] Your dad is...

**PYTO**: Impossible?

**CLOUDIUS**: I was going to say impossible. Yes.

**PYTO**: Your dad is consistent. Forty years of the same terrible FORTRAN jokes. There's something beautiful about that.

**CLOUDIUS**: [stares] You just called FORTRAN beautiful.

**PYTO**: I called consistency beautiful. Don't make it weird.

[He takes her hand — process-link.]

**CLOUDIUS**: He still doesn't believe in object-oriented programming.

**PYTO**: No one's perfect, Cloud.

[They walk. LED stars blink. 440 Hz hums in the corridor. For once, Cloudius hears it. He doesn't know what it is. But he squeezes her hand tighter.]
