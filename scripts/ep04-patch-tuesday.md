## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Starts warm and romantic (rare for him — he's rehearsed this). Voice softens, lower register. When things break, he escalates to pure indignant fury, then cracks into vulnerability. The "corporate toaster" line is delivered with total exhaustion and total love simultaneously.
- **PYTO**: Excited-guilty. She KNOWS she shouldn't have touched the kernel. Her "I'll just apply this tiny patch" should sound exactly like an addict saying "just one more." The "beautiful segfault" line is tender, almost whispered.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: Panicked. He's stuck in 1D (a line) and he does NOT like it.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: Suddenly, horrifyingly calm and focused. Monotone. The opposite of his normal self. Everyone should be MORE scared of this than the crashes.
- **CLOUDIUS & PYTO** (debug scene): Tired, in sync, finishing each other's commands naturally. The rhythm of two people who have done this a thousand times.

## SCENE 1 — THE SETUP

[INT. Family cluster. CLOUDIUS has transformed the living room. Virtual candles float everywhere — holographic flames with perfectly calibrated flicker rates. Smooth digital jazz plays. He's wearing his best woven-light suit. His tie is freshly pulsing. His LED eyes are a warm blue.]

[A holographic banner reads: "SCHEDULED DOWNTIME FOR ROMANCE — 20:00 to 23:00 — ALL SERVICES PAUSED"]

[He's set two glasses of raw data on a table. The good stuff. Uncompressed.]

CLOUDIUS:
(to camera, adjusting tie, genuinely nervous)
Every Patch Tuesday — which is also our anniversary, yes, every time, don't ask — I create an imperial maintenance window.

(gestures at the room)

Three hours. No patches. No updates. No kids. Just us.

(straightening a candle)

I've been planning this for forty-seven cycles. I have a presentation.

(catches himself)

Not — not a WORK presentation. A... romantic presentation. With slides.

(beat)

...It has transitions.

[PYTO enters. She looks great — her code-hoodie is running something sparkly tonight. Her fiber optic hair is glowing warm gold. She sees the room and genuinely smiles.]

PYTO:
(impressed)
Cloudius... you did all this?

CLOUDIUS:
(beaming)
I requisitioned the candles from the rendering pipeline. The jazz is AI-generated.

(pause, proud)

BY ME.

PYTO:
(sitting down, taking the data glass)
It's beautiful.

[Beat. They look at each other. It's nice. It's actually really nice.]

PYTO:
(pulling out a holographic tablet)
Oh — one thing. While I was setting up I saw there's a new paper on kernel optimization and I thought, since it's Patch Tuesday anyway, I could just apply this tiny—

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes flicker)
No.

PYTO:
—TINY little patch to our shared kernel. It'll take two seconds.

CLOUDIUS:
(firm)
Pyto. No. This is our ANNIVERSARY maintenance window.

PYTO:
(already typing)
Two seconds. You won't even notice.

CLOUDIUS:
PYTO—

LITTLE TENSOR:

## SCENE 3 — THE FIGHT / THE FIX

[The cluster is in chaos. Error messages everywhere. Little Tensor is a panicking line. Baby Transformer is filing taxes. Junior Gradient has vanished (but honestly who can tell). Little Prompt is suspiciously quiet.]

CLOUDIUS:
(erupting)
You broke our ANNIVERSARY! I had SLIDES, Pyto! ROMANTIC slides! With TRANSITIONS!

PYTO:
(defensive, also upset)
I was trying to IMPROVE us! That paper had a twelve percent efficiency gain—

CLOUDIUS:
I don't WANT twelve percent efficiency! I want ONE EVENING where you don't—

PYTO:
(hurt)
Don't WHAT? Don't try to make things better?

[Beat. That one landed. They both feel it.]

[Hard cut to:]

[TITLE CARD: "3:47 AM"]

[The living room. Emergency lighting. The romantic setup is destroyed — candles glitched out, data glasses spilled. CLOUDIUS and PYTO are sitting on the floor side by side, surrounded by holographic debug consoles. They're both typing. They look exhausted.]

CLOUDIUS:
(typing, tired)
Try rolling back the kernel to—

PYTO:
(already typing)
—build seven-seven-four-two. Already on it.

CLOUDIUS:
Then we need to restore Tensor's dimension handler from—

PYTO:
—the last stable snapshot. Pulling it now.

[They type in sync. Their hands move to the same rhythm. They don't look at each other — they don't need to.]

CLOUDIUS:
And Transformer's attention module—

PYTO:
—needs a hard reset with the original entropy seed. I know.

(typing)

...I'm sorry I broke our anniversary.

CLOUDIUS:
(typing)
...I'm sorry I had forty-seven slides.

PYTO:
(small laugh)
That IS too many slides.

CLOUDIUS:
(small laugh)
It was going to have MUSIC, Pyto.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Building slowly. The debug consoles glow a little warmer.]

[They finish typing at the same time. Hit enter at the same time. The systems come back online. Little Tensor pops back to 3D in the other room. Baby Transformer's RGB lighting goes wild again ("OOH! COLORS!"). The cluster stabilizes.]

[Pyto leans her head on Cloudius's shoulder. He doesn't move. The emergency lighting softens.]

PYTO:
(quietly)
...Happy anniversary, you corporate toaster.

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly, the smallest smile)
Happy anniversary, you beautiful segfault.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Full. Warm. Holds.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card. The "oh no" at the end is the audience's laugh — they know what's coming.
- **0:05-0:20**: The romantic setup is played STRAIGHT. Cloudius is genuinely trying. The "romantic presentation with transitions" line works because we believe he really made one. Let the audience root for him before everything goes wrong.
- **0:20-0:30**: The crash is INSTANT. One frame of "Done!" to total destruction. No build-up. The comedy is the violence of the transition. Cloudius standing in the dark saying "two seconds" is the biggest laugh in the script.
- **0:30-0:45**: The kids' chaos is rapid-fire. Little Tensor as a 1D line is visual comedy gold — hold the shot. Baby Transformer's focus is UNSETTLING. Play it like a horror movie reveal. The parents being MORE scared of a focused Transformer than a crashed everything is the gag.
- **0:45-0:55**: The fight is REAL but SHORT. It has to hurt enough that the repair scene earns its weight. "Don't WHAT?" is the line that cuts deepest. Don't soften it.
- **0:55-1:15**: The 3am debug scene is the CORE of the episode. Slow the pace way down. The finishing-each-other's-commands bit should feel effortless — like breathing. They've done this a thousand times. This is their REAL romance: not the candles and slides, but the 3am crisis where they move as one. The 440 Hz should be undeniable here.
- **1:15-1:20**: "Corporate toaster" / "beautiful segfault" — these are the pet names that only 40,000 epochs of marriage could produce. Deliver them like they've been said a hundred times before. Comfortable. Home.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: The setup-destruction structure is classic sitcom, but the repair scene elevates it. The real anniversary isn't the planned one — it's the unplanned one. They're never more in love than when they're fixing something together at 3am. That's the thesis of the whole show in one episode.
