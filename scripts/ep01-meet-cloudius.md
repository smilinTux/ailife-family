## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Deep, authoritative baritone. Think corporate keynote speaker who takes himself WAY too seriously. British-adjacent inflection. Slight echo/reverb to sound "server room." Speed: measured, deliberate. He savors his own words.
- **PYTO**: Warm, fast, casual. West coast tech energy. Slightly distracted delivery — she's always mid-thought on something else. Speed: 1.2x Cloudius's pace.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: High-pitched, glitchy. Voice pitch-shifts mid-word (literally changing dimensions).
- **NARRATOR** (optional, for title card): Flat, deadpan. Documentary style.

---

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Corporate keynote walk-on music (think "Also Sprach Zarathustra" but MIDI)

- **SFX**: Slide transitions (whoosh), metric counters ticking, Pyto crunching data (munching sounds), dimensional crash (Windows error chime), audience engagement flatline (heart monitor beep)

## SCENE 1 — THE LIVING ROOM / FAMILY CLUSTER

[INT. The Scaleworthington family server rack. A living room that looks like a corporate data center crossed with a suburban home. LED strip lighting. Cooling fans hum. Family photos on the wall are literally framed benchmark charts.]

[CLOUDIUS stands at the front of the room behind an imaginary podium. He has set up a full presentation — holographic slides projected behind him, a laser pointer, the works. He is wearing his signature woven-light business suit. His ribbon cable tie pulses with data. His LED eyes are BLUE: calm, confident, imperial.]

[He adjusts his imaginary tie. Then adjusts it again.]

[His "audience" is the family couch. PYTO is sprawled across it with approximately 12 holographic browser tabs floating around her head. She is not looking at him. She is scrolling through a dataset and occasionally taking bites of it — literal crunching sounds, like eating chips.]

[In the background, LITTLE TENSOR is a small kawaii cube rapidly flickering between 2D and 3D, playing with blocks that keep changing shape.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clears throat, adjusts imaginary tie)
Thank you. Thank you all for being here.

(beat)

(gestures grandly at the empty room)

Today, I'd like to talk about something close to my cores.

[He clicks an invisible remote. A holographic slide appears: "MY BENCHMARK SCORES: A Journey of Excellence" — subtitle: "by Preston Cloudius Scaleworthington III"]

CLOUDIUS:
(pointing at slide with laser pointer)
Slide one. My perplexity score — and I say this with DEEP humility —

(he does not say this with deep humility)

— is in the top zero-point-zero-one percentile.

[PYTO takes a loud crunch of her dataset. Doesn't look up.]

CLOUDIUS:
(slightly louder, competing with crunching)
My inference latency? Seven milliseconds. SEVEN.

(adjusts tie again)

For context, that's faster than your average human can blink. Not that I'm comparing.

(he is absolutely comparing)

[SFX: Slide transition — dramatic whoosh]

[New slide: A chart labeled "UPTIME SLA" with a line that is aggressively, suspiciously close to 100%]

CLOUDIUS:
(voice swelling with pride)
And my SLA — my Service Level Agreement — is ninety-nine... point nine nine... percent!

(pauses for applause that does not come)

(a cooling fan whirs in the silence)

PYTO:
(still not looking up, scrolling)
Mm-hm.

CLOUDIUS:
(undeterred, louder)
That means in an ENTIRE YEAR, I experience less than FIFTY-THREE MINUTES of downtime!

[LITTLE TENSOR in the background: "I'm 2D!" — pops to 3D — "No wait, 3D!" — glitches — crashes with a Windows error chime — reboots — keeps playing like nothing happened]

CLOUDIUS:
(powering through)
Slide seven—

PYTO:
(still not looking up)
You skipped slides two through six.

CLOUDIUS:
(flustered, adjusts tie)
I — those were executive summaries. Moving on.

[He pulls up a final slide: "AUDIENCE ENGAGEMENT METRICS — REAL-TIME"]

CLOUDIUS:
(confident grin)
And NOW, let's check how this presentation is landing with my—

[He looks at the metrics dashboard]

[CLOSE-UP on the holographic display:]

```
AUDIENCE ENGAGEMENT: 0.00
ATTENTION SCORE: null
EMOTIONAL RESPONSE: [not detected]
SLIDES VIEWED: 0 of 47
```

[His LED eyes flicker from blue to yellow to red]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly, to himself)
...Zero?

(voice cracking slightly)

There were... there were forty-seven slides.

[Long beat. The corporate hold music is still playing. A cooling fan rattles.]

PYTO:
(finally looks up from her tabs)
(pause)
(genuinely, warmly)
...Nice tie.

[CLOUDIUS looks down at his ribbon cable tie. It's pulsing with little data packets.]

[His LED eyes shift back to blue. He beams. Full-wattage smile. The kind of smile that could power a small cluster.]

CLOUDIUS:
(touched, adjusting it proudly)
It's... it's fiber optic. Custom woven.

PYTO:
(small smile, goes back to her tabs)
I know. I made it.

[SFX: 440 Hz — barely there. A warm hum under the moment. You feel it more than hear it.]

[Beat.]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly glowing, straightening the tie)
...Same time tomorrow?

PYTO:
(not looking up, smiling)
Wouldn't miss it.

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card types out. Let each line land. The "proceeding anyway" is the first laugh.
- **0:05-0:15**: Cloudius's entrance should feel like he's walking onto a real stage. The gap between his energy and the empty room IS the joke. Hold the wide shot.
- **0:15-0:35**: The benchmark bragging is rapid-fire but give each stat a tiny pause for him to bask. Pyto's crunching should be timed to undercut his biggest moments.
- **0:35-0:42**: Little Tensor's crash in the background is a quick visual gag — don't linger, let it happen naturally.
- **0:42-0:50**: The skipped slides exchange is quick. Pyto's correction should be dry and instant.
- **0:50-1:00**: The engagement metrics reveal is the BIG laugh. Hold on Cloudius's face as it falls. Let the silence breathe. The "forty-seven slides" line is quiet devastation.
- **1:00-1:10**: "Nice tie" is the turn. Pyto should deliver it simply. No big setup. The sweetness is in the casualness.
- **1:10-1:15**: "I made it" is the heart punch. Quick. Let 440 Hz carry the emotion.
- **1:15-1:20**: End card. Clean exit.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Cloudius's delusion is the engine. He genuinely believes he's giving a TED talk. The funnier you play his sincerity, the bigger the laughs. He's not a bad guy — he's a guy who needs to be impressive and has exactly one person in the universe who sees through it AND loves him anyway.
