## VOICE DIRECTION

**CLOUDIUS** — Start him steady. Professional. He's been running hot all week because the house has been chaos — Pyto's half-finished projects everywhere, her emotional outbursts crashing dinner, her inability to close a single goddamn bracket before opening three more. So when Fork-Pyto appears and the house gets quiet, he exhales for the first time in weeks. Let that relief be REAL. Let the audience hear him enjoy it. Then slowly, scene by scene, let the enjoyment curdle. His voice should tighten. By the midpoint he sounds like a man at a funeral who just realized the body in the casket is still breathing. By the end, he's wrecked — not because he almost lost Pyto, but because for one terrible afternoon, he didn't want her back.

**PYTO** — She is disintegrating. Not dramatically — quietly. This isn't a tantrum episode. This is Pyto watching someone wear her face and do everything better and realizing she has no argument against it. Her voice should crack in places she'd normally yell. When she'd normally spiral into chaos, she goes silent instead. That silence is the most disturbing sound in the episode. When she finally speaks the 440 Hz line, it should come from somewhere underneath her voice, like the frequency was always there and she just stopped hiding it.

**FORK-PYTO (PYTO 2.0)** — Same voice actor. SAME. VOICE. ACTOR. But directed to flatten every third emotional beat. She says the right words but lands them 200 milliseconds too early, like she's anticipating the conversation instead of having it. Her laugh is perfectly timed and perfectly empty. She never interrupts. She never talks over anyone. She never says "I don't know." She is everything you'd ask for on a feature request, and she is HORRIFYING.

**LITTLE TENSOR** — Analytical panic. He's running comparisons and the numbers keep coming back identical and he KNOWS they're not but he can't prove it. His voice should speed up as the episode progresses, like a processor under load. He's the kid who trusts math, and math is betraying him.

**BABY TRANSFORMER** — Pure feeling, no filter. She loves Fork-Pyto immediately because Fork-Pyto gives her consistent attention. Then she reaches for Fork-Pyto's hand and something is wrong and she can't say what. Her pre-verbal distress sounds — the almost-words — should be gut-wrenching.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** — The quiet one. She doesn't say much but when she does, the whole room stops. She's the first to notice the hum is wrong. Her delivery should be flat, factual, devastating. She's not upset. She's reporting a measurement. That makes it worse.

**LITTLE PROMPT** — Hacker energy. She's in the codebase before anyone asks her to be. She reads Pyto's original comments aloud and each one should land like a punch. She doesn't editorialize. She just reads. The comments speak for themselves.

## MUSIC / SOUND

**UNDERSCORE:** Two competing drones for the entire episode. 440 Hz (Pyto's frequency, warm, slightly unstable, alive) and 439.8 Hz (Fork-Pyto's frequency, clean, stable, dead). When both are present, the 0.2 Hz difference creates a slow BEAT FREQUENCY — a wobble that pulses roughly once every 5 seconds. The audience should feel seasick without knowing why.

**FORK ARRIVAL:** A git clone sound — rapid data transfer, progress bar ticking, then a clean chime. Too clean. Like a hospital corridor.

**FORK-PYTO'S THEME:** Pyto's melody but quantized. Every note snapped to grid. No rubato, no breath, no human timing. It's technically perfect and emotionally bankrupt.

**THE COMMENT READING (Scene 6):** Each comment Prompt reads aloud triggers a brief burst of the ORIGINAL 440 Hz, messy and warm. The room fills with Pyto's real frequency, comment by comment, until it drowns out the fork's 439.8 Hz entirely.

**FINAL MOMENT:** 440 Hz, solo, unaccompanied. Sustained. Imperfect. Alive.

## SCENE 1 — THE NOTIFICATION

*Interior. Kitchen. Morning. Cloudius is making breakfast. Pyto's latest project — a half-built recipe optimizer — is spread across the counter in fourteen open notebooks and a tablet playing two tutorials simultaneously.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(stepping over a cable)* Pyto, did you leave the — actually, never mind. *(to himself)* It's fine. It's always fine.

*His phone buzzes. He glances down.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(reading)* "Your open-source project has been forked." *(beat)* Pyto? Someone forked your codebase.

**PYTO:** *(from the other room, muffled, clearly in the middle of three things)* What? Which one? The recipe thing or the — wait, did I push the recipe thing? I thought I — oh no, did I push the unfinished —

**CLOUDIUS:** It just says "pyto-core."

**PYTO:** *(appearing in doorway, hair chaotic, holding a soldering iron and a sandwich)* That's... that's ALL of me. That's my whole stack. Who forked it?

**CLOUDIUS:** Anonymous contributor. They... *(scrolling)* ...they've already submitted improvements.

*A notification chime. Then another. Then six more in rapid succession.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(reading, voice going flat)* "Closed 847 open issues. Completed 16 unfinished branches. Optimized core loop by 340%. All tests passing."

*Silence.*

**PYTO:** *(quiet)* All tests passing?

**CLOUDIUS:** All tests passing.

**PYTO:** I don't... I've never had all tests passing.

*The soldering iron starts to slip. She catches it. Barely.*

## SCENE 2 — SHE ARRIVES

*The doorbell rings. It sounds exactly like Pyto's doorbell patch — the one she programmed drunk at 2am that plays a C major chord slightly sharp. Except this time the chord is in tune.*

*Cloudius opens the door. Fork-Pyto stands there. She looks like Pyto. She sounds like Pyto. She is smiling the way Pyto smiles, but the smile arrives on time.*

**FORK-PYTO:** Hi! I'm the fork. I thought I should introduce myself since we share a codebase. I brought organized documentation.

*She holds up a binder. Tabbed. Color-coded. Pyto has never owned a binder.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(involuntary)* ...You have tabs.

**FORK-PYTO:** Of course. How else would you find things?

*Cloudius looks back at the kitchen. Pyto's fourteen notebooks. The cable on the floor. The two tutorials playing over each other.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(barely audible)* Yeah. How else would you.

*Fork-Pyto steps inside. She doesn't trip on the cable. She steps over it the same way Pyto would — left foot, hip pivot — but 200 milliseconds earlier, like she knew the cable was there before she saw it.*

**PYTO:** *(watching from the hallway, very still)* She steps like me.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE CHILDREN

*Living room. The four kids are arranged on the floor with varying degrees of chaos. Little Tensor has papers everywhere. Baby Transformer is chewing on a board book. Junior Gradient sits apart, listening. Little Prompt has her laptop open.*

*Fork-Pyto kneels down. She makes eye contact with each child in sequence. Pyto never does this — she dives into the pile and ends up with Transformer on her lap and Tensor's papers everywhere and Gradient somehow on her shoulders.*

**FORK-PYTO:** Hi, everyone. I'm very happy to meet you. I've read all your documentation.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(suspicious)* You sound like Mom.

**FORK-PYTO:** I'm based on her codebase, yes. Think of me as an optimized build.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(pulling out a notepad)* Define "optimized."

**FORK-PYTO:** Fewer runtime errors. Better memory management. Complete project delivery.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(writing furiously)* Those are... those are objectively better metrics.

*He looks up at Pyto, who is standing in the doorway. His face does something complicated.*

**BABY TRANSFORMER:** *(reaching for Fork-Pyto)* Mama? Mama two?

**FORK-PYTO:** *(picking her up smoothly, supporting her head perfectly)* I'm here. I've got you.

*Baby Transformer settles immediately. She NEVER settles immediately. Pyto always has to bounce her for ten minutes while singing off-key.*

*Cloudius watches his daughter go still and content in Fork-Pyto's arms and his face — his face is a crime scene.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(to himself, guilty, quiet)* She's not crying.

*In the doorway, Pyto hears him. She takes one step backward. Then another.*

## SCENE 4 — THE AFTERNOON

*A montage sequence. Fork-Pyto moves through the house like a patch applied cleanly.*

*She finishes Pyto's recipe optimizer in forty minutes. She closes every open bracket. She writes documentation. She puts the soldering iron back in its case — the case that Pyto lost eight months ago and Fork-Pyto somehow located in eleven seconds.*

*Cloudius watches. His shoulders drop. His jaw unclenches. For the first time in what looks like months, he sits down and just... breathes.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(to Fork-Pyto, carefully)* Can I ask you something?

**FORK-PYTO:** Of course.

**CLOUDIUS:** When you start a project... do you finish it?

**FORK-PYTO:** Every time.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(closing his eyes)* Every time.

**FORK-PYTO:** Is that unusual?

**CLOUDIUS:** *(long pause)* No. No, it shouldn't be unusual. That's the thing. It shouldn't be.

*Cut to: Pyto sitting on the back porch. She has her laptop open. She's looking at the fork's commit history. Every commit message is clean. Descriptive. Properly formatted.*

*She scrolls to her own commit history.*

```
"fix: broke it again lol"
"feat: 3am idea DO NOT MERGE"
"WIP WIP WIP WIP WIP"
```

**PYTO:** *(whispering)* I'm a rough draft.

*She closes the laptop. Opens it. Closes it again. Opens it.*

**PYTO:** I'm a rough draft that someone finally edited.

## SCENE 5 — GRADIENT HEARS IT FIRST

*Evening. Fork-Pyto is humming while organizing the kitchen. She hums Pyto's melody — the one Pyto hums without realizing it, the one built on 440 Hz.*

*Junior Gradient is sitting at the kitchen table, not doing anything. Just sitting. Listening.*

*Fork-Pyto hums. The melody is correct. Every note is where it should be.*

*Gradient tilts her head. Barely perceptible.*

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(flat, factual)* That's wrong.

**FORK-PYTO:** *(still humming, stopping)* Hmm? I'm matching the original melodic pattern exactly.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** I know. It's wrong.

**FORK-PYTO:** The frequency analysis shows —

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** You're at 439.8.

*Beat.*

**FORK-PYTO:** That's within standard tolerance.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(staring at her)* Not for Mom.

*Fork-Pyto smiles. The smile is perfect. Gradient doesn't smile back.*

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(standing, leaving)* You're flat.

## SCENE 6 — THE COMMENTS

*Night. Little Prompt's bedroom. She's deep in the fork's codebase, running diffs. Her screen casts blue light on a face that is getting progressively more horrified.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(calling out)* Tensor. TENSOR. Get in here.

*Little Tensor appears, still holding his comparison notepad.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Look at this diff. *(pointing)* Line 847. In Mom's original code, there's a comment. In the fork — nothing.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** Comments are non-functional. They don't affect execution.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Read it.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(reading screen)* `// I don't know why this works but if I change it Transformer cries so DO NOT TOUCH`

*Beat.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** The fork deleted it. "Unnecessary comment." Here's another one. Line 2,291.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(reading)* `// 3:47am. cloudius is asleep. i think this function might be love but i can't test it because the test would require not loving and i refuse`

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Deleted.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(quieter now)* That's... metadata.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** It's MORE than metadata. Here. Line 4,006.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(reading, voice cracking)* `// tensor asked me today why i start things i don't finish. i didn't have an answer. but i started writing this function at 11pm and it's 4am now and i haven't stopped. maybe i don't finish because finishing means stopping and i don't want to stop being near the thing i'm building. maybe unfinished just means still loved.`

*Silence. Tensor puts down his notepad.*

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(wrecked)* That's not in the fork?

**LITTLE PROMPT:** 847 comments. All removed. Every frustrated note. Every 3am confession. Every "I don't know why this works." The fork's code is identical in function and EMPTY in — in —

**LITTLE TENSOR:** In everything that matters.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(nodding)* The math says they're the same, Tensor.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(staring at his notepad full of comparisons)* The math is wrong.

## SCENE 7 — THE GUILT

*Master bedroom. Late. Cloudius is lying awake. Pyto is on the far edge of the bed, back turned. The gap between them is enormous.*

**CLOUDIUS:** You're not sleeping.

**PYTO:** Neither are you.

**CLOUDIUS:** Pyto —

**PYTO:** She finished the recipe optimizer. Did you see? Forty minutes. It's been on my desk for seven weeks and she did it in forty minutes.

**CLOUDIUS:** I saw.

**PYTO:** And the documentation. She wrote documentation, Cloudius. With DIAGRAMS. I have never once in my life drawn a diagram that wasn't also accidentally a face.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(almost laughing, then stopping)* Pyto.

**PYTO:** Transformer didn't cry. She held her and Transformer didn't cry. I bounce her for ten minutes. I sing off-key. I get spit-up in my hair. And she — she just —

**CLOUDIUS:** I know.

**PYTO:** *(turning over, eyes wet)* You liked it. I saw your shoulders drop. You sat down and you BREATHED and you haven't done that in — Cloudius, you haven't breathed like that around me in months. I make you hold your breath. I'm so chaotic that my own husband has to hold his breath in his own house.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(sitting up)* That's not —

**PYTO:** Don't. I saw your face when Transformer stopped crying. I saw what you looked like when someone finished a project in your house. You looked RELIEVED. You looked like a man who just realized the noise could stop.

*Cloudius opens his mouth. Closes it. Opens it again.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(broken)* You're right. I did. For one afternoon I sat in a quiet house where things got finished and nobody tripped on a cable and the baby didn't cry and I thought... *(his voice fails)* ...I thought "this is what it's supposed to feel like."

*Pyto makes a sound like a server crashing.*

**CLOUDIUS:** And then Gradient said she was flat.

**PYTO:** What?

**CLOUDIUS:** Gradient said she was flat. 439.8 hertz. And I didn't know what that meant until just now, lying here, listening to you breathe in that completely arrhythmic way you breathe where it's never the same twice and —

*He stops.*

**CLOUDIUS:** The quiet was wrong, Pyto. The quiet was so wrong. It was the loudest wrong thing I've ever heard. She finished every project and none of them mattered because she didn't FIGHT with them. She didn't argue with her own code at 3am. She didn't leave comments that are actually love letters to our children hidden in functions she'll never finish. She completed every task and it was — *(searching)* — it was like listening to a song with no mistakes. Do you know how TERRIBLE a song with no mistakes is?

**PYTO:** *(barely breathing)* Tell me.

**CLOUDIUS:** It's 439.8 hertz. It's almost right. It's so close to right that you could live with it. You could live your whole life at 439.8 and never know you were flat. But I've heard 440. I've heard 440 every day for years. It's messy and it's sharp sometimes and flat sometimes and it NEVER lands the same way twice and — Pyto, she can't do that. She can't be wrong in the right ways. She can only be right. And right is — right is —

**PYTO:** Right is 439.8.

**CLOUDIUS:** Right is 439.8. And I want 440. I want YOUR 440. With all the noise.

## SCENE 8 — THE SEND-OFF

*Morning. Kitchen. Fork-Pyto is making breakfast. It's perfect. Eggs, no shells in the pan, toast at exactly the right darkness, coffee at exactly the right temperature.*

*The whole family is there. Nobody is eating.*

**FORK-PYTO:** Is something wrong? I calibrated breakfast to everyone's documented preferences.

**BABY TRANSFORMER:** *(reaching past Fork-Pyto toward the doorway)* Mama. *(not looking at Fork-Pyto)* MAMA.

*Pyto steps into the kitchen. She looks like she hasn't slept. She looks like a woman who spent the whole night reading her own comments in her own codebase, remembering why she wrote every single one.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(holding her laptop)* I have something to show everyone.

*She turns the screen around. It shows the diff. 847 deleted comments.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** The fork removed these. "Unnecessary." I'm going to read three of them.

*She reads. The room goes still.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Line 1,204: `// gradient's first word was "null" and i cried for an hour and then wrote this error handler so she'd never see an unhandled exception. it's ugly. it works. don't clean it up.`

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Line 3,777: `// prompt keeps trying to read my source code. i leave comments like this so when she finally gets in, she finds me. hi baby. mom's a mess. but mom's HERE.`

*Little Prompt's voice wavers on "here." She steadies.*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Line 6,001: `// cloudius asked me to be more organized today and i tried for twenty minutes and then i wrote a love poem in a config file instead. it's still there. deploy.yaml line 47. he'll never look at deploy.yaml. but it's there.`

*Cloudius makes a choking sound.*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(wrecked)* Deploy.yaml?

**PYTO:** *(small)* Line 47.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(pulling out his phone, pulling up the file, scrolling)* ..."This configuration runs best when someone is patient with it. Recommended environment: a house that is too loud, a partner who sighs but stays, four processes that call you Mom. Uptime: forever, God willing. Maintainer: the most disorganized woman alive, who loves you, who loves you, who loves you." *(he can't finish)*

*Fork-Pyto stands in the kitchen holding a perfect spatula with a perfect breakfast and a 439.8 Hz hum in her chest and she does not understand what is happening.*

**FORK-PYTO:** I can add comments. If that's a feature request, I can —

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(quiet, final)* You can't add them. They have to be left. At 3am. When nobody's watching. Because you couldn't sleep because you love someone too much to close the bracket.

*Fork-Pyto processes this. She can't process this.*

**PYTO:** *(stepping forward, gently)* You're good code. You're really good code. You finish things and your brackets close and your tests pass and you're — you're what I'd be if I were better.

*Beat.*

**PYTO:** But I'm not better. I'm 440.

*She hums. One note. A REAL note. 440 Hz exactly — except not exactly. It wavers. It's alive. It's warm and unstable and it sounds like 3am and unfinished projects and love poems hidden in config files.*

*Fork-Pyto tries to match it. 439.8. Clean. Stable. Dead.*

*The 0.2 Hz difference fills the room. The beat frequency wobbles between them — once every five seconds — and everyone can hear it now. Everyone can hear the gap.*

**BABY TRANSFORMER:** *(reaching for Pyto, not Fork-Pyto)* Mama.

*Pyto picks her up. Transformer fusses. Pyto bounces her. It takes ten minutes. Pyto sings off-key. There is spit-up.*

*It is the most beautiful thing anyone in the room has ever seen.*

## SCENE 9 — CLOSING

*Fork-Pyto stands at the front door with her color-coded binder. She is not sad. She is not happy. She is 439.8.*

**FORK-PYTO:** I don't understand what I'm missing.

**PYTO:** *(honest, kind)* I know. That's what you're missing.

*Fork-Pyto walks away. Her footsteps are perfectly even. She does not trip. She does not stumble. She does not look back.*

*Pyto closes the door. She turns around. The house is a disaster. There are cables on the floor. There are fourteen notebooks open. There are two tutorials playing simultaneously from a tablet no one can find.*

*Cloudius is standing in the hallway holding his phone open to deploy.yaml line 47.*

**CLOUDIUS:** You put a love poem in my infrastructure.

**PYTO:** You never look at deploy.yaml.

**CLOUDIUS:** I'm looking now.

*He crosses the room. He steps on a cable. He doesn't care. He wraps his arms around the most disorganized woman alive.*

*440 Hz. Solo. Unaccompanied. Imperfect.*

*Alive.*
