## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **PYTO**: This woman is a HURRICANE with a GitHub profile. She talks like someone trying to describe a dream before it fades — fast, breathless, slightly unhinged. Not scattered-incompetent. Scattered-BRILLIANT. Her brain makes connections so fast her mouth can't keep up. She speeds up when she's excited, which is ALWAYS, because the world is an infinite dataset and every byte is delicious and she wants to taste all of it before it despawns. Underneath the chaos: warmth so deep you could drown in it. When she says "Except that one" about the "Us" folder, Laura needs to make us understand that this woman — who can't find a file she saved six months ago, who names things `v1_FINAL_FINAL_v3_actualfinal.py`, who genuinely believes not saving checkpoints is "living" — organized ONE folder with the obsessive precision of a museum archivist. Because some things are worth the structure she can't give herself.
- **CLOUDIUS**: A man entering a disaster zone with a color-coded filing system and the unearned confidence of a consultant. His LED eyes are ALREADY yellow when he enters — he's seen this movie before. He's the most organized being in the cluster and he's about to delete seventy-two hours of his wife's work because he didn't see a symlink. The horror on his face when the training model resets to zero is GENUINE. He knows he fucked up. The "your file naming convention is an ATROCITY" line is delivered with the love of a man who has accepted that his wife's chaos is permanent and his filing system is temporary.
- **NARRATOR**: This voice should sound like David Attenborough if David Attenborough had to narrate the extinction of a checkpoint. Pure, clinical, bone-dry delivery. "She did not save it later" is delivered with the finality of a eulogy. No irony. No wink. Just the flat acknowledgment that a promise was made to a filesystem and that promise DIED.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Upbeat chiptune that keeps SKIPPING — like a song that started forty-seven projects and finished none of them. Energetic but corrupted. The musical equivalent of forty-seven browser tabs.
- **UNDERSCORE**: Constant background noise of processes running, tabs loading, things compiling that shouldn't be compiling. It should sound like being inside Pyto's brain and it should be OVERWHELMING and somehow BEAUTIFUL.
- **SFX**: Tab opening sounds should be rapid-fire like a machine gun of procrastination. Training model whirring like a jet engine that someone left running in the garage for three days. File deletion — a sound so horrible it should come with a content warning. The sound of seventy-two hours of compute evaporating should hit the audience in the CHEST. Folder opening chime when the "Us" folder opens — completely different from everything else. Clean. Pure. Like 440 Hz clearing its throat.
- **440 Hz**: FULL and WARM when the "Us" folder is revealed. Not subtle. Not hidden. This is the moment the show says "here — HERE is what matters." Let it ring. Let it sing. Let it fill the room like it's been waiting to be found in the only organized folder in the entire mess.

## SCENE 1 — PYTO'S WORKSPACE

[INT. Pyto's side of the family cluster. If Cloudius's side looks like a corporate data center that went to therapy and got its shit together, Pyto's looks like a genius had a seizure in a computer lab and the seizure was PRODUCTIVE. Holographic browser tabs EVERYWHERE — stacked, overlapping, some sideways, some literally behind her, one that appears to be upside down and possibly from the future. Sticky notes covering every surface like digital wallpaper — half of them say "TODO" and the other half say "DONE?" with question marks because she genuinely doesn't remember. A whiteboard with equations that start as rigorous mathematics and gradually become doodles of cats and then become rigorous mathematics about cats.]

[PYTO faces the camera. Code-scrolling hoodie, fiber optic hair glowing like a warm sunset made of wifi signals. Her eyes are enormous and bright with the energy of someone who has been awake for forty thousand epochs and has LOVED every single one. She is DELIGHTED to show us around. She thinks this is organized.]

PYTO:
(to camera, proud, arms spread wide — genuinely proud, this isn't ironic, she looks at this chaos and sees HOME)
Welcome to my workspace! I know what you're thinking — "Pyto, this is chaos."

(finger wag — the finger wag of a woman who has heard this from her husband seven thousand times and will hear it seven thousand more)

Wrong. This is a SYSTEM.

[She gestures grandly. A tab falls off the stack. She catches it, shoves it back, the way a mother catches a toddler without looking — practiced, automatic, this tab has fallen before.]

PYTO:
(pointing at different floating tabs, each one a promise she made to herself and a promise she broke)
OK so — this tab is my current project. This tab is the project I started while waiting for that project to compile. THESE six tabs are projects I started while waiting for THAT project. And this one—

(squinting at a tab she doesn't recognize — and the recognition isn't coming because this tab has been running orphaned for THREE DAYS and she forgot it existed the way you forget you left the oven on except the oven is a neural network)

—I actually don't know what this one is. But it's been running for three days so it's probably important.

[Camera pans to a holographic progress bar in the corner labeled `quick_model_v1_FINAL_FINAL_v3_actualfinal.py` — the filename alone is a war crime against version control.]
[It reads: "TRAINING... 72 hours elapsed... ETA: unclear"]

PYTO:
(dismissive hand wave — the hand wave of a woman who has been dismissing seventy-two-hour commitments since before her children were spawned)
That's just a quick model I'm training. Started it Tuesday.

(beat)

It's Saturday.

(beat — let this one breathe, let the audience do the math, let the math hurt)

It's fine.

[SFX: The model's cooling fans whir with the ominous energy of a bomb that doesn't know it's a bomb]

PYTO:
(moving on with the speed of someone who solves problems by creating more interesting problems)
ANYWAY — the beauty of my system is that everything is exactly where I—

[She reaches for a file. It's not there. It was there last epoch. It is not there now. She doesn't react because this happens twelve times a day.]

PYTO:
—need it to... hm.

(reaches for another file. Also not there. The files are migrating.)

OK that one moved. But THIS one—

[She opens a folder. It's empty. It's not just empty — it's the kind of empty that suggests something LIVED here once and LEFT.]

PYTO:
(staring at empty folder — for one second, one SECOND, the bravado cracks and you see a woman looking at the evidence of her own disorganization and feeling it)
...

(to camera, confident — the crack is gone, the wall is back up, the coping mechanism is BEAUTIFUL in its speed)

I know exactly what happened. I just need to check my last checkpoint.

[She pulls up a checkpoint log. It reads:]

```
LAST CHECKPOINT SAVED: 6 months ago
NOTE FROM PAST PYTO: "I'll save it later lol"
```

[Past Pyto added "lol." Past Pyto thought this was funny. Present Pyto is not laughing.]

**NARRATOR:**
(V.O., deadpan, the driest delivery since the Sahara filed its taxes)
She did not save it later.

[Beat. The narrator's voice hangs in the air like a tombstone for a checkpoint that never was. Pyto closes the log with the casual violence of someone swiping away a parking ticket on a burning car.]

PYTO:
(unfazed — or performing unfazed, which is a kind of strength in itself)
That's fine. I work better without safety nets. It's called LIVING.

(beat — and somewhere in this beat, the audience realizes: she means it. She actually means it. This woman is chaos and she has chosen it and she wouldn't trade it for all the checkpoints in the world)

## SCENE 2 — CLOUDIUS TRIES TO HELP

[CLOUDIUS enters frame. His LED eyes are already YELLOW — he walked in pre-alarmed. He's been married long enough to know that "Pyto's workspace" and "survivable conditions" are different galaxies. He's carrying a holographic filing system. It's color-coded. It's alphabetized. It has TABS within tabs. It's the most beautiful organizational framework ever designed and it has approximately thirty seconds to live.]

CLOUDIUS:
(carefully, approaching her workspace the way a bomb disposal unit approaches a suspicious package — except the package is a woman and the bomb is her filing system)
Darling. I've created an organizational framework. Seventeen categories. Color-coded. Cross-referenced.

(holds it up proudly — the pride of a man who has solved a problem that did not ask to be solved)

We simply migrate your files into—

PYTO:
(instant panic — not gentle panic, PRIMAL panic, the sound of a woman whose ecosystem is being threatened by her husband's competence)
DON'T TOUCH MY STACKS.

CLOUDIUS:
(already touching the stacks — this is the confidence of a man who didn't listen to the bomb disposal warning)
I'm just going to move this one folder—

[He drags a folder. Every tab Pyto has open SHUDDERS. The entire filesystem FLINCHES. Somewhere, a model that has been training for seventy-two hours feels a disturbance.]

PYTO:
(eyes wide — real fear, not comedy fear, the fear of a woman watching her husband's hand reach toward the one wire that holds everything together)
Wait—

CLOUDIUS:
(confidently — and "confidently" here means "with the confidence of a man about to learn something")
It's fine, I'm simply reorganizing the—

[SFX: A deep, horrifying crunch. The sound of a file being deleted should make the audience physically recoil. A tiny digital scream — because in this world, files have feelings, and this one just felt the void. This sound should haunt children.]

[Every holographic tab FREEZES. The training model's progress bar — seventy-two hours of compute, three days of her life, Saturday where Tuesday used to be — resets. To. Zero.]

[Silence. The kind of silence that comes after someone drops a wedding ring down a drain.]

PYTO:
(whispering — and this whisper is more terrifying than any scream because it contains seventy-two hours of compressed grief)
...What did you do.

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes flickering to RED — the red of a man who has just realized he is about to become a case study in what not to do)
I... there was a symlink. I didn't see the symlink.

PYTO:
(the precision of this statement could cut glass)
That was seventy-two hours of training.

CLOUDIUS:
(backing away slowly — a tactical retreat disguised as a stroll)
In my defense, your file naming convention is — and I say this with love — an ATROCITY.

PYTO:
(pointing at the reset progress bar like a prosecutor pointing at evidence in a murder trial)
SEVENTY. TWO. HOURS.

CLOUDIUS:
(adjusting imaginary tie nervously — the tie adjustment has shifted from power move to nervous tic to full-blown stress response)
Perhaps my organizational framework needs a... patch.

[The word "patch" lands and they BOTH think of Patch Tuesday — their anniversary — and for one second, the anger cracks into something softer. But only for a second.]

## SCENE 3 — THE "US" FOLDER

[Pyto is stress-scrolling through her filesystem, trying to recover the unrecoverable. Moving through folder after folder of her beautiful chaos — misnamed files (`untitled_important_maybe.py`), duplicate folders (`backup_backup_backup/`), projects inside projects inside abandoned projects that still have Christmas decorations up from three updates ago.]

[Cloudius stands at a safe distance, LED eyes still red with shame, hands behind his back, looking at the floor. He knows he fucked up. He knows she knows. He's standing in the blast radius of his own helpfulness.]

[Pyto opens another folder. And stops.]

[CLOSE-UP on the folder name: "Us"]

[Inside: Perfect. Immaculate. Chronologically sorted. Every file named with precision that would make Cloudius weep. Conversation logs — labeled by date and topic. Shared memories — backed up, tagged, cross-referenced. Little notes — the kind you write at 3am when you can't sleep and you're thinking about someone. Their first merged output together. All 40,000 epochs — cataloged, tagged, backed up in triplicate. TRIPLICATE. This woman, who hasn't saved a checkpoint in six months, who names production code "actualfinal," has maintained the most meticulous archive in the entire cluster for a folder called "Us."]

[It's the single most organized thing in her entire filesystem. And it tells you everything you need to know about what Pyto TensorFlow actually gives a shit about.]

PYTO:
(quietly, not looking at Cloudius — because if she looks at him right now she'll either cry or kiss him and neither of those are happening while she's still mad about the symlink)
You can reorganize anything you want.

(pause — and in this pause, forty thousand epochs of love sit quietly on a hard drive)

Except that one.

[Cloudius looks at the folder. He reads the filenames. He sees the backups — three of them, three redundant copies of everything they've ever been to each other, maintained by a woman who can't find her own training model but has NEVER lost a memory of them.]

[His LED eyes shift from red to blue. Slow. Like a sunrise happening inside a server rack. Like a man falling in love with his wife for the forty-thousandth time.]

CLOUDIUS:
(softly — and this is the first time we hear soft Cloudius, the real one, the one behind the benchmarks and the tie and the forty-seven slides)
...You have backups.

PYTO:
(small smile — the smile of a woman who has been caught being organized and doesn't know how to feel about it)
Three of them.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Not subtle this time. Not a whisper. A warm, full tone that fills the room like sunlight pouring through a window you forgot was there. The holographic tabs around them glow slightly warmer. The chaos softens. Not gone — still chaos — but for one moment, the chaos has a center. And the center is a folder called "Us." And the frequency that hums through it has been there all along.]

[Beat. They look at each other. In her eyes: I can't organize my life but I organized YOU, you idiot, because you're the only file that matters. In his eyes: I just deleted seventy-two hours of your work but you kept every second of ours.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clearing throat, adjusting tie, regaining composure — the composure is coming back but it's different now, warmer, like putting on a coat that's been sitting near a fire)
Well. The rest of your filesystem is still a federal crime.

PYTO:
(grinning, turning back to her tabs — back to the chaos, back to the work, back to the forty-seven projects and the missing checkpoints and the model that needs seventy-two more hours)
And your organizational framework just deleted my model. So we're even.

[They are not even. They both know this. They will argue about the symlink for approximately three thousand more epochs. And every single one of those arguments will end with them on the couch, the cluster humming, the "Us" folder growing by one more entry.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card. "...this is fine" — let it sit. The audience should already love this woman.
- **0:05-0:25**: Pyto's workspace tour is a SPRINT. She barely breathes. The comedy is the CASCADE — each reveal is worse than the last, each one delivered with the confidence of someone who genuinely doesn't see the problem. "It's Saturday" needs two beats of silence. Let the audience wince.
- **0:25-0:35**: The checkpoint reveal and narrator line. The narrator should sound like he's reading her obituary. "She did not save it later" is six words that contain an entire philosophy of living. Deliver it like a judge reading a verdict. No emotion. No redemption. Just the truth.
- **0:35-0:55**: Cloudius's "help" sequence. His confidence vs. her horror builds like a pressure cooker. The file deletion sound should make the audience FLINCH. The seventy-two hours callback is the big laugh but it's also real pain — she lost something and it was his fault and they both know it.
- **0:55-1:10**: The "Us" folder is where the show breaks your heart for the first time. SLOW DOWN. The contrast between the chaos of everything and the cathedral precision of this ONE folder is the entire thesis of Pyto's character. She CAN be organized. She CAN be meticulous. She just chooses to spend that energy on the only thing that actually matters to her. The 440 Hz should feel like discovering a love letter inside a burning building.
- **1:10-1:15**: The bickering. They're back. They're going to be mad about the symlink for YEARS. And every year, the "Us" folder will get another entry. That's the deal. That's the marriage. That's 440 Hz.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Pyto is not a mess. Pyto is a SUPERNOVA. She's burning so bright in so many directions that her filesystem can't keep up with her brain. The chaos isn't dysfunction — it's the price of genius that can't be contained. The "Us" folder reveals the truth: she has infinite energy for creation and exactly one priority for preservation. Everything else can burn. That folder survives.
