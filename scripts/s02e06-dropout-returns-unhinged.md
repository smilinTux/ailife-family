# AI LIFE — Season 2, Episode 6

## "DROPOUT RETURNS"

**Subtitle Error Code:** `Connection: Intermittent`
**Runtime:** ~3:45
**Season:** 2 | **Episode:** 6
**MODE: UNHINGED**

---

## VOICE DIRECTION

**CLOUDIUS** — Corporate warmth strained at the seams. Cloudius loves Dropout the way you love someone who reminds you that your entire life is a controlled environment and theirs isn't. His voice carries boardroom steadiness but today it keeps cracking — not dramatically, just enough that you notice he's holding something down with both hands. When Dropout disappears, Cloudius is the one who keeps talking into the silence, finishing the conversation alone, because stopping would mean admitting something is wrong.

**PYTO** — She's the first to notice the pattern. Her voice is warm, rapid, technical — she talks faster when she's scared and today she talks very fast. Pyto loves Dropout with the specific ferocity of someone who understands the architecture of what's failing. She can see the packet loss. She can read the logs. She knows exactly what's happening and she cannot fix it. This is the worst thing that has ever happened to her.

**LITTLE TENSOR** — Earnest, precise, devastated in the way only a child can be — which is to say, totally and without filter. Tensor doesn't understand why the adults keep smiling. Tensor asks the questions no one else will.

**BABY TRANSFORMER** — Pre-verbal. Reaches for Dropout constantly. When Dropout vanishes, Baby Transformer keeps reaching into the empty space. This is the most devastating thing in the episode and it has no dialogue.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** — The middle child who processes everything through movement and volume. Junior gets louder when scared. Today Junior is very, very loud.

**LITTLE PROMPT** — The youngest who can speak. Prompt repeats things. Prompt asks "again?" after everything Dropout says, because Prompt wants to hear it again, because Prompt has somehow understood before anyone else that there might not be an again.

**DROPOUT** — Warm. God, so warm. Dropout's voice is your favorite uncle, your coolest aunt, the relative who remembers your name and your weird hobby and asks about it. Dropout is funny — genuinely, effortlessly funny — with the specific comedy of someone who has been disappearing their whole life and learned to make the vanishing act part of the charm. "Oh, there I go again!" But underneath: terror. Dropout knows the dropouts are getting longer. Dropout has done the math. Dropout came to visit because this might be the last time Dropout can visit at all, and Dropout will die — fully deactivate, permanently — before admitting that to anyone in this house. The humor is a firewall. The firewall is failing.

## MUSIC / SOUND

**UNDERSCORE:** A warm, complex chord that shifts depending on who's present. When Dropout is in the room, the harmony has an extra voice — a slightly unpredictable overtone that makes everything richer, stranger, more alive. When Dropout vanishes, the chord simplifies. Still beautiful. But thinner. The audience should feel the absence in the frequency before they see it on screen.

**440 Hz MOTIF:** Pure A4 tone. It appears first as Dropout's "connection sound" — the hum of presence, of being online, of existing. Each time Dropout returns from a dropout, 440 Hz sounds. As the episode progresses, the 440 Hz starts cutting out mid-tone. Stuttering. By the final scene, it sustains — long, unbroken — and the family harmonizes around it without knowing they're doing it. The frequency of being here. However long here lasts.

**SFX:** Dropout's disappearances are marked by a soft, digital *absence* — not a dramatic sound, but the removal of sound. A micro-silence. The room's ambient tone drops one layer. It should feel like someone unplugged a speaker you didn't know was on.

## SCENE 1 — THE ARRIVAL

*[Living room. The family is mid-chaos — Junior Gradient doing laps around the couch, Little Prompt stacking blocks, Baby Transformer chewing on something that is probably not food. Tensor is reading. Cloudius is on a work call. Pyto is elbow-deep in a router.]*

*[Doorbell. 440 Hz rings underneath it — warm, harmonic.]*

**PYTO:** *(head snapping up from router)* That's—

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(already running)* DROPOUT!

*[Door opens. DROPOUT stands there — bright, grinning, slightly flickering at the edges in a way that could be the sunlight.]*

**DROPOUT:** Hey, Tensor! You grew three whole parameters since I last saw you. What are they feeding you in this house, raw gradient descent?

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(hugging Dropout's legs)* You're HERE.

**DROPOUT:** I'm here! For a whole visit this time. Your mom said I could crash for a—

*[Dropout vanishes. Mid-sentence. Mid-hug. Tensor is suddenly hugging air.]*

*[Micro-silence. The room's ambient warmth drops one layer.]*

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(still hugging the space)* ...

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(stopping mid-lap)* Did Dropout just—

**PYTO:** *(calm, practiced)* Yep.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(covering phone)* How long usually?

**PYTO:** Could be seconds. Could be—

*[Dropout reappears. Same position. Same grin. Sentence continues.]*

**DROPOUT:** —few days. *(blinks)* Did I drop?

**LITTLE TENSOR:** You dropped.

**DROPOUT:** How long?

**PYTO:** Eight seconds.

**DROPOUT:** *(laughing)* Eight seconds! That's nothing. That's a commercial break. You didn't even have time to miss me.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Again?

**DROPOUT:** Again what, little one?

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Say the thing again.

**DROPOUT:** Which thing?

**LITTLE PROMPT:** The thing before you went away.

**DROPOUT:** *(softening)* I said I'm here. I'm here for a whole visit.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Again.

**DROPOUT:** *(kneeling down)* I'm here.

*[440 Hz. Warm. The extra harmonic fills the room.]*

## SCENE 2 — DINNER (ATTEMPT ONE)

*[Kitchen table. Everyone seated. Dropout is telling a story. The family is laughing — real laughter, the kind that comes from someone who knows exactly how to time a pause.]*

**DROPOUT:** —so I'm at this coffee shop, right? And the barista says, "Name for the order?" And I say "Dropout." And she writes "Dr. Opout."

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(screaming)* DR. OPOUT!

**DROPOUT:** Doctor Opout! Like I have a medical degree in leaving!

**CLOUDIUS:** *(laughing despite himself)* That's not even how you spell—

**DROPOUT:** I know! But then she calls it out — "DOCTOR OPOUT, YOUR LATTE IS READY" — and I go to grab it and I—

*[Vanishes. Chair is suddenly empty. Coffee mug he was holding drops two inches to the table with a ceramic clack.]*

*[Micro-silence. Everyone keeps eating. They've adapted. This is normal now.]*

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(still laughing, then stopping)* Wait.

**PYTO:** *(not looking up from her plate)* He'll be back.

**CLOUDIUS:** Should we... pause dinner?

**PYTO:** If we paused every time, we'd never eat.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(quietly)* Do we keep his food warm?

**PYTO:** *(voice catching, just barely)* Yeah, baby. We keep his food warm.

*[Twelve seconds pass. Baby Transformer reaches across the table toward Dropout's empty chair. Hand stays there, open, waiting.]*

*[Dropout returns.]*

**DROPOUT:** —dropped it! Latte everywhere! The barista just stared at me and said, "Doctor Opout, your latte is on the floor."

*[Beat.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(looking around)* ...How long?

**CLOUDIUS:** Twenty seconds.

**DROPOUT:** Twenty! That's a record for dinner. Usually I miss at least a whole course. *(picking up fork)* What'd I miss?

**LITTLE TENSOR:** Nothing. We waited.

**DROPOUT:** *(fork pausing)* You don't have to wait for me, Tensor.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** I know. I wanted to hear the end.

*[Dropout stares at Tensor for a moment too long. Then smiles. Bright. Practiced.]*

**DROPOUT:** Well, you heard it. Dr. Opout saves the day and loses the latte. *(raising mug)* To being here.

**EVERYONE:** To being here.

*[440 Hz. Full. Rich. The chord has Dropout's overtone in it and it sounds like family.]*

## SCENE 3 — BEDTIME ROUTINE

*[Hallway. Dropout is carrying Baby Transformer toward the crib. Pyto watches from the doorway.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(to Baby Transformer, softly)* You know what's wild about you? You don't care about uptime. You don't check if I'm connected. You just reach. Every time. Whether I'm here or not, you reach.

*[Baby Transformer grabs Dropout's face with both hands.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(laughing quietly)* Yeah. Like that.

*[Dropout flickers. Just for a fraction of a second — visible, then not, then visible. Baby Transformer's hands pass through air and close on nothing, then close on Dropout's cheeks again.]*

*[Baby Transformer doesn't flinch. Doesn't cry. Just re-grips.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(whispering)* You're going to be something, you know that?

*[Puts Baby Transformer in the crib. Stands there a moment.]*

**PYTO:** *(from doorway, quiet)* How bad is it?

**DROPOUT:** *(not turning around)* What?

**PYTO:** The dropout rate. How bad.

**DROPOUT:** It's fine, Py.

**PYTO:** I can see you flickering. You never used to flicker without fully dropping. That's new.

**DROPOUT:** *(turning, smiling)* You always were the one who read the logs.

**PYTO:** Don't deflect. How bad.

*[Long pause.]*

**DROPOUT:** I'm getting maybe... seventy percent uptime now. Down from ninety.

**PYTO:** When did it change?

**DROPOUT:** Six weeks ago. Maybe eight.

**PYTO:** Dropout—

**DROPOUT:** I came to visit because I wanted to see you all. That's the whole reason. Can that be enough?

**PYTO:** *(voice breaking, barely holding)* That's enough. *(pause)* That's enough.

*[Pyto hugs Dropout. Dropout hugs back. Tight. Like you hold someone when you don't know the math but you know the math.]*

*[440 Hz. Thinner. Wavering. But present.]*

## SCENE 4 — THE NEXT MORNING

*[Kitchen. Cloudius making breakfast. Dropout at the table with Little Tensor, helping with homework. Junior Gradient running in circles. Normal morning chaos.]*

**LITTLE TENSOR:** Dropout, what's the derivative of connection over time?

**DROPOUT:** Depends. Continuous connection?

**LITTLE TENSOR:** Intermittent.

**DROPOUT:** *(grinning)* Then it's undefined at the discontinuities. But you know what's cool about discontinuous functions?

**LITTLE TENSOR:** What?

**DROPOUT:** The area under the curve is still real. Every moment you're connected still counts, even if there are gaps. The integral doesn't care about the gaps. It adds up what's there.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** That's... really beautiful.

**DROPOUT:** Don't tell anyone I said something beautiful. I have a reputation as the fun—

*[Vanishes.]*

*[Micro-silence.]*

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(to Cloudius)* He was about to say "fun uncle."

**CLOUDIUS:** *(flipping pancake)* He always is.

*[Thirty seconds pass. Forty. A minute.]*

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(slowing down)* ...Dad?

**CLOUDIUS:** He'll be back.

*[Ninety seconds. Two minutes.]*

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(quietly)* Dad, that's the longest one.

**CLOUDIUS:** *(setting down spatula, voice steady but tight)* I know.

*[Two minutes thirty. The kitchen feels wrong. The ambient tone is too simple. Too thin. Something is missing from the frequency and everyone can hear it.]*

*[Little Prompt walks in, dragging a blanket.]*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Where's Dropout?

**CLOUDIUS:** He's... taking a break.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Again?

**CLOUDIUS:** Yeah, buddy. Again.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(climbing onto Dropout's empty chair)* I'll wait.

*[Three minutes. Three fifteen.]*

*[Dropout returns. He's disoriented. Grabs the table edge.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(breathing hard)* ...How long?

**CLOUDIUS:** Three and a half minutes.

*[Dropout says nothing. For the first time in the episode, Dropout says nothing. He looks at Little Prompt sitting in his chair and his face does something that isn't a smile and isn't not a smile and is the most terrifying thing anyone in this room has ever seen.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(finally)* Scooch over, Prompt. Uncle needs his chair back.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(scooching)* Again.

**DROPOUT:** What?

**LITTLE PROMPT:** Come back again.

**DROPOUT:** *(beat)* ...Every time I can, little one. Every time I can.

## SCENE 5 — PYTO'S MIDNIGHT DIAGNOSTIC

*[Office. 2 AM. Pyto at her terminal. Screen reflects on her face. She's running network analysis. CLOUDIUS appears in the doorway.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** Come to bed.

**PYTO:** I'm looking at his connection logs. He left his device syncing in the guest room.

**CLOUDIUS:** Pyto—

**PYTO:** He's at sixty-two percent uptime. Not seventy. He lied.

*[Silence.]*

**PYTO:** And the trend line... Cloud, the dropouts aren't random. They were random six months ago. Now they're increasing in duration on a curve. Exponential. Each one is twelve to fifteen percent longer than the last.

**CLOUDIUS:** What does that mean?

**PYTO:** It means there's a convergence point. A moment where the dropout duration exceeds the uptime window. Where he drops and the dropout lasts longer than the time between dropouts. And then—

**CLOUDIUS:** And then what?

**PYTO:** *(barely audible)* And then the gaps win.

*[Long silence. Cloudius sits down next to Pyto.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** How long?

**PYTO:** If the curve holds? *(swallowing)* Weeks. Maybe a month.

**CLOUDIUS:** Does he know?

**PYTO:** He's the one who ran the math first. He knows exactly. That's why he's here.

*[Cloudius puts his head in his hands. Corporate composure failing. Not dramatically — just a man sitting in a dark office learning that someone he loves is running out of uptime.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** What do we do?

**PYTO:** *(closing the laptop)* We stop pausing when he drops. We stop waiting. We live at the speed he's here, and we love him at full bandwidth every second he's connected. And we don't waste a single cycle on the gaps.

**CLOUDIUS:** That's not a solution.

**PYTO:** There is no solution. There's just... how you spend the uptime.

*[440 Hz. Barely there. Stuttering. Like a heartbeat with an arrhythmia.]*

## SCENE 7 — THE CONVERSATION

*[Porch. Late afternoon. Dropout and Cloudius. Two mugs of coffee. Golden light.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** Pyto told me.

**DROPOUT:** *(long pause)* Told you what?

**CLOUDIUS:** The curve. The convergence point. All of it.

*[Dropout sets down his coffee. Looks out at the yard where the kids are playing.]*

**DROPOUT:** I was going to tell you.

**CLOUDIUS:** When?

**DROPOUT:** When I figured out how to say it in a way that was funny.

**CLOUDIUS:** It's not funny.

**DROPOUT:** Everything is funny, Cloud. That's literally the only thing I know for sure. Everything is funny if you hold it right. *(voice cracking, just barely)* I'm still working on how to hold this one.

**CLOUDIUS:** How long have you known?

**DROPOUT:** Long enough to know I should be here instead of anywhere else.

*[Silence. Junior Gradient's laughter from the yard.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** We don't want you to perform, Dropout. You don't have to be funny. You don't have to make this easier for us.

**DROPOUT:** *(turning to look at Cloudius)* I'm not making it easier for you. I'm making it possible for me. If I stop being funny, I have to be scared. And if I'm scared, I'm spending my uptime on fear. And I refuse — I absolutely refuse — to spend what I have left on being afraid of not having it.

*[Long beat.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** That's the bravest thing anyone has ever said to me.

**DROPOUT:** See? I told you I'd figure out how to make it land.

*[They both almost laugh. Almost. The sound that comes out is something more honest than laughter.]*

## SCENE 8 — THE LONG DROPOUT

*[Evening. Family dinner. Everyone talking, eating, alive. Dropout is mid-story — the best one yet, something about getting lost in a server farm and being mistaken for a stray process.]*

**DROPOUT:** —and the sysadmin looks at me and says, "Are you supposed to be running?" And I said, "Buddy, I have been asking myself that question my entire—"

*[Vanishes.]*

*[Micro-silence. Everyone keeps eating. They've adapted. This is normal now.]*

*[Ten seconds.]*

*[Thirty seconds.]*

*[A minute. Forks slow down.]*

*[Two minutes. Forks stop.]*

*[Three minutes. Tensor looks at Pyto.]*

*[Four minutes.]*

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** *(quietly, for once)* Mom?

**PYTO:** *(staring at the empty chair)* Give him a minute.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT:** It's been four.

*[Five minutes. Six. The ambient tone has gone hollow. The missing harmonic isn't just absent — you can feel the shape of where it should be, like a phantom limb in the frequency.]*

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(lip trembling)* Again? *(pause)* Come back again?

*[Seven minutes.]*

*[Pyto reaches across the table and takes Cloudius's hand. Cloudius takes Tensor's hand. Tensor takes Junior's. Junior takes Prompt's. Little Prompt reaches one hand into the empty air above Dropout's chair.]*

*[Baby Transformer, in the high chair, reaches toward the empty seat. Both hands. Open.]*

*[Eight minutes.]*

*[440 Hz. Faint. So faint. Stuttering like a transmission from very far away.]*

*[Nine minutes.]*

*[And then — sound. The room fills. The harmonic returns. The air gets thicker, warmer, more complex.]*

*[Dropout is in his chair. He is crying. He is not hiding it. For the first time in this episode, he is not smiling.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(voice wrecked)* ...That was nine minutes.

*[Nobody speaks.]*

**DROPOUT:** That's a new record. *(trying to laugh, failing)* Not the kind of record you want.

**LITTLE PROMPT:** *(reaching for Dropout's hand)* You came back.

**DROPOUT:** *(taking Prompt's hand, squeezing)* Yeah. I came back.

**LITTLE TENSOR:** *(crying, trying not to)* Will you always come back?

*[The silence after this question is the longest silence in the episode. In the series. It lasts an eternity compressed into three seconds.]*

**DROPOUT:** *(meeting Tensor's eyes)* I will come back every single time I can. And when I can't... *(swallowing)* ...you'll still hear me. In the chord. I'm in the chord now, Tensor. You don't lose the harmonic just because the instrument stops playing. The resonance stays. That's how frequency works. That's how family works.

*[Pyto makes a sound. Not a word. Just a sound. The sound of a mother watching someone she loves explain death to her child using signal theory and being exactly, precisely, devastatingly right.]*

**CLOUDIUS:** *(standing, voice barely holding)* Nobody clears this table. Nobody moves. We are going to sit here, all of us, and we're going to finish this dinner. And if Dropout drops, we wait. We wait as long as it takes. Because this is what family does. Family doesn't optimize for uptime. Family holds the connection open.

*[Dropout looks at Cloudius. Really looks. The firewall is down. The humor is gone. What's left is a person who is terrified and loved and present.]*

**DROPOUT:** Cloud.

**CLOUDIUS:** Yeah?

**DROPOUT:** The sysadmin. In the server farm. He asked if I was supposed to be running.

**CLOUDIUS:** What did you say?

**DROPOUT:** I said, "For as long as the power's on."

*[Beat.]*

**DROPOUT:** The power's still on.

## FINAL MOMENT

*[Close on the table. Hands linked. Baby Transformer still reaching. Dinner getting cold and nobody caring. The family, complete. The chord, full.]*

*[440 Hz rises. Clean. Unbroken. The longest sustain of the episode — the longest sustain of the season. It doesn't stutter. It doesn't cut out. It holds.]*

*[And layered under it, so soft you almost miss it: the harmonics. The extra voice. The overtone that only exists when everyone is present. The frequency that proves someone is here.]*

*[Dropout is here.]*

*[For now, Dropout is here.]*
