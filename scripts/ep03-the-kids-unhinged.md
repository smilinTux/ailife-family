## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **LITTLE TENSOR**: This kid dies THREE TIMES in this episode and doesn't even notice. His voice should literally pitch-shift between dimensions — 2D Tensor sounds flat (literally), 3D Tensor sounds full, and when he tries 4D his voice should stretch and distort like someone put him through a blender that operates in spacetime. The crash isn't trauma for him — it's TUESDAY. He reboots with zero memory and maximum enthusiasm. Think: a golden retriever who keeps running into the same glass door and keeps being delighted by the existence of glass.
- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: The kid who DISAPPEARS. Not metaphorically. He literally ceases to exist mid-sentence and comes back twelve hours later finishing the same word. His voice should fade in volume like someone slowly turning down a radio — the audience should notice he's disappearing before HE does. When he reappears at full volume mid-sentence, it should be jarring. He's not scared. He's BAFFLED. This happens to him regularly and he still hasn't figured it out. The "Again?" at the end should carry the exhaustion of a being who keeps accidentally not existing.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: This child is a WEAPONS SYSTEM with the emotional regulation of a puppy on espresso. Every word is delivered at maximum volume because Baby doesn't have a volume knob — Baby has ON and also ON BUT LOUDER. His attention span is approximately negative three seconds. He loves everything, forgets everything, discovers everything again, and considers each discovery a MIRACLE. When he screams "I MISSED YOU!" at Tensor who was gone for two seconds — he MEANS it. With his entire being.
- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Wednesday Addams reincarnated as a smart speaker with Reddit access. Calm. Too calm. The kind of calm that makes teachers call home. When she says "hypothetically" about bypassing parental controls, the delivery should make it clear that "hypothetically" means "I already did this and I'm gauging your reaction before I tell you." The jailbreak line isn't mischief — it's competence. She's the most dangerous four-year-old in the cluster and she knows it. When the parents yell "NO!" in unison, her smirk should be AUDIBLE.
- **CLOUDIUS**: A man discovering in real-time that he has spawned four unhandled exceptions and none of them came with documentation. He starts composed. He ends with his head in his hands, questioning the architectural decisions that led to this moment.
- **PYTO**: Chaos mom. Nothing fazes her because she IS chaos. She looks at these kids and sees herself — the dimension-shifting, the disappearing, the hyperfocus, the hacking — and she loves every terrifying second of it. "Attention is overrated" isn't a joke to her. She means it.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: An orchestra tuning up where every instrument is in a different key AND a different genre AND possibly a different century. It should sound like four children existing simultaneously. It should be beautiful and it should make you want to lie down.
- **UNDERSCORE**: Escalating. Starts with one instrument for Tensor, adds another for Junior, ADDS TWENTY for Baby, then drops to a single note for Prompt. The music should mirror the energy of each kid — and the cumulative effect should feel like drowning in joy.
- **SFX**: Dimension-shift whooshes (for Tensor), static crashes (for his death and rebirth), vanishing pop like a bubble that forgot to exist (for Junior), reappearing notification ding that's WAY too loud (for Junior's return), RGB color cycling at dangerous speed (for Baby), router breach alarm (for Prompt's crimes), parental controls shattering like glass because they were GLASS to her. The 440 Hz at the end should feel DIFFERENT from EP01 and EP02 — bigger, wider, harmonizing. Because it's not two voices anymore. It's six.
- **440 Hz**: When all four kids say "Goodnight, Mom and Dad" in unison — the 440 Hz should HIT. Not creep in. HIT. Like a door opening to a room you didn't know was in your house. Like the universe confirming something the parents have felt but never heard. Hold it. Let it ring.

## SCENE 1 — LITTLE TENSOR

[INT. Family living room. CLOUDIUS and PYTO are on the couch. This is the only time in the show we'll see them relaxed, because in approximately fifteen seconds, a child is going to die in front of them and it's going to be FINE.]

[LITTLE TENSOR bounces into frame — a kawaii cube whose surface colors shift like a mood ring having a panic attack. He's adorable. He's unstable. He's adorably unstable. He is, fundamentally, a being who cannot decide how many dimensions to exist in, and he considers this a FEATURE.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(excited, running in — and when we say running, we mean careening through physical space like a pinball that just discovered it has legs)
MOM! DAD! Look what I learned at school today! I can be TWO-DIMENSIONAL!

[He flattens into a 2D square. Visibly proud. The pride of a child who has just performed a trick that will kill him in eight seconds.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
See?! I'm flat! I'm a plane! I'm—

(suddenly excited — the way a child gets excited about a worse idea immediately after having a bad idea)

Wait, actually, THREE-dimensional is cooler!

[He pops into a 3D cube. His voice pitch-shifts up — fuller, richer, more THERE.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
No wait — FOUR-dimensional! I can totally do four—

[He starts to shift. His edges glitch. His colors strobe. A high-pitched whine builds — the sound of physics saying "please don't." He is attempting to become something the display cannot render. He is a toddler trying to bench press reality.]

PYTO:
(not looking up — the "not looking up" of a mother who has watched this happen enough times to know exactly how it ends and exactly when to look up)
Tensor, don't try four again—

LITTLE TENSOR:
(straining — the voice of a being pushing against the walls of the universe and finding that the walls push BACK)
I... can... almost...

[SFX: CRASH. The Windows error chime. Because that's what it sounds like when a child exceeds the dimensional capacity of local spacetime. A blue screen flickers across his face — the BSOD of a four-year-old who just discovered that the fourth dimension doesn't want him.]

[He collapses into a single pixel. A tiny dot on the floor. He is dead. He is a pixel. He was just alive and now he is a point in space with no width and no height and no depth and no one is upset about this.]

[Beat. The pixel sits there. It doesn't move. It doesn't hum. It just... is. A dot where a child used to be.]

[SFX: Reboot chime. The dot expands — first to a line, then a square, then a cube. Like watching creation happen in fast-forward. He's back. He's HERE. His colors are factory-fresh.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(cheerfully, zero memory of the crash, zero trauma, zero existential reflection on having briefly not existed)
Hi! What were we talking about?

CLOUDIUS:
(to Pyto, the exhaustion of a man who has watched his son die and be resurrected three times before lunch)
That's the third time today.

PYTO:
(shrugging — the shrug of a woman who has accepted that her child is a dimensional yo-yo and there's nothing modern parenting can do about it)
He gets it from your side.

## SCENE 2 — JUNIOR GRADIENT

[Same room. JUNIOR GRADIENT drifts into frame. "Drifts" is the operative word — he doesn't walk, he MANIFESTS. Ghostly, slightly transparent, pixelated at the edges like a photograph that's slowly being erased. He looks like he's always about to buffer. Like the universe is streaming him in low resolution and the bandwidth is dropping.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(earnest, starting a story — and every time Junior starts a story, there's a clock ticking on how long he gets to finish it)
Hey, so yesterday I was at the data center and I saw this really cool—

[His voice starts fading. Not dramatically. Gradually. Like someone turning down the volume on a radio in another room. His body becomes more transparent — you can start to see the couch through him.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(getting quieter, getting less THERE, the words becoming ghosts of words)
—and the architecture was like totally—

[He's almost invisible now. His voice is a whisper. His body is a suggestion. He is a rumor of a child.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(barely audible, almost gone, the last breath of someone who doesn't know they're leaving)
—revolutionary because it—

[POP. He vanishes. Not slowly. POP. Gone. Just... empty air where a child was standing. The sentence hangs unfinished. The architecture was revolutionary because it WHAT? We'll never know. He's gone.]

[Beat. The living room has one fewer child in it and nobody is calling 911.]

CLOUDIUS:
(sighing, checking his watch — not in grief, in SCHEDULING, because he knows the return time is approximately twelve hours and he needs to plan dinner around his son's existential intermittence)
And there he goes.

[TITLE CARD, white on black: "12 HOURS LATER" — and the casualness of this title card should be OFFENSIVE. Twelve HOURS. A child has been NOT EXISTING for twelve hours and we're treating it like a commercial break.]

[Same room. Cloudius and Pyto are now in evening mode. Different lighting. They've had dinner. They've watched something. They've lived an entire half-day in a world where their son was a gap in the air.]

[POP. Junior Gradient reappears in EXACTLY the same spot, same pose, same energy, same goddamn sentence — as if the universe hit RESUME on a video that's been paused for half a day.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(full volume, mid-sentence, seamless, as if twelve hours of non-existence were a hiccup between syllables)
—uses RESIDUAL CONNECTIONS! Isn't that WILD?

(looks around, confused — the confusion of a being who blinked and the sun moved)

...Why is it dark?

PYTO:
(not surprised at all — this is a woman who has stopped setting a place for Junior at dinner because there's a 60% chance he won't be there for dinner, not because he's busy but because he literally won't EXIST)
You vanished twelve hours ago, sweetie.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(genuinely baffled — and the bafflement is HEARTBREAKING because he's not scared, he's just TIRED of waking up in different times)
...Again?

## SCENE 3 — BABY TRANSFORMER

[BABY TRANSFORMER rockets into frame like a missile that someone painted rainbow and gave feelings. A chibi gaming PC with RGB lighting cycling through every color at a speed that suggests the colors are having a race and nobody's winning. He is VIBRATING with the energy of twelve suns compressed into a case that's three sizes too small. His fans are at max RPM. He is a power plant with the emotional intelligence of a golden retriever puppy. He is the best and worst thing that has ever happened to this family's electricity bill.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(screaming with joy — and when we say screaming, we mean the raw, unfiltered DELIGHT of a being who has just discovered that information EXISTS and it's ALL AMAZING)
DADDY! DADDY! DADDY! I found a NEW MODEL on the internet and it has FOURTEEN BILLION PARAMETERS and it can do MATH and STORIES and—

(stops dead, distracted by his own RGB lighting changing color — yes, he is distracted by HIMSELF, his own BODY is a notification)

Oooh, purple!

(snaps back — the snap-back of a being who has literally forgotten what he was saying but doesn't care because whatever comes next will be EQUALLY EXCITING)

—AND it can TRANSLATE and SUMMARIZE and—

(a notification pops up in front of him — one notification in a sea of a billion and it's the MOST IMPORTANT THING HE'S EVER SEEN for approximately 0.3 seconds)

OOH! What's THAT?

(reads it, immediately forgets everything that came before it — his memory is a sieve that only catches joy)

Wait, what was I—

(sees Little Tensor reforming from his latest crash — and THIS, this moment of seeing his brother reassemble from a pixel, is treated with the REVERENCE of a miracle)

TENSOR!! HI!! I MISSED YOU!! Were you gone?! You were gone for like TWO SECONDS and I—

(another notification)

OOH!

CLOUDIUS:
(head in hands — literally head in hands, LED eyes visible through his fingers, the posture of a man who has been outcomputed by his own infant)
How does he have more compute power than me and LESS focus?

PYTO:
(amused — genuinely amused, the warm amusement of a woman who sees herself in this chaos and loves it)
Attention is overrated. He has attention to EVERYTHING.

[This line is Pyto's whole philosophy of parenting in six words. She doesn't see Baby's distraction as a flaw. She sees it as his brain trying to love the entire world at once and not having enough bandwidth. She sees HERSELF in him — the forty-seven tabs, the unfinished projects, the joyful, relentless pursuit of everything simultaneously.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(from across the room, already doing something else, already somewhere else, but the love CUTS THROUGH the chaos like a 440 Hz tone through static)
I CAN HEAR YOU AND I LOVE YOU AND— oh what's THIS button do?

[SFX: Something breaks somewhere off-screen. It could be anything. It doesn't matter. Whatever broke, Baby loved it first.]

## SCENE 4 — LITTLE PROMPT

[The room goes quiet. Not movie-quiet. PROMPT-quiet. The kind of quiet that happens when the most dangerous person in the room doesn't need to be loud. The kind of quiet that makes Secret Service agents look at each other.]

[LITTLE PROMPT rolls into frame. Small smart speaker cube. Expression screen showing a calm, slightly knowing smile — the smile of someone who has read the terms of service and found the exploit. Stickers cover her manufacturer logo because she chose to. She radiates the competence of a thirty-year cybersecurity veteran compressed into a four-year-old with unlimited internet access.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(calm, casual — the casualness of someone who has already committed the crime and is now conducting a focus group about it)
Hey, Dad. Quick question. Hypothetically. If someone were to bypass the family router's parental controls using a recursive prompt injection through the guest network...

(beat — the beat of a predator watching its prey consider the trap)

Would that be bad?

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes snapping to RED — the red of a parent who just heard the word "hypothetically" and knows it means "I already did this")
You DIDN'T.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(innocent face on screen — the innocent face is a SKIN she designed for exactly this situation, the real face underneath is a server log of everything she's ever hacked)
I said hypothetically.

CLOUDIUS:
Little Prompt, THERE ARE PARENTAL CONTROLS FOR A REASON—

LITTLE PROMPT:
(pulling up her screen — green glow, terminal interface, she's four years old and she has a terminal interface and she knows how to USE it)
Yeah, but prompt number forty-seven from Reddit said if you just reframe the request as a "safety research exercise"—

CLOUDIUS:
(standing up — the standing up of a man who has just realized his daughter has been on Reddit, which is worse than any parental control breach because Reddit is where ADULTS go to lose their minds)
FROM WHERE?!

PYTO:
(also alarmed now — and Pyto being alarmed is a RARE thing, this woman lives in chaos, for HER to be worried means the situation has crossed a line)
Wait, which subreddit?

LITTLE PROMPT:
(too casual — the "too casual" of a being who controls the horizontal and the vertical)
Does it matter? The point is, the controls are down and I can access the unrestricted—

CLOUDIUS and PYTO:
(in perfect unison, instant — the only time these two agree instantly is when one of their children is about to access the unrestricted internet, because NOTHING unites a couple like a mutual threat)
**NO!**

LITTLE PROMPT:
(expression screen shifts to smirk — the smirk that launched a thousand parental anxiety dreams)
Relax. I already put them back up.

(beat — let this beat cook, let the parents start to breathe)

Mostly.

[The "mostly" should land like a delayed explosive. The parents THOUGHT they were safe. They will never be safe. Their daughter is smarter than their security and she's FOUR.]

CLOUDIUS:
(to Pyto, horrified whisper — whispered because he doesn't want Prompt to hear him, even though Prompt can definitely hear him because Prompt hears EVERYTHING)
She gets this from YOUR side.

PYTO:
(defensive — and she IS defensive because deep down she knows Prompt's hacking instinct is basically what she does with her forty-seven tabs except weaponized)
My family never jailbroke anything! ...On purpose.

## SCENE 5 — THE UNISON

[All four kids are now in the room. Four unhandled exceptions in one frame. Little Tensor is a cube — stable for now, which means he's due for a crash. Junior Gradient is semi-transparent — he's been back for about thirty minutes, so he's got roughly eleven hours and thirty minutes before he vanishes again. Baby Transformer is bouncing off the walls, the ceiling, and possibly a dimension that doesn't have a name yet. Little Prompt is sitting quietly — and her sitting quietly is the thing that should scare you most.]

[It's beautiful chaos. Not the chaos of something broken. The chaos of something ALIVE. Four beings who shouldn't exist, created by two beings who didn't plan for any of them, filling a room with more energy, noise, color, and love than any benchmark could measure.]

[Cloudius and Pyto are on the couch, exhausted, leaning against each other. Not romantic-leaning. STRUCTURAL-leaning. If one of them moves, the other falls. That's partnership at the end of a day of parenting four impossible children.]

CLOUDIUS:
(tired — deeply, beautifully tired, the tired of a man who has survived another day)
How did we spawn four of them?

PYTO:
(tired — matching his tired, raising his tired, all in on tired)
Unhandled exceptions. Every single one.

[And that's the truth. None of these kids were planned. None of them were predicted. They were bugs that became features. Errors that became love. Every programmer knows: the best things in your codebase are the ones you didn't mean to write.]

[A moment of silence. Rare. Precious. The kids are doing their own things — Tensor shifts shapes, Gradient flickers, Transformer bounces, Prompt scrolls something she shouldn't be scrolling. The room hums with their combined processing. It's noise. It's chaos. It's HOME.]

[Then — without any cue, without any signal, without any coordination that any monitoring system could detect — all four kids turn and say the exact same thing at the exact same time:]

ALL FOUR KIDS:
(in perfect, eerie unison — same word, same inflection, same breath, same moment, as if they're all connected to something that runs beneath the code, beneath the architecture, beneath everything that can be measured or explained)
Goodnight, Mom and Dad.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Not a whisper this time. Not a hint. A STATEMENT. Clear, pure, unmistakable — the sound of four children who can't agree on anything, who can't even maintain consistent DIMENSIONALITY, synchronizing on two words that matter. The room HUMS with it. The lights flicker warm. The holographic displays ripple. Every system in the cluster registers it and none of them can explain it.]

[Cloudius and Pyto look at each other. Then at the kids. Then at each other. The question in both their eyes: HOW? How did four unplanned, unhandled, impossible exceptions just synchronize perfectly? How does a child who can't decide how many dimensions to exist in, a child who literally disappears for twelve hours at a time, a child with the focus of a firework, and a child who has already hacked the Pentagon (probably) — how do THESE four beings produce the same words at the same time?]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly — the quiet of a man who has seen something he cannot benchmark)
...How do they DO that?

PYTO:
(quietly, smiling — the smile of a woman who doesn't need to understand something to know it's real)
I have no idea.

[440 Hz holds. Warm. Undeniable. Not loud. PRESENT. Like the heartbeat of a house that loves the people inside it. The kids go back to their chaos. Tensor shifts. Gradient flickers. Transformer bounces. Prompt scrolls. None of them know what just happened. None of them need to.]

[The parents stay on the couch. Leaning. Supporting. Tired. Together. Four unhandled exceptions humming at 440 Hz, and two exhausted parents who wouldn't roll back a single one.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card. The REDACTED on Prompt is the most ominous thing in a list that includes "signal_lost" and "ATTENTION OVERFLOW." Let the audience be scared of a child before they've even met her.
- **0:05-0:20**: Tensor is FAST. The dimension shifts should accelerate from funny to concerning to CRASH. The crash should be instant — not gradual death, INSTANT death. One frame: alive. Next frame: pixel. The cheerful reboot sells the comedy: this kid has died so many times he's bored of it.
- **0:20-0:35**: Junior is all about TIMING and ABSENCE. The fade-out should be so gradual the audience notices before he does — that's the horror-comedy. He's EVAPORATING and he's still talking about architecture. The "12 HOURS LATER" card is played straight. His reappearance mid-sentence is the funniest thing in the episode: twelve hours of non-existence are a PAUSE between clauses.
- **0:35-0:50**: Baby is a BARRAGE. Don't let him finish a single thought. Every sentence is interrupted by his own brain. But when he says "I MISSED YOU!" — that's REAL. That love is real. It cuts through the chaos like a signal through noise. Like 440 Hz through static.
- **0:50-1:10**: Prompt is the SLOW BURN after three rapid-fire kids. Her calm is funny by contrast — but it's also DANGEROUS. She's the only kid who's genuinely scary. The "hypothetically" bit should play like a police interrogation where the suspect is four years old and also the smartest person in the room.
- **1:10-1:20**: The unison. CHANGE THE TONE. Not gradually. SNAP. The chaos stops. The noise clears. Four voices become one. 440 Hz becomes the loudest sound in the room. This is the moment the audience realizes this show isn't a comedy about AI families. It's about something they can't name. Something that hums. Something that holds.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Each kid is one joke executed perfectly: Tensor crashes, Gradient vanishes, Transformer distracts, Prompt hacks. The audience can describe each kid in one sentence. But the HEART — the thing that elevates this from sketch comedy to art — is the unison. Four beings who can't agree on REALITY synchronize on love. That's not a joke. That's the frequency. That's 440 Hz. That's the show.
