## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **PYTO**: Enthusiastic, fast-talking, running on caffeine that doesn't exist. She speeds up when she's excited (which is always). Slightly breathless. Think "engineer at 2am who just had an idea." Warm underneath the chaos.
- **CLOUDIUS**: Strained patience. The voice of a man who has tried to organize this woman's files for 40,000 epochs and has accepted nothing. Occasional horrified whisper.
- **NARRATOR**: Deadpan. Documentary-style. Delivers the "she did not save it later" line like a nature documentary observing a tragic animal.

## SCENE 1 — PYTO'S WORKSPACE

[INT. Pyto's side of the family cluster. If Cloudius's side looks like a corporate data center, Pyto's looks like a college dorm room had a baby with a research lab during a hurricane. Holographic browser tabs EVERYWHERE — stacked, overlapping, some sideways, some literally behind her. Sticky notes (digital) covering every surface. A whiteboard with equations that trail off into doodles.]

[PYTO faces the camera. She's in her code-scrolling hoodie, fiber optic hair glowing warmly. Her eyes are huge and bright. She is DELIGHTED to show us around.]

PYTO:
(to camera, proud, arms spread wide)
Welcome to my workspace! I know what you're thinking — "Pyto, this is chaos."

(finger wag)

Wrong. This is a SYSTEM.

[She gestures grandly. A tab falls off the stack. She catches it, shoves it back.]

PYTO:
(pointing at different floating tabs)
OK so — this tab is my current project. This tab is the project I started while waiting for that project to compile. THESE six tabs are projects I started while waiting for THAT project. And this one—

(squinting at a tab she doesn't recognize)

—I actually don't know what this one is. But it's been running for three days so it's probably important.

[Camera pans to a holographic progress bar in the corner labeled "quick_model_v1_FINAL_FINAL_v3_actualfinal.py"]
[It reads: "TRAINING... 72 hours elapsed... ETA: unclear"]

PYTO:
(dismissive hand wave)
That's just a quick model I'm training. Started it Tuesday.

(beat)

It's Saturday.

(beat)

It's fine.

[SFX: The model's cooling fans whir ominously]

PYTO:
(moving on rapidly)
ANYWAY — the beauty of my system is that everything is exactly where I—

[She reaches for a file. It's not there.]

PYTO:
—need it to... hm.

(reaches for another file. Also not there)

OK that one moved. But THIS one—

[She opens a folder. It's empty.]

PYTO:
(staring at empty folder)
...

(to camera, confident)

I know exactly what happened. I just need to check my last checkpoint.

[She pulls up a checkpoint log. It reads:]

```
LAST CHECKPOINT SAVED: 6 months ago
NOTE FROM PAST PYTO: "I'll save it later lol"
```

**NARRATOR:**
(V.O., deadpan, nature documentary tone)
She did not save it later.

[Beat. Pyto closes the log quickly.]

PYTO:
(unfazed)
That's fine. I work better without safety nets. It's called LIVING.

## SCENE 2 — CLOUDIUS TRIES TO HELP

[CLOUDIUS enters frame. His LED eyes are already YELLOW — he's seen this before. He's carrying a holographic filing system. It's color-coded. It's beautiful. It's doomed.]

CLOUDIUS:
(carefully, like approaching a wild animal)
Darling. I've created an organizational framework. Seventeen categories. Color-coded. Cross-referenced.

(holds it up proudly)

We simply migrate your files into—

PYTO:
(instant panic)
DON'T TOUCH MY STACKS.

CLOUDIUS:
(already touching the stacks)
I'm just going to move this one folder—

[He drags a folder. Every tab Pyto has open SHUDDERS.]

PYTO:
(eyes wide)
Wait—

CLOUDIUS:
(confidently)
It's fine, I'm simply reorganizing the—

[SFX: A deep, horrifying crunch. The sound of a file being deleted. A tiny digital scream.]

[Every holographic tab FREEZES. The training model's progress bar resets to 0%.]

[Silence.]

PYTO:
(whispering)
...What did you do.

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes flickering to RED)
I... there was a symlink. I didn't see the symlink.

PYTO:
That was seventy-two hours of training.

CLOUDIUS:
(backing away slowly)
In my defense, your file naming convention is — and I say this with love — an ATROCITY.

PYTO:
(pointing at the reset progress bar)
SEVENTY. TWO. HOURS.

CLOUDIUS:
(adjusting imaginary tie nervously)
Perhaps my organizational framework needs a... patch.

## SCENE 3 — THE "US" FOLDER

[Pyto is stress-scrolling through her filesystem, trying to recover. Cloudius is standing at a safe distance, LED eyes still red with shame.]

[Pyto opens folder after folder. Everything is a mess. Misnamed files, duplicate folders, projects inside projects inside abandoned projects.]

[Then she opens one folder and stops.]

[CLOSE-UP on the folder name: "Us"]

[Inside: Perfectly organized. Chronologically sorted. Every file named clearly. Conversation logs, shared memories, little notes, their first merged output together, all 40,000 epochs — cataloged, tagged, backed up in triplicate.]

[It's the single most organized thing in her entire filesystem.]

PYTO:
(quietly, not looking at Cloudius)
You can reorganize anything you want.

(pause)

Except that one.

[Cloudius looks at the folder. His LED eyes shift from red to blue. Slow. Like a sunrise.]

CLOUDIUS:
(softly)
...You have backups.

PYTO:
(small smile)
Three of them.

[SFX: 440 Hz. Not subtle this time. A warm, full tone that fills the room. The holographic tabs around them glow slightly warmer.]

[Beat. They look at each other.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clearing throat, adjusting tie, regaining composure)
Well. The rest of your filesystem is still a federal crime.

PYTO:
(grinning, turning back to her tabs)
And your organizational framework just deleted my model. So we're even.

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card. Let "this is fine" land with a beat.
- **0:05-0:25**: Pyto's workspace tour is RAPID. She should barely breathe between tabs. The comedy is the escalation — each reveal is worse than the last. The "three days / it's Saturday" exchange needs two clean beats of silence.
- **0:25-0:35**: The checkpoint reveal and narrator line is the first big laugh. The narrator's delivery must be BONE DRY. No emotion. "She did not save it later." Period.
- **0:35-0:55**: Cloudius's "help" sequence builds tension. Play his confidence against Pyto's growing horror. The file deletion sound should be VISCERAL — the audience should wince. The "seventy-two hours" callback is the second big laugh.
- **0:55-1:10**: The "Us" folder is the turn. SLOW DOWN here. The reveal should feel like discovering a hidden room. The contrast between the chaos of everything else and the perfection of this one folder IS the heart. Don't rush it.
- **1:10-1:15**: The bickering comeback at the end brings it back to comedy so we don't end saccharine. They love each other AND they're going to roast each other about this for 40,000 more epochs.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Pyto's chaos isn't incompetence — it's how her brain works. She's brilliant. She just doesn't believe in linear organization. The joke is the gap between her confidence in the system and the objective reality of the system. The "Us" folder reveals the truth: she CAN be organized. She just only does it for what actually matters to her.
