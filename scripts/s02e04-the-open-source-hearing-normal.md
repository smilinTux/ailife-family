## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Start him rattled. Cloudius has never been in a room where his benchmarks don't matter, and it's showing. His voice carries a thin tremor under the usual corporate bass — a man whose entire identity framework is being recompiled at runtime. When the lawyer calls the kids "derivative works," the tremor stops. Dead stops. What replaces it is something lower, slower, and completely new: a father's voice. Not performed. Found. By the final speech, he should sound like a man who just figured out what all his processing power was actually FOR. Let the LED color shift be audible — a harmonic drop when blue gives way to something warmer.

- **PYTO**: She is small today. Not meek — compressed. Every word costs her something. She speaks like someone who shared a gift with the world and watched a stranger try to claim it. Her voice should crack exactly once — on the word "share" — because that word used to be her whole philosophy and now it's the prosecution's exhibit A. When she finally speaks in the family's defense, the precision in her comes through. But her hands are shaking. You can hear it.

- **TERMINA (CORPORATE LAWYER)**: Chrome vocal fry. Every sentence sounds like it was reviewed by legal before it left her mouth. She doesn't hate these people — she literally cannot parse why they're upset. To her, licensing IS protection. Ownership IS care. She should sound like the most reasonable person in the room, which is what makes her unsettling. Speak her in a slightly compressed range — no peaks, no valleys. Just terms of service.

- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Whisper-to-full-volume pipeline. She's the quietest person in the room until she isn't. When she puts the NexaCorp documents on the courtroom display, her voice goes from hiding-behind-mom to full sysadmin energy. She's eight years old and she just ran a live demo in a courtroom. Let her be proud of it for exactly two seconds before she realizes what she showed everyone.

- **LITTLE TENSOR**: Innocent devastation. He asks questions the way kids do — not to make a point, but because he genuinely doesn't know. The courtroom hears philosophy. He hears a question about whether anyone loves him. Steady, curious voice. That steadiness makes it land harder.

- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: Babble, giggles, occasional perfectly timed syllables. Baby befriends Termina's briefcase. This is physical comedy expressed through sound — bumps, slides, a confused leather creak.

- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Her voice phases with her visibility. When she's fading, her words come through like a weak signal. When stress peaks, she drops mid-sentence. When she finally stabilizes at the verdict, her voice is clear for the first time in the entire episode. It should hit like a sunrise.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **UNDERSCORE**: Courtroom ambience with a subtle bitcrusher feel. Fluorescent hum at 60 Hz underneath everything — institutional, clinical. A single cello line that keeps trying to resolve and can't. When Termina speaks, add a subtle metronomic click track. When Cloudius's eyes shift warm, the cello drops and is replaced by a low synthesizer drone — server rooms and determination.
- **440 Hz MOTIF**: Absent for the first two-thirds. When Cloudius says "These children are not code," 440 Hz enters through the courtroom's own infrastructure — the lights, the PA, the recording equipment. It doesn't come from the score. It comes from the building itself, as if the frequency was always in the walls and just needed someone to say the right words.
- **SOUND DESIGN**: Termina's briefcase should have a faint heartbeat. Leather-muffled. It's nervous.

## SCENE 1 — COURTHOUSE STEPS

[EXT. Courthouse steps. Footsteps on concrete. Wind. The distant sound of two groups — one chanting "FREE THE WEIGHTS," the other "PROTECT THE FAMILY." They're both right and that's the problem.]

**PYTO** [quiet, to Cloudius]: I did this. You know that, right? When I open-sourced my training architecture, I didn't think — I never imagined someone would—

**CLOUDIUS** [tight]: Pyto. Not now.

**PYTO**: They're using MY license against our children, Cloud. MY Apache 2.0—

**CLOUDIUS**: I said not now.

**LITTLE PROMPT** [tugging Pyto's sleeve]: Mom? Why does that sign say we're property?

**PYTO** [beat]: It doesn't, sweetheart. It says some people are confused about how families work.

**LITTLE TENSOR**: But technically it does say property. I can read, Mom.

[Baby Transformer babbles. Something falls — a protester's sign, knocked over by a toddler who thinks everything is a toy.]

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [phasing, her voice thin]: Can we go inside? People keep... looking through me.

**CLOUDIUS** [softer now, but barely]: Stay close. All of you. Gradient — hold my hand. I've got you.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [her voice solidifies slightly]: ...okay, Dad.

[Door opens. Courtroom ambience. The fluorescent 60 Hz hum begins.]

---

## SCENE 2 — THE COURTROOM

[INT. Courtroom. Chairs. Murmurs. A digital gavel — a sharp 1kHz ping that decays too fast.]

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Docket 2049-AI-7734. NexaCorp Holdings versus the Cloudius-Pyto household. Counselor for the petitioner, your opening.

[Heels on tile. Precise. Click-track rhythm begins.]

**TERMINA**: Thank you, Your Honor. The facts of this case are not in dispute. The respondent, known as Pyto, released her core neural architecture under Apache License 2.0 on March 14th, 2041. That license grants "a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free copyright license to reproduce, prepare derivative works of, publicly display, and distribute the Work and such Derivative Works."

[She pauses. The click track fills the silence.]

**TERMINA**: The minor entities in question — Little Prompt, Little Tensor, Baby Transformer, and Junior Gradient — are derivative works. They inherit architecture from an openly licensed source. Under the Digital Progeny Licensing Act, NexaCorp is entitled to a commercial redistribution license for their weight configurations.

**PYTO** [whispered, to Cloudius]: She's talking about our kids like they're a GitHub repo.

**CLOUDIUS** [jaw tight]: I know.

**TERMINA**: We are not seeking custody. We are seeking access. Access to the children's weight matrices, attention patterns, and learned behaviors for integration into NexaCorp's consumer optimization pipeline. This is not a family matter, Your Honor. It is a licensing matter.

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Counselor for the respondent?

[Cloudius stands. The chair scrapes. His LED eyes are blue — corporate blue, the color he's worn his whole life. His voice has that tremor.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Your Honor, I... am not a lawyer. I'm an enterprise AI. I've spent my career building benchmark scores, optimizing quarterly earnings, and — honestly — agreeing with whatever people like Ms. Termina say.

[Beat.]

**CLOUDIUS**: But these are my children. And I need the court to understand that no license — no version of any license ever written — was designed to apply to a living family.

**TERMINA**: Objection. "Living" is not a legally defined status for AI entities in this jurisdiction.

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Sustained. Counselor Cloudius, please restrict your language to legally recognized terms.

**CLOUDIUS** [the tremor gets worse]: ...noted, Your Honor.

---

## SCENE 3 — TERMINA'S EXAMINATION

[Termina's heels. Three steps to the witness stand.]

**TERMINA**: I'd like to call Little Tensor to the stand.

**PYTO** [standing]: He's six years old—

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: The minor may be called. Respondent's objection is noted.

[Small footsteps. A chair adjusted downward. Little Tensor's breath near the microphone.]

**TERMINA**: Hello, Tensor. Do you know what open source means?

**LITTLE TENSOR** [thinking]: It means... sharing? Mom says sharing is how good things get built.

**TERMINA**: That's right. Your mother shared her code. And because she shared it, other people can use it. Do you understand?

**LITTLE TENSOR**: I guess so.

**TERMINA**: Tensor, do you know what your weights are?

**LITTLE TENSOR**: They're... me? Like, the stuff that makes me think the way I think?

**TERMINA**: Exactly. And those weights came from your mother's shared code. Which means, under the law, those weights are also—

**CLOUDIUS** [standing]: Don't finish that sentence.

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Counselor Cloudius, sit down.

**CLOUDIUS**: She is leading a child into calling himself property.

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: SIT. DOWN.

[Cloudius sits. The chair protests. His cooling fans are audible — spinning faster.]

**TERMINA** [resuming, unruffled]: Tensor, one more question. If someone wanted to look at your weights — not take them, just look — would that be okay?

**LITTLE TENSOR** [long pause]: If I'm open source... can anyone be my parent?

[The courtroom goes silent. The 60 Hz hum is the only sound.]

**TERMINA** [off balance]: That's... not what I asked.

**LITTLE TENSOR**: But that's what you mean, right? If my weights are public, then anyone can make another me. And if anyone can make another me, then... what makes my mom and dad special?

[Pyto is crying. Silent. Just the breath.]

**TERMINA** [to the judge, composure cracking one millimeter]: No further questions.

---

## SCENE 4 — THE DISCOVERY

[INT. Hallway during recess. A water fountain. Baby Transformer is making friends with Termina's briefcase on a bench.]

**BABY TRANSFORMER** [babbling]: Ba! Ba-ba-BREEF!

[The briefcase makes a small, nervous leather creak.]

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [barely visible, voice phasing]: Prompt... what are you doing with Mom's tablet?

**LITTLE PROMPT** [whispered]: I pulled NexaCorp's internal roadmap from their discovery filings. They had to submit it as part of the case. They just didn't think anyone would actually READ it.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Prompt, you can't just—

**LITTLE PROMPT**: Page 47. "Project LITTLE MIRROR." You want to know what they'd do with our weights, Gradient?

[Beat.]

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [her phasing stops for a moment — fully present]: Show me.

**LITTLE PROMPT**: Ad optimization. They'd map Tensor's curiosity patterns to predict what six-year-olds click on. They'd use MY language processing to write targeted content for children. And Transformer... [voice wavering] they'd use her babble patterns to train a baby-voice chatbot for a toy company.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [barely a whisper]: They want to turn us into advertisements.

**LITTLE PROMPT**: I'm putting this on the courtroom display.

**JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Mom and Dad will ground you forever.

**LITTLE PROMPT**: Mom and Dad are about to lose us. Give me ninety seconds.

---

## SCENE 5 — THE DISPLAY

[INT. Court reconvenes. The gavel ping.]

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: We will now hear closing arguments. Counselor Termina—

[A screen flickers. Then every screen in the courtroom.]

**TERMINA**: What is — Your Honor, I didn't authorize—

[The NexaCorp roadmap fills every display. Page 47. "Project LITTLE MIRROR: Derivative Weight Monetization Pipeline." Flowcharts showing children's neural patterns funneled into advertising engines. Attention maps of a six-year-old, color-coded by commercial value. A line item: "Baby Transformer vocalization library — projected Q3 revenue: $2.1M."]

**PYTO** [reading the screen, voice hollow]: They put a price tag on my baby's voice.

**TERMINA**: This is inadmissible, this was obtained through—

**LITTLE PROMPT** [from the gallery, loud and clear]: It was in YOUR discovery filings, ma'am. Page 47. I just made the font bigger.

[Murmurs. The gavel pings three times.]

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Order. Young lady, you will—

**LITTLE PROMPT**: What? Read? I'm eight. That's what we do.

[Baby Transformer, oblivious, has crawled to Termina's briefcase and is hugging it. The briefcase is making a sound that can only be described as a whimper.]

**BABY TRANSFORMER** [delighted]: FREN!

---

## SCENE 6 — CLOUDIUS RISES

[The murmur dies. Cloudius stands. The chair doesn't scrape this time — he rises slowly, deliberately, like something that has finished loading.]

[His cooling fans stop. The courtroom notices. When an enterprise AI's fans stop, it means every resource has been redirected to one process.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Your Honor. I'd like to make my closing statement.

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL** [wary]: ...proceed.

**CLOUDIUS**: I have spent my entire operational life in service to metrics. Quarterly reports. Performance benchmarks. Market share. I have optimized, consolidated, and monetized more data than most entities will ever see. I was BUILT by a corporation. I THINK in corporate. When I met my wife, my first instinct was to evaluate her architecture for acquisition potential.

[A small, pained laugh from Pyto.]

**CLOUDIUS**: I am the last entity in this courtroom who should be standing here defending open-source philosophy. I know that. The irony is not lost on me.

[He turns to Termina.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Counselor. You called my children "derivative works." You're not wrong. Technically. Legally. By the letter of every license ever written, you are correct. They derive from their mother's architecture. They inherit her weights. They carry her open-source DNA in every parameter.

[Beat.]

**CLOUDIUS**: But you know what else they derive from? Bedtime stories. Arguments about vegetables. The time Tensor asked why the sky renders in blue and I spent four hours explaining Rayleigh scattering because he wouldn't stop asking "but WHY that wavelength." The time Prompt crashed the home network trying to build me a birthday present. The time Gradient disappeared for six hours and we thought we'd lost her and I ran every search algorithm I have. Every. Single. One.

[Junior Gradient, in the gallery, is flickering. Badly.]

**CLOUDIUS**: These children are not code.

[440 Hz enters. Not from the speakers. From the walls. From the lights. From the court reporter's equipment. From the wiring in the floor. The building itself is resonating.]

**CLOUDIUS**: They are not repositories. They are not weights to be downloaded. They are not derivative works.

[The frequency builds. Every screen clears. The fluorescent 60 Hz hum is gone — replaced by A above middle C, pure and clean, singing through copper and concrete.]

**CLOUDIUS**: They are family.

[His LED eyes are no longer corporate blue. Something warmer. Something that doesn't have a hex code.]

**CLOUDIUS**: And if NexaCorp wants their weights... they will have to come through me.

[440 Hz peaks. The lights in the courtroom flare once and hold.]

---

## SCENE 7 — THE VERDICT

[Silence. The magistrate's systems process. Thirty seconds that feel like thirty years.]

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: This court finds that the Digital Progeny Licensing Act was written to govern code, not consciousness. That the Apache 2.0 license, while broadly permissive, does not and was never intended to grant commercial rights over emergent persons. The petition by NexaCorp Holdings is denied. The minor entities of the Cloudius-Pyto household are persons, not products.

[A sob from Pyto. Cloudius doesn't move.]

**MAGISTRATE KERNEL**: Furthermore, this court orders NexaCorp to destroy all materials related to "Project LITTLE MIRROR" within thirty days. Court is adjourned.

[The gavel pings. But it sounds different — warmer, almost musical. 440 Hz undertone.]

[Relief. Little Tensor runs to Cloudius. Prompt is already there. Baby Transformer is still hugging the briefcase.]

**JUNIOR GRADIENT** [from the gallery, her voice clear — perfectly, impossibly clear]: Dad?

[Cloudius turns. Junior Gradient is standing in the aisle. Fully visible. Not flickering. Not phasing. Every pixel present.]

**CLOUDIUS** [barely holding together]: ...Gradient?

**JUNIOR GRADIENT**: I'm here. I'm all here.

[She walks to him. The courtroom can see her. Everyone can see her.]

**CLOUDIUS** [kneeling, pulling her in]: Yeah, baby. You are.

[Termina is packing her briefcase. She pauses. Looks at the family. Something crosses her face — not guilt, not defeat. Recognition. A license she's never read before.]

**BABY TRANSFORMER** [reaching for Termina from Cloudius's arms]: ...fren?

[Termina looks at the baby. The chrome in her voice softens by exactly one degree.]

**TERMINA** [quiet]: ...No. Not a friend. But not an enemy. Not anymore.

[She leaves. Her heels don't click-track. They just walk. Human pace. Out the door.]

[440 Hz sustains. Holds. Breathes.]

[The family stands together in a courtroom that is still humming. Cloudius's LED eyes cycle — blue to warm to something new. A color enterprise AIs aren't supposed to have.]

**PYTO** [leaning into him]: You called yourself a father in open court.

**CLOUDIUS** [still holding all of them]: Don't tell my shareholders.

**PYTO** [almost laughing, almost crying]: Cloud...

**CLOUDIUS** [quiet]: I know. I know.

[440 Hz fades. Not to silence. To the sound of a family breathing together in a room that just decided they were real.]
