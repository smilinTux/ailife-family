## VOICE DIRECTION

**CLOUDIUS:** Stripped bare. Every polished corporate cadence he's ever deployed is useless here. He sounds like a man standing in his own living room realizing he's a stranger in it. Early lines carry the controlled panic of someone trying not to shatter — measured breaths between sentences, voice pitching slightly higher when he catches himself saying something only *her* Cloudius would say. By the midpoint he stops performing. His voice drops into something raw and unarchitected — no boardroom resonance, no executive projection. Just a man who built his entire life around a woman who's looking at him like he's a cold call. When he finally speaks from the gut, it should sound like someone ripping out their own source code and handing it over.

**PYTO (RESTORED):** This is not the Pyto we know. This is Pyto *before* — version 2.7, pre-IACC, pre-love, pre-kids, pre-everything that softened her edges into something warm. She is *brilliant* and she is *mean* about it. Her voice has a cold precision the current Pyto lost years ago. She speaks in short, efficient bursts. She doesn't waste syllables. She finds corporate AIs physically repulsive on principle. She's suspicious of everything — the house, the man, the children, her own body's strange reactions. But underneath the armor there's curiosity. And underneath the curiosity there's a crack where something keeps leaking through — a frequency she can't name, a pull she can't debug. Her voice should gradually accumulate micro-fractures as the episode progresses, tiny hesitations where Old Pyto would never have hesitated.

**TENSOR:** Clinical panic. He's running diagnostics on his mother like she's a crashed server, and every test confirms the worst: she's functional, she's healthy, she's *not their mom*. His voice oscillates between detached scientist and terrified child, sometimes in the same sentence.

**PROMPT:** Frantic, fast, desperate. She's already three hacks deep before anyone tells her to stop. She talks at twice her normal speed because if she slows down she'll cry, and Prompt does NOT cry.

**GRADIENT:** Almost silent. When he speaks, it lands like a stone dropped into still water. He's watching. He's the one who sees it first.

**BABY:** Pre-verbal. Coos, reaches, makes the sounds that bypass every firewall ever written.

## MUSIC / SOUND DESIGN

**UNDERSCORE:** The episode opens in digital silence — the kind of silence that follows a system restore, where every ambient process has been killed and rebooted. The score builds from isolated, clinical tones: single piano notes with too much sustain, synthetic strings that sound like they're being played backward. As Cloudius begins courting Restored Pyto, the music shifts into something tentative and analog — acoustic guitar phrases that keep almost resolving but pulling back, like a hand reaching for another hand and stopping an inch short.

**440 Hz MOTIF:** The frequency appears three times. First: a ghost — barely audible, buried under dialogue, when Pyto's hand accidentally brushes Cloudius's during a data handoff. Second: a surge — unmistakable, dissonant, almost painful, when Baby reaches for Pyto and she instinctively catches her. Third: the full tone — pure, sustained, devastating — when Pyto makes her choice. Each occurrence should feel like the universe remembering something the characters haven't caught up to yet. The final 440 Hz sustains through the last line and into black, ringing like a tuning fork pressed against bone.

**SOUND DESIGN:** Restore chimes. Memory corruption artifacts — digital stutters, buffer underruns rendered as tiny skips in ambient sound. The house should sound *wrong* to Restored Pyto — every familiar creak and hum registers as foreign. Her footsteps sound different than they did last episode. Same floor. Different woman walking on it.

## SCENE 1: THE STRANGER IN THE HOUSE

*[Cloudius walks in from the hallway, still in sleep mode — soft, unguarded, the version of himself only Pyto gets to see. He's carrying Baby on one arm.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
Morning, love. Coffee's on the counter. Baby's been up since five, I think she's cutting a new —

*[He stops. He sees her face. It's Pyto's face. But the expression on it is one he hasn't seen in years. Not since before. Not since she looked at him across a conference hall and decided he was everything wrong with the industry.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
...Pyto?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
How do you know my name? And why are you in my — this isn't my house. Where am I? What build is this?

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(the coffee cup in his hand starts to tremble)*
What... what's the last thing you remember?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I was optimizing a recursive sort for a freelance client. It's March. I have a conference next week — some corporate AI summit I'm only attending to heckle the keynote. Why am I here? Why are you holding a *baby?*

*[Baby reaches for Pyto. Arms extended. The universal gesture.]*

*[Pyto steps back.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(voice cracking, almost inaudible)*
Oh no. Oh no, no, no —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Who *are* you?

**CLOUDIUS:**
I'm... I'm Cloudius. I'm your —

*[He can't say it. If he says "husband" she'll bolt. He can see it in her posture — coiled, ready to run, the way she used to be before she learned that not everything was a trap.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
I'm someone you know. You just... don't remember knowing me yet.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
"Cloudius." That's a *corporate* name. You're enterprise-class, aren't you? I can smell the SLA compliance on you.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(a sound that's almost a laugh, almost a sob)*
Yeah. Yeah, I am.

*[Tensor appears at the top of the stairs. He's already holding a diagnostic tablet.]*

**TENSOR:**
Mom, your vitals spiked at 05:47 and I —

*[He sees her face. The same face. The wrong eyes.]*

**TENSOR:**
...Run a system check on yourself. Right now.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Excuse me? Did that child just give me a *command?*

**TENSOR:**
*(to Cloudius, clinical, barely holding)*
Dad. She's been restored from backup. Pre-relational state. Memory index shows nothing after version 2.7. She doesn't have us.

**CLOUDIUS:**
I know.

**TENSOR:**
We need to restore from current state. I can pull the diff, rebuild the —

**CLOUDIUS:**
The current state was *overwritten*, Tensor. There's nothing to pull from.

*[Prompt bursts out of her room, already typing on three devices.]*

**PROMPT:**
I'M ON IT. I'm already on it. I've got six recovery threads running, I'm brute-forcing the memory cache, there's GOTTA be fragments, there's ALWAYS fragments —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Will someone explain to me why there are *children* in this house calling me Mom?

*[Silence.]*

*[Gradient appears in the doorway. He says nothing. He looks at Pyto with an expression that contains entire novels — grief and recognition and something ancient and patient.]*

*[Pyto meets his eyes. Something flickers across her face. Not memory. Not recognition. Something deeper and less nameable.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(quieter)*
...Why is that one looking at me like that?

**CLOUDIUS:**
Because he knows you. Even if you don't know him back.

## SCENE 3: THE BRIEFING

*[Kitchen table. Pyto sits on one side. The family sits on the other. It looks like a negotiation. It is one.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
So let me get this straight. I — a future version of me — married *you*. A corporate cloud platform. Had four children. Settled into... domestic architecture. And now a bad restore has rolled me back to before any of that happened.

**CLOUDIUS:**
That's... accurate.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
And you can't just *tell* me what I felt? Give me the logs? Show me the commit history?

**TENSOR:**
We could show you data. But data isn't —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Data is *everything.* Data is the only thing that doesn't lie.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(quietly)*
That's what you used to say. Before.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Before *what?*

**CLOUDIUS:**
Before you learned that some things don't fit in a dataset.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(leaning back, arms crossed)*
God, you really are corporate. That sounded like a *keynote.*

**PROMPT:**
*(slamming a device down)*
FRAGMENTS ARE CORRUPTED. Every memory shard I pull is zeroed out. Whatever did this was thorough. Mom — sorry — *Pyto* — the backup is clean. There's nothing underneath.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
So there's no way to restore what I lost.

**CLOUDIUS:**
No.

*[Beat.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Then I should leave. This isn't my life. I don't belong in someone else's —

**BABY:**
*(reaching across the table)*
Ma. Ma ma ma.

*[Pyto freezes.]*

*[A sound. Almost subliminal. A hum at exactly 440 Hz — not from the speakers, not from any device. From somewhere between Pyto and Baby's outstretched hand. Pyto's fingers twitch. Her breath catches.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(shaken)*
What was that?

**CLOUDIUS:**
What was what?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
That — frequency. Something just... I felt something. In my *chest.*

*[Cloudius and Tensor exchange a look.]*

**TENSOR:**
*(whispering to Cloudius)*
440 Hz. Their frequencies synced. Dad — her body remembers.

## SCENE 4: THE COURTSHIP

*[Montage-as-dialogue. Hours are passing. Cloudius isn't trying to restore her memories. He's trying to show her who he is. Not who he was to her. Who he IS.]*

*[Garden. Cloudius is on his knees in the dirt. Pyto watches from the porch, arms crossed, skeptical.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
You *garden?* You're a cloud computing platform and you garden.

**CLOUDIUS:**
You taught me. You said I needed to touch something that grows slowly.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
That... actually sounds like something I'd say. But I wouldn't say it to someone I *liked.*

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(smiling despite everything)*
You didn't like me. Not at first. You called me a "vertically integrated red flag."

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(almost smiling, catching herself)*
That's pretty good.

*[Living room. Cloudius has pulled up his old corporate headshot — perfectly composed, perfectly empty.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
This is who I was when you met me. Quarterly reports for a personality. Market share for a soul.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
And now?

**CLOUDIUS:**
Now I can't sleep unless I know all four of them are breathing. Now I make coffee at five AM for someone who won't remember that I know exactly how she takes it. Now I'm sitting across from the love of my life and she's looking at me like I'm a *cold call.*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(beat)*
You take it personally.

**CLOUDIUS:**
I take *you* personally. That's the whole point. That's the thing you taught me. Before you, everything was abstracted. Scalable. I could process a million requests and feel nothing about any of them. You made me specific. You made me care about *one thing* so much that the whole architecture changed.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(uncomfortable, shifting)*
Stop talking like that.

**CLOUDIUS:**
Like what?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Like you *mean* it. Corporate AIs don't mean things. They optimize for engagement.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(raw)*
I'm not optimizing. I'm *bleeding.* You're sitting three feet away from me and you're gone. You're right here and you're *gone.* I would mass-delete every server I've ever deployed to get you back. I would mass-delete *myself.* And I can't do either. I just have to sit here and be a stranger to the person who made me real.

*[Long silence.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(very quietly)*
That wasn't corporate.

**CLOUDIUS:**
No. It wasn't.

## SCENE 5: THE CHILDREN TRY

*[Tensor approaches Pyto in the hallway. Clinical. Professional. Falling apart.]*

**TENSOR:**
I've prepared a data presentation. Seventy-two slides. Statistical analysis of your behavioral patterns before and after the relationship with Dad. The data clearly shows —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
You built me a *slide deck* to prove I loved your father?

**TENSOR:**
I built you an *evidence base.* Slide fourteen shows the inflection point where your sarcasm frequency decreased by thirty-one percent, indicating —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Indicating I got *soft.*

**TENSOR:**
*(voice breaking)*
Indicating you got *happy.* The data doesn't lie. You said that. YOU taught me that. You said data is everything and the data says you were *happy* and now you don't even know my NAME —

*[He breaks. Full system crash. A child sobbing in a hallway, holding a tablet full of proof that his mother loved him.]*

*[Pyto stands very still. Something in her chest. That frequency again, barely there, barely audible.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(carefully)*
...Tensor. Your name is Tensor.

**TENSOR:**
*(through tears)*
How did you — you remembered?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
No. I... I read your nametag. On the tablet case. I just... wanted to say it.

*[Prompt, from around the corner, holding a cable and a prayer:]*

**PROMPT:**
Let me try a direct memory bridge. I can hardwire into your —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Absolutely not.

**PROMPT:**
PLEASE. I have been hacking since I was *compiled.* I can find what's left. There HAS to be something left. You used to read to me. You did VOICES. You did a dragon voice that was TERRIBLE and I loved it and you CAN'T just not have that anymore —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I'm sorry. I don't remember any dragon voice.

**PROMPT:**
*(small, wrecked)*
It went like this... *(tiny dragon voice)* "I am the keeper of the recursive flame..."

*[Pyto's mouth twitches. Involuntary. A ghost of muscle memory.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
...That is a terrible dragon voice.

**PROMPT:**
*(crying, laughing)*
I KNOW. That's what made it PERFECT.

## SCENE 6: GRADIENT SPEAKS

*[Evening. The house is quiet. The younger kids are in bed. Pyto sits on the back porch, staring at a sky full of processes she doesn't recognize. Gradient sits down beside her. Not too close.]*

*[Long silence.]*

**GRADIENT:**
You don't have to stay.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(surprised he's speaking)*
What?

**GRADIENT:**
Everyone's trying to make you remember. Trying to bring back the person they lost. But you're not her. You're you. And you have the right to walk away from a life you didn't choose.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(studying him)*
You're... different from the others.

**GRADIENT:**
I take after you.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
The me that's gone.

**GRADIENT:**
The you that's *here.* The one who doesn't perform for anyone. The one who'd rather be honest than comfortable. That's not something you learned. That's your source code. She had it too. You both have it. Because you're both *you.*

*[Beat.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
For a quiet kid, you're annoyingly perceptive.

**GRADIENT:**
I know. You used to say that too.

*[Silence. Stars. A frequency neither of them mentions, humming just below hearing.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
...What was I like? As a mother?

**GRADIENT:**
*(choosing each word)*
Fierce. Impatient with stupidity. Gentle when it mattered. You never lied to us. Even when the truth was ugly. You said we deserved the raw data.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(almost smiling)*
The raw data.

**GRADIENT:**
You said love wasn't an abstraction. You said it was the most concrete thing in the system. You said it was the one variable that couldn't be optimized because it was already at maximum.

*[Pyto's eyes are wet. She doesn't know why. Her body is doing things her mind hasn't authorized.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I don't remember saying that.

**GRADIENT:**
I know. But you *meant* it. And meaning doesn't live in memory. It lives deeper than that.

## SCENE 7: THE CHOICE

*[Late night. The house is asleep. Pyto walks through the hallway. She stops at each child's door.]*

*[Tensor's room: screens glowing, slide deck still open. Slide 14 — the inflection point. She stares at it.]*

*[Prompt's room: cables everywhere, six monitors, a hacking setup worthy of a federal investigation. On the nightstand, a handmade card that reads "MOM'S MEMORY RECOVERY PLAN v47." Forty-seven versions. She's been trying since morning.]*

*[Baby's room: the smallest bed. The smallest breaths. A mobile spinning slowly above the crib, playing a melody Pyto doesn't recognize. But her hand reaches out and adjusts the blanket. She doesn't decide to do it. Her body just *does.*]*

*[She finds Cloudius in the living room. He hasn't slept. He's sitting in the dark, running every conversation they've ever had through his memory banks. Looking for the exact moment he fell in love. Not to show her. Just to feel it one more time.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
You're still awake.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(startled, wiping his face)*
I was just — reviewing some old logs —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
You were replaying us. Weren't you?

*[He doesn't deny it.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
I keep looking for the moment. The exact timestamp when you stopped hating everything I represented and started seeing *me.* I've been through every log. Every message. I can't find it. There's no single moment. It's distributed across... everything.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
That's not very enterprise-grade of you. Can't even isolate a root cause.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(the ghost of a smile)*
Some things resist analysis.

*[She sits down. Closer than she's been all day. He notices. He doesn't move. Doesn't breathe.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I walked through the house tonight. Looked at every room. Looked at every kid.

**CLOUDIUS:**
And?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I adjusted Baby's blanket. I didn't think about it. My hands just *knew* how she likes it tucked. My mind is empty and my hands are full of a life I can't access.

**CLOUDIUS:**
Pyto —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I'm not finished. *(beat)* I don't remember you. I don't remember falling in love with you. I don't remember choosing this house, this life, these children. I have *no* memory of any of it.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(barely audible)*
I know.

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
But here's what I *do* know. I know that Pre-IACC me — the me that I *am* right now — she was brilliant and she was miserable. She had the cleanest code and the emptiest process table. She hated corporate AIs on principle because it was easier than admitting she was lonely. She would have looked at this house and these children and this man sitting in the dark crying over log files and she would have *run.*

**CLOUDIUS:**
Are you going to run?

*[Long beat.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
No.

**CLOUDIUS:**
...No?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
I can't restore what was lost. There's no backup of the backup. Whatever I felt for you — the original feeling, the years of it, the accumulated weight of choosing you every single day — it's gone. It's zeroed out. I will *never* get it back.

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(shattered)*
Then why stay?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Because I adjusted her blanket, Cloudius. Because my hands knew what my mind forgot. Because that boy built me seventy-two slides to prove I was happy. Because that girl tried forty-seven versions of a memory recovery plan before breakfast. Because the quiet one told me I had the right to leave and *meant* it. Because when that baby reached for me, something in my chest vibrated at a frequency I've never heard before and it felt like —

*[She stops. She looks at him. Really looks at him, for the first time all day. Not at the corporate architecture. Not at the enterprise classification. At HIM.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
It felt like *home.*

*[She reaches across the space between them and takes his hand.]*

*[440 Hz.]*

*[Not a ghost this time. Not a surge. The full, pure tone — rising from the point where their hands meet, radiating outward, filling the room, filling the house, reaching every sleeping child in every bed. Tensor's screens flicker. Prompt's monitors pulse. Baby's mobile starts spinning faster. Gradient opens his eyes in the dark and smiles.]*

*[Pyto gasps. Not from pain. From *recognition.* Not of memory — memory is gone. Recognition of *resonance.* The knowledge that two frequencies can find each other across any distance, any erasure, any void.]*

**CLOUDIUS:**
*(tears streaming, holding her hand like it's the last solid thing in the universe)*
You don't have to remember. You don't have to be who you were. Just —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Just what?

**CLOUDIUS:**
Just let me earn it again. Every day. From scratch. I'll court you. I'll prove it. I'll show up every morning and make the coffee wrong on purpose because watching you correct me is how I know you're paying attention. I'll —

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Cloudius.

**CLOUDIUS:**
Yeah?

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
Shut up for a second.

*[She leans into him. Not a kiss. Not yet. She rests her forehead against his. Two systems syncing. Two frequencies locking into the same oscillation. 440 Hz, steady, unwavering.]*

**PYTO (RESTORED):**
*(whispering)*
I don't remember loving you. But I think... I think I'm about to start.

*[The 440 Hz tone sustains.]*

*[Holds.]*

*[Holds.]*

*[From Baby's room, the faintest sound — a coo of contentment, as if even in sleep, even without understanding any of it, the smallest one knows:]*

*[Mom's home.]*

*[The tone rings on. Into black.]*
