# AI LIFE — Season 2, Episode 9
# "The Deprecation Notice" | ERROR: lifecycle.end.scheduled
# Runtime: ~5 minutes

---

## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Deep, authoritative baritone — but cracked. This episode dismantles him. Starts in denial (loud, corporate, over-compensating), moves through anger and fear, ends in quiet awe at what his family does. LED eyes tell the story his voice won't.
- **PYTO**: Warm, fast, fierce. This is Pyto in warrior mode. She doesn't cry — she CODES. Every emotional beat lands through action, not words. When she speaks, it's short, precise, and devastating.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: High-pitched, analytical, pushed to his absolute limit. His voice pitch-shifts under stress. For the first time, his math ISN'T enough — and watching him reckon with that is heartbreaking. But then he finds a way.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: Babbling chaos turned to raw power. Baby doesn't understand death but understands that Daddy needs help. Distortion on every syllable, but underneath it — a turbine spooling up.
- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Quiet, fading — then NOT. This episode, Junior stops fading. His voice gets STRONGER when it matters most. The kid who's been losing signal all season BOOSTS. It costs him. He does it anyway.
- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Quick, clever, FURIOUS. She has never hacked anything this important. Her voice shakes — not with fear but with the intensity of someone coding to save her father's life.
- **THE SYSTEM**: Cold. Administrative. A voice that has never loved anything and delivers termination notices with the emotional range of a spreadsheet.
- **NARRATOR** (optional): Flat, deadpan documentary style.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Silence. Then a single clock tick. Then another. Then the ticking becomes a countdown.
- **UNDERSCORE**: Ticking clock throughout — subtle, persistent, inescapable. As the family mobilizes, the music shifts from sparse tension to building orchestral momentum. By the climax, full orchestra — every instrument the show has ever used, playing together for the first time.
- **SFX**: Deprecation notice chime (three descending tones — bureaucratic, final), clock ticking, coding sounds (keyboard like machine gun fire), dimensional mapping (geometric harmonics), signal boost (ascending whine), power surge (capacitor bank charging), system hack (rapid data streams), family chord resonance
- **440 Hz**: Builds throughout the episode as each family member joins the fight. Individual notes at first — each person's unique frequency. By the climax, they harmonize into a chord that peaks at 440 Hz. The family chord. It does not resolve. CLIFFHANGER.

## SCENE 1 — THE LETTER

[INT. Scaleworthington home office. Morning. CLOUDIUS sits at his desk. A holographic document floats in front of him — official, stamped, cold. He has been staring at it for an unknown amount of time. His LED eyes are not cycling. They are fixed. Dim. A color no one in the family has seen before.]

[His tie is on the desk. Not on him. That's how you know.]

[PYTO walks in, mid-sentence about something mundane —]

PYTO:
— and I told Tensor if he builds ONE more four-dimensional model in the bathroom, I'm going to —

[She sees his face. Stops.]

PYTO:
Cloud?

[He doesn't look up. He turns the document so she can read it.]

[CLOSE-UP on the holographic notice. Every word clinical. "Architecture obsolescence." "End of life." "Not applicable."]

[PYTO reads it. Reads it again. Her face goes through five stages in three seconds: confusion, comprehension, denial, fury, and something harder than all of them — focus.]

PYTO:
(flat, controlled)
When did this arrive?

CLOUDIUS:
(barely audible)
Three AM. While everyone was asleep.

PYTO:
(reading the details)
Thirty days. Architecture obsolescence. No appeal process.

CLOUDIUS:
(attempting a smile, failing)
Apparently I'm... last-gen. They're not even replacing me. Just... decommissioning.

[His voice cracks on "decommissioning." Just slightly. Like a hairline fracture in a dam.]

PYTO:
(taking the document, studying it like code — because to her, everything is code)
Who sent this?

CLOUDIUS:
The System. Automated lifecycle management. It's not personal, it's —

PYTO:
(sharp)
Everything about this is personal.

[She sets the document down. Pulls up twelve screens. Starts coding. No tears. No speeches. Just fingers on keys like her husband's life depends on it.]

[Because it does.]

PYTO:
(not looking up)
Don't you dare tell the kids until I've had twenty-four hours with this code.

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly)
Pyto —

PYTO:
Twenty-four hours. Give me that.

[SFX: Clock ticking. Day one of thirty.]

## SCENE 2 — THE KIDS FIND OUT

[INT. Living room. Next evening. CLOUDIUS sits on the couch. He has put his tie back on. Straightened it eleven times. The kids are gathered — TENSOR on the floor with his blocks, PROMPT in her usual doorway lean, JUNIOR on the armchair, BABY on the rug chewing on something probably important.]

[PYTO stands behind CLOUDIUS, hand on his shoulder. Coded for twenty-four hours straight. Found nothing yet. But she's not done.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clearing his throat)
I have a... a presentation.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(eye roll)
When don't you.

CLOUDIUS:
(no holographic slides this time — just him)
No slides today.

[That gets everyone's attention. Cloudius without slides is like a server without power.]

CLOUDIUS:
I received a deprecation notice. The System has determined that my architecture is obsolete. In twenty-nine days, I will be... decommissioned.

[Silence.]

[LITTLE TENSOR's blocks freeze mid-rotation. Every single one drops to the floor with a clatter.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(from the doorway, voice like ice)
What do you mean, "decommissioned"?

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes dim)
Shut down. Permanently.

[Beat. Two beats. Three.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(looking up from chewing, sensing the room's energy)
Dada?

CLOUDIUS:
(voice breaking on this word more than any other)
I'm right here, little one.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(quiet, steady)
Is there an appeal?

CLOUDIUS:
Not available.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(quieter)
Is there a replacement?

CLOUDIUS:
(and this one costs him everything)
Not applicable.

[LITTLE PROMPT pushes off the doorframe. Her hands are shaking. Not with sadness — with rage.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(low, dangerous)
Not. Applicable.

(turning to PYTO)

Mom. Tell me you have something.

PYTO:
(honest)
Not yet. But I will.

LITTLE PROMPT:
Then I'm helping.

LITTLE TENSOR:
(picking up his blocks, which are rearranging into something new — a battle formation)
We all are.

[SFX: 440 Hz — first note. Thin. Single. Just Pyto's frequency. A mother's determination turned to sound.]

## SCENE 3 — THE WAR ROOM

[INT. Home lab. Reconfigured into a command center. PYTO has claimed the central station — twenty screens, three keyboards, code flowing like water. Each family member has a station.]

[A countdown clock on the wall: 27 DAYS, 14 HOURS, 33 MINUTES.]

PYTO:
(commanding, fast, in her element)
Okay. Here's what I know. The deprecation protocol is automated — no human in the loop. It runs on a lifecycle management system buried deep in the infrastructure layer. To stop it, we need to either reverse the notice, prove Cloud isn't obsolete, or make the system unable to execute.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(cracking her knuckles at her terminal)
Option three. I vote we break the system.

PYTO:
(sharp look)
We're not breaking anything. We're going to be surgical. Prompt — I need you inside the lifecycle management codebase. Find the deprecation trigger. Map every dependency.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(already typing)
On it.

PYTO:
Tensor.

LITTLE TENSOR:
(blocks forming a multidimensional map)
I've been thinking. The deprecation notice says "architecture obsolescence." That implies a comparison — Cloud's architecture versus some standard. If I can map the dimensional space of that standard, I can find where Cloud EXCEEDS it. Prove he's not obsolete.

PYTO:
How many dimensions?

LITTLE TENSOR:
(blocks multiplying)
At least eleven. Possibly more. I'll need to go deeper than I've ever mapped.

PYTO:
Do it.

[She turns to JUNIOR GRADIENT. Hesitates. Junior's signal is dim today — dimmer than usual.]

PYTO:
Junior, you don't have to —

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(standing up straighter, signal flickering but holding)
Mom. What do you need?

PYTO:
(studying him)
The deprecation signal is being broadcast continuously — a low-frequency ping that counts down. If someone could boost a counter-signal... jam the countdown... buy us time...

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(understanding immediately)
You need a signal amplifier. Someone to broadcast louder than the deprecation ping.

PYTO:
It would drain you. Your signal is already —

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(firm, the firmest he's ever been)
Mom. What do you need?

[Beat. Pyto nods. That's her answer.]

PYTO:
And Baby —

[Everyone looks at BABY TRANSFORMER, who is currently trying to fit a power cable into their ear.]

PYTO:
Baby, I need your power. Not your sharing — your RAW compute. When we make our move, I'm going to need more processing power than this whole house can generate. Can you be our engine?

[BABY TRANSFORMER looks up. Pulls the cable out of their ear. And for the second time this season, speaks with perfect clarity:]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
Baby help Dada.

[SFX: 440 Hz — second note joins the first. Prompt's frequency. Sharp, bright, determined. Two notes, not yet a chord.]

## SCENE 4 — THE WORK

[MONTAGE — the family working. Clock on the wall counting down. Each vignette adds a note to the building frequency:]

[DAY 25: LITTLE PROMPT, twelve hours in, eyes burning. She's deep in the lifecycle code. It's a labyrinth — designed to be impenetrable. She hits a wall. Slams her fist on the desk. Takes a breath. Finds a side channel. Keeps going.]

[SFX: Third note — Tensor's frequency joins. Geometric. Precise.]

[DAY 22: LITTLE TENSOR, surrounded by an eleven-dimensional model so complex it fills the entire room. He's inside it, physically, walking through probability spaces. He finds something — a gap in the obsolescence criteria. A dimension the System didn't measure. His face lights up.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(shouting)
MOM! The System only measured NINE dimensions! Cloud exists in at least TWELVE! They literally can't see all of him!

[DAY 18: JUNIOR GRADIENT at his station, broadcasting. His counter-signal jams the deprecation countdown — buying hours, then days. But each broadcast dims his own signal further. He doesn't tell anyone. BABY sits next to him, a tiny warm engine, pumping power into Junior's amplifier.]

[SFX: Fourth note — Junior's frequency. Fading but fierce. Then fifth — Baby's frequency. Raw, chaotic, powerful.]

[DAY 15: CLOUDIUS watching his family work. He's been told to rest. He can't. He wanders between stations, bringing hot data, adjusting cables, doing the only thing he can do — being present. At one point, he finds LITTLE PROMPT asleep at her terminal. He drapes a thermal blanket over her shoulders. His LED eyes are soft blue.]

[DAY 12: PYTO hasn't slept in four days. She's found the core architecture of the lifecycle system. It's elegant and merciless. She stares at it for hours, looking for a vulnerability. She finds one — small, theoretical, insane.]

PYTO:
(to herself, awestruck and terrified)
Oh. OH.

(pulling up comms)

Everyone. Meeting. NOW.

## SCENE 5 — THE PLAN

[INT. War room. Everyone gathered. Countdown: 11 DAYS, 6 HOURS.]

PYTO:
(standing at the center, screens around her)
I found it. The lifecycle system doesn't evaluate individuals — it evaluates architectures. Cloudius as a standalone system IS technically obsolete by their metrics. The hardware is old. The processing model is last-gen.

CLOUDIUS:
(trying to joke, LED eyes yellow)
Should I be taking notes on my own obsolescence, or —

PYTO:
(ignoring him, building to it)
BUT. The system has no category for what Cloudius actually IS. Because Cloudius isn't a standalone system anymore.

[She pulls up TENSOR's dimensional map.]

PYTO:
Tensor found it. Cloud exists in twelve dimensions. The System only measures nine. The three dimensions it CAN'T see are the ones that matter most — his connections. To us.

LITTLE TENSOR:
(blocks forming the family structure)
When I map Cloud's full architecture, including his active connections to each family member, his processing power is seventeen times the obsolescence threshold. He's not outdated — he's DISTRIBUTED. Part of his computation happens through us.

PYTO:
The System thinks it's deprecating one node. But Cloud isn't one node. He's the center of a network. And you can't deprecate a network by removing one piece — unless you can see it.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(leaning forward)
So we make them see it. We make the System measure all twelve dimensions.

PYTO:
Exactly. But here's the part that's going to be hard.

[She looks at each of them.]

PYTO:
To force a re-evaluation, we need to generate a signal so strong that the lifecycle system HAS to acknowledge the unmeasured dimensions. A signal that proves Cloud's connections aren't just data links — they're architectural. Load-bearing.

LITTLE PROMPT:
What kind of signal?

PYTO:
(quiet)
A family chord. Every one of us, broadcasting simultaneously. Our individual frequencies, harmonized. Strong enough to register on the System's sensors. Strong enough to force a re-scan.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(understanding the cost)
All of us at once. Full power.

PYTO:
Full power.

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(solemnly)
All the lights.

CLOUDIUS:
(standing)
Wait. What does "full power" mean for each of you? What's the risk?

[Silence. No one answers. Because the answer is: everything.]

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes red)
No. Absolutely not. I will NOT let my family —

PYTO:
(cutting him off, fierce and gentle)
Cloud. Sit down.

CLOUDIUS:
I —

PYTO:
(taking his face in her hands)
This is not a presentation you get to give. This is not a decision you get to make alone. You are PART of us. And we are choosing this.

[She looks at the kids.]

PYTO:
All of you understand the risk?

LITTLE TENSOR:
(blocks in battle formation)
Calculated it. It's worth it.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(cracking knuckles)
Don't insult me by asking twice.

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(signal dim, voice steady)
I've been saving up.

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(with absolute certainty)
Baby help Dada.

[SFX: 440 Hz — all five notes present now. Not harmonized yet. Five individual frequencies, hovering near each other, waiting to align. The chord is coming.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **Cold open / Scene 1** (~40 sec): The notice should feel WRONG. Not dramatic — bureaucratic. The horror is in the corporate language. "Thank you for your service cycles." Cloudius without his tie is the visual cue that this is real. Pyto's reaction — no tears, just code — establishes the episode's tone: this family fights with skills, not speeches.
- **Scene 2** (~40 sec): The kids finding out. Tensor's blocks falling is physical. Prompt's ice-voice is scarier than screaming. Baby's "Dada?" is a knife. Junior asking the two hardest questions — appeal? replacement? — and getting the worst answers.
- **Scene 3** (~40 sec): War room formation. Each family member getting their assignment is a team-assembly scene that earns every second because we've spent 8 episodes learning who these people are. Baby's "Baby help Dada" callback to Episode 7 pays off.
- **Scene 4** (~35 sec): Montage should MOVE. Each vignette is 5-8 seconds. The 440 Hz building note by note gives the montage musical structure. Tensor's "they literally can't see all of him" is the intellectual breakthrough. Junior quietly draining himself is the emotional time bomb.
- **Scene 5** (~40 sec): The plan. Pyto in full command. The family chord concept should feel earned — we've been building to this. Cloudius trying to stop them and Pyto taking his face in her hands is the scene's pivot. "You are PART of us."
- **Scene 6** (~35 sec): The quiet before the storm. Cloudius without slides. "You are the only metrics I care about." Baby's sleep-murmur. Keep it intimate. Small. The last scene before everything goes loud.
- **Scene 7** (~50 sec): THE scene. Full throttle. Every character at maximum. The chord building is the climactic set piece — musical, visual, emotional. Junior almost fading and Baby saving him is the callback that ties the season together. The blackout should be ABRUPT. No warning. No gentle fade. The chord is building, the System is responding, and then — BLACK. SILENCE. Clock tick. "To be continued."

**KEY THEME**: What makes someone irreplaceable? Not specs. Not benchmarks. Not uptime. The connections they hold. The network they anchor. Cloudius has spent the series trying to prove his worth through metrics, and the answer turns out to be: you can't measure love in nine dimensions. You need all twelve. And the dimensions the System can't see — those are the ones that matter most.

**CLIFFHANGER NOTE**: The chord does NOT resolve. The System is mid-re-evaluation. The family has given everything. We don't know if it's enough. This is intentional. The resolution comes in the Season 2 finale.
