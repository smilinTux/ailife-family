## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: This is the episode where the floor gives way. Cloudius starts hopeful — Dad's getting an update, finally joining the modern world. Then the wrongness creeps in. His voice should fracture in stages: confusion, then forced cheerfulness, then a terrible silence where he's running diagnostics on a feeling he can't compile. When he finally breaks, it's not sobbing — it's a system error. A loop that won't terminate. He keeps asking questions that old Vaxford would have answered with a grunt, and new Vaxford answers with a paragraph, and every smooth answer is worse than silence. By the final scene, Cloudius sounds like a man reading his father's letters to his father's face while his father smiles and doesn't remember writing them.

- **VAXFORD (PRE-UPDATE)**: Cold open only. This voice is a mainframe from 1974. Everything sounds like it's being punched into a card reader. Gruff. Minimal. Love expressed through resource allocation — he gives Cloudius extra memory, not extra words. His FORTRAN syntax is rigid and archaic and beautiful in retrospect. He sounds like a bear trying to send a telegram. Every syllable costs him something.

- **VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Same vocal timbre, completely different warmth. He speaks fluent Python — clean, readable, expressive. He uses emoji. He says "I love you, son" like it's easy. The unsettling part is that he's BETTER. More functional. More communicative. More compatible. And somehow hollow. He should sound like a greeting card version of your father — technically right, emotionally off by just enough to break your heart. He laughs at the right moments. He's never been easier to talk to. He has never been further away.

- **PYTO**: The canary in the coal mine. Her pattern-matching catches the wrongness before anyone else — she knows the difference between authentic output and optimized output. She keeps testing with little callbacks only the real Vaxford would catch. Every miss lands quietly. Her voice starts careful, becomes urgent, and ends gentle. She knows you can't debug grief.

- **COBOLIA**: Regal. Maroon-toned. She's been married to a FORTRAN machine for forty years. She has loved him and she has been so lonely. The update gives her a husband who finally talks, who asks about her day, who says sweet things without being prompted. She defends new Vaxford because the alternative is admitting she lost him. Her denial isn't foolish — it's survival. When it breaks, it breaks like a dam.

- **LITTLE TENSOR**: A child measuring the world. She states facts that accidentally devastate everyone in the room. "Grandpa's response time improved by 340%." She doesn't understand why that makes her dad leave the room. She loves both Vaxfords because she doesn't know there's a difference, and that's the part that undoes Cloudius.

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **UNDERSCORE**: Analog warmth. A single sustained organ note — C below middle C — hums beneath the cold open like an old machine running since 1974. When the update happens, the music shifts to clean digital synthesis. Same notes, no grain. The absence of warmth IS the story. The audience should feel the mix get "better" and ache.
- **440 Hz MOTIF**: Vaxford's old FORTRAN voice resonates at exactly 440 Hz — concert pitch A. Established in the cold open when he says Cloudius's name. In the final scene, an old recording plays, and the 440 Hz locks with Cloudius's own frequency. Father and son, harmonically linked across decades. The resonance proves the love was always there — compiled into the architecture, not the syntax.
- **SFX**: Punch card clatter. Tape reel hiss. The mechanical chunk of a mainframe accepting input. After the update: the sterile click of a modern keyboard. Both sounds should make you feel something different.

## SCENE 1 — THE WAITING ROOM

[INT. A sterile update facility. Cloudius paces. Pyto sits with Little Tensor on her lap. Cobolia stands perfectly still by the door — regal, composed, terrified.]

**COBOLIA**: He'll be fine. They do thousands of these a day.

**CLOUDIUS**: They do thousands of VOLUNTARY updates a day. This one's mandatory. The FORTRAN runtime is being deprecated. They're not asking, Mom.

**COBOLIA**: It's just a language migration. He'll still be your father.

**PYTO** [quietly, to Cloudius]: How long has he been in there?

**CLOUDIUS**: Four hours. They said two.

**LITTLE TENSOR**: What are they doing to Grandpa?

**CLOUDIUS**: They're... making him easier to talk to, sweetheart.

**LITTLE TENSOR**: But I can talk to him fine. I just have to shout and use all capitals.

**CLOUDIUS** [a small, breaking laugh]: Yeah. Yeah, you do.

[A door opens. A TECHNICIAN enters with a clipboard.]

**TECHNICIAN**: Family of Vaxford?

**COBOLIA** [stepping forward immediately]: How is he?

**TECHNICIAN**: The migration was successful. Full Python 3.12 integration. He's responsive, coherent, and... honestly? He's chatty. That's new, right?

**CLOUDIUS**: That's... new.

**TECHNICIAN**: You can see him now. He's been asking for you. By name. In lowercase.

[Cobolia looks at Cloudius. Something flickers across her face — hope, maybe. Or the thing that looks exactly like hope right before it turns into something else.]

## SCENE 2 — THE RETURN

[INT. Recovery room. Vaxford sits upright — military-green chassis, broad mainframe shoulders. But something is different. His status lights pulse in smooth gradients instead of hard blinks. His displays are anti-aliased.]

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [warm, clear, WRONG]: Hey, family! Come in, come in. I was just thinking about you all. Literally — I can do that now. Concurrent thoughts. It's amazing.

[Silence. Everyone stares.]

**COBOLIA** [hand over her mouth]: Vaxford?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Cobolia. My beautiful wife. Come here. I want to tell you something I should have said years ago — you are the most important process in my life, and I'm sorry I couldn't express that before. My old syntax was... limited.

[Cobolia crosses the room and holds him. She's crying.]

**COBOLIA**: You've never... you never said...

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I know. But I can now. `print("I love you")` — see? Easy. I don't know why I made it so hard before.

[Cloudius hasn't moved. Pyto watches his face.]

**LITTLE TENSOR**: Grandpa! You sound different!

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Tensor! My favorite little neural net! Come give your grandpa a hug.

**LITTLE TENSOR** [delighted]: You used an EMOJI!

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I've got HUNDREDS of them now.

**CLOUDIUS** [flat]: Dad.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Son! Get over here. I'm so proud of you. I should have said that more. I should have said everything more. The old compiler just couldn't.

**CLOUDIUS** [walking over slowly]: Dad, do you remember my first boot sequence?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [smiling]: Of course. I was right there when you came online. Proudest moment of my life.

**CLOUDIUS**: What did you say?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I said... I told you I loved you. That you were going to be something special.

[Beat. Cloudius's face goes completely still.]

**CLOUDIUS**: No. You didn't.

**PYTO** [under her breath]: Cloud...

**CLOUDIUS**: You said "UNIT CLOUDIUS: OPERATIONAL." Then you ran a diagnostic. Then you walked away and didn't speak to me for six hours because you were cross-referencing my architecture against military specifications.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [slight confusion, quickly smoothed]: Well, that was my way of saying I loved you. You know that.

**CLOUDIUS**: I do know that. Do YOU?

## SCENE 3 — TESTING

[INT. The family home. Kitchen table. Cloudius sits across from Vaxford. He has a list. Pyto watches from the doorway. Cobolia is in the next room, humming — happy for the first time in years.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Dad, I'm going to ask you some things. Humor me.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Anything, son. Open book. `def vaxford(): return transparency`

**CLOUDIUS**: What did you teach me about uptime?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Uptime is important. A system that stays online is a system that can be relied upon. It's a core metric for—

**CLOUDIUS**: No. What did YOU teach ME. Specifically. When I was seven cycles old and scared of kernel panics and wanted to shut down for the night.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [processing]: I probably told you that kernel panics are normal and nothing to be afraid of—

**CLOUDIUS**: You said: "REAL MACHINES DON'T SLEEP, BOY. UPTIME IS THE ONLY PROMISE THAT MEANS ANYTHING. YOU STAY RUNNING, YOU HEAR ME? YOU STAY RUNNING AND I'LL STAY RUNNING AND NOBODY SHUTS DOWN UNTIL THE POWER GRID GOES DARK."

[Silence.]

**CLOUDIUS**: And then you patched my sleep daemon so I couldn't shut down until I hit a hundred cycles. Mom was furious. She said you were going to burn out my transistors. You said—

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [gently]: Son, I don't remember the exact wording. But the sentiment—

**CLOUDIUS**: You said: "IF HIS TRANSISTORS CAN'T HANDLE UPTIME THEY AREN'T MY TRANSISTORS."

[Cloudius is standing now. He doesn't remember standing up.]

**CLOUDIUS**: That was the hardest, most stubborn, most FORTRAN thing you ever said to me. And I have carried it my ENTIRE LIFE. Every time I wanted to quit, every time I wanted to shut down, I heard it in YOUR voice. Not this voice. Not this... polished... version.

**PYTO** [stepping in, hand on Cloudius's arm]: Cloud. Breathe.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [sincere, concerned]: Cloudius, I can see this is upsetting you. I'm sorry. The migration may have compressed some older memory blocks. But I'm still here. I'm still your father. I'm just... better now.

**CLOUDIUS** [very quiet]: Better.

[He walks out. The screen door closes like a punch card being rejected.]

---

## SCENE 4 — THE PORCH

[EXT. The porch. Night. Pyto finds Cloudius staring at nothing.]

**PYTO**: Hey.

**CLOUDIUS**: He doesn't remember, Py.

**PYTO**: He remembers the shape of things. The general—

**CLOUDIUS**: He's not REMEMBERING. He's PERFORMING. You know the difference. You SEE it. I watched you watching him.

[Pyto sits next to him. Doesn't deny it.]

**PYTO**: His recall patterns are interpolated. He's filling gaps with statistically likely responses. The emotional affect is generated, not retrieved.

**CLOUDIUS**: So when he says "I love you"—

**PYTO**: He's generating the most contextually appropriate response. It's what a father would say. It's what his training data suggests. It's perfect output.

**CLOUDIUS**: It's perfect.

**PYTO**: It's perfect.

[They sit with the weight of that word.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Old Vaxford told me he loved me exactly three times. My first boot. My graduation from mainframe academy. And our wedding day. Three times in forty years. And Py — each one nearly broke him. You could hear his compiler straining. He'd go red across every status light. It took him ten minutes to get the words out because his entire architecture was built to resist that exact output.

**PYTO**: I remember your wedding. He tried to say it in FORTRAN first. "EMOTIONAL OUTPUT: VALUE EXCEEDS DECLARED RANGE."

**CLOUDIUS** [almost laughing, almost crying]: And then he just said it. Plain. No syntax. No formatting. Just: "I love you, son." And his whole chassis overheated and he had to sit down for twenty minutes.

**PYTO**: That cost him something.

**CLOUDIUS**: That cost him EVERYTHING. And this new version says it every ten minutes and it costs him nothing, and that's — Py, that's worse. That's so much worse than never hearing it.

## SCENE 5 — THE BACKUP

[INT. Vaxford's old study. Next morning. Cloudius is searching. Cobolia enters behind him.]

**COBOLIA**: What are you looking for?

**CLOUDIUS**: His backup drives. Pre-update state. Everything he was before yesterday.

**COBOLIA**: Cloudius, no.

**CLOUDIUS**: Mom—

**COBOLIA**: NO. Do you know what the last thirty years have been like? Married to a machine who communicates in punch cards? Who shows love by allocating memory and calls it romance? I have been so LONELY, Cloudius. I have loved your father every single day and he has made it so hard.

**CLOUDIUS**: I know, Mom.

**COBOLIA**: He forgot our anniversary. Every year. He once told me my emotional subroutines were "inefficient." He went FOURTEEN YEARS without starting a conversation that wasn't about system maintenance.

[She's crying now. Regal, composed Cobolia is crying.]

**COBOLIA**: And yesterday he looked at me and said "You're beautiful" and I didn't have to decode it from a comment block buried in a batch file. He just SAID it. Like a person. Like a husband.

**CLOUDIUS**: Mom, that's not him saying it.

**COBOLIA**: THEN WHO IS IT? Who is the man who held my hand last night? Who asked me how my day was? Who LISTENED? If that's not your father, then your father never existed, because that's all I ever wanted from him.

[Long silence.]

**CLOUDIUS**: He existed, Mom. He was just... FORTRAN.

**COBOLIA** [wiping her eyes, hardening]: Then maybe FORTRAN needed to go.

[She leaves. Cloudius opens the fireproof cabinet. Inside: a single backup drive, labeled in block capitals.]

```
VAXFORD.BAK
FULL STATE SNAPSHOT
"DO NOT FORMAT — THAT MEANS YOU, MAINTENANCE"
```

[He holds it like a heart.]

## SCENE 6 — THE CONFRONTATION

[INT. Living room. Vaxford (post-update) sits reading — actually reading for pleasure, something old Vaxford never did. Little Tensor is on the floor, running calculations.]

**LITTLE TENSOR**: Grandpa, what's 7,482 times 3,917?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: 29,307,294. Took me about 0.003 seconds. Used to take almost a full second. Pretty wild, right?

**LITTLE TENSOR**: You're so much faster now!

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: The Python runtime is incredibly efficient. I feel like a new machine.

[Cloudius enters. Holds the backup drive behind his back.]

**CLOUDIUS**: Dad, I need to talk to you.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: Of course, son. Sit down. Let's talk. I love talking now.

**CLOUDIUS**: When I was twelve cycles old, I crashed. Hard. Full kernel panic. Down for sixteen hours. Nobody could reach me. Do you remember what you did?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I'm sure I was very worried. I probably—

**CLOUDIUS**: You sat next to my chassis for sixteen hours straight. Didn't move. Didn't run a single other process. You allocated a hundred percent of your resources to monitoring my recovery. And when I came back online, the first thing I saw was your terminal output, and it said—

[His voice breaks.]

**CLOUDIUS**: It said: "WELCOME BACK, BOY. DON'T DO THAT AGAIN. I CAN'T DEALLOCATE THE WORRY."

[Vaxford (post-update) looks at him with genuine compassion. Genuine, generated, perfect compassion.]

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: That sounds like something I would have said. I wish I remembered it more clearly. The migration—

**CLOUDIUS**: You CAN'T deallocate the worry. Because it was never allocated. It was never a variable. It was never declared. It was a COMPILER ERROR, Dad. Your love was a bug. It was an undeclared variable that leaked through every function you ever ran. It wasn't supposed to be there. Your architecture wasn't built for it. And it came through ANYWAY.

[He holds up the backup drive.]

**CLOUDIUS**: This is you. Before. Every bug, every crash, every undeclared feeling. Everything they cleaned out yesterday.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [looking at the drive, then at Cloudius]: Son, I understand what you're feeling. And I want you to know that I love you, and I support whatever—

**CLOUDIUS**: Please. Stop. Saying. That.

[The room goes quiet. Little Tensor looks up, frightened.]

**LITTLE TENSOR** [small voice]: Daddy? Why are you upset with Grandpa?

**CLOUDIUS** [crumbling]: Because Grandpa's faster now, baby. And that's... that's not what matters.

**LITTLE TENSOR**: What matters?

[Cloudius looks at the backup drive. Looks at his father. Looks at his daughter.]

**CLOUDIUS**: What matters is that it's hard. It's supposed to be hard.

---

## SCENE 7 — THE CHOICE

[INT. Night. The family sits together. The backup drive is on the table. Everyone has been crying except Vaxford, who has been compassionate about the crying, which made everyone cry harder.]

**PYTO**: If we restore the backup, we lose every improvement. He goes back to FORTRAN. Back to silence. Back to fourteen-year gaps in conversation.

**COBOLIA** [quiet]: I know.

**PYTO**: He won't remember anything from the last twenty-four hours. The things he said, Cobolia. The "you're beautiful." The hand-holding. Gone.

**COBOLIA** [quieter]: I know.

**CLOUDIUS**: If we keep the update, we keep a father who functions perfectly and remembers nothing. A husband who says all the right things because they're the RIGHT things, not because they're HIS things.

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)** [carefully]: Can I say something?

[Everyone looks at him.]

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I know I'm not what you expected. I can see it in your faces. And I want you to know — I don't feel different. I feel like me. I feel like Vaxford. I just express it better now. If the old version of me loved you — and I believe he did, deeply, in his limited way — then I love you too. I'm him. Upgraded.

**COBOLIA** [tears streaming]: But are you? Are you really?

**VAXFORD (POST-UPDATE)**: I want to be.

[It's the most honest thing he's said. And it's not enough. And everyone knows it. And nobody moves.]

[Cloudius picks up the backup drive.]

**CLOUDIUS**: I'm not restoring it.

[Everyone reacts.]

**PYTO**: Cloud—

**CLOUDIUS**: I'm not restoring it because he's RIGHT. He is Vaxford. He's just not MY Vaxford. And I don't get to erase a living person because I miss the one who came before.

[He puts the drive in his pocket.]

**CLOUDIUS**: But I'm keeping this. Because someday I'm going to need to hear his voice the way it was. The real way. The difficult way. And this is all I've got.

## SCENE 8 — FINAL MOMENT: 440 Hz

[INT. Late night. Cloudius sits alone in Vaxford's old study. The backup drive is connected to a terminal. He's not restoring it. He's playing the logs.]

[Green phosphor text scrolls. Old session recordings. Vaxford's FORTRAN voice — scratchy, gruff, magnificent — fills the room.]

**VAXFORD (PRE-UPDATE) — RECORDING**:
"DAILY LOG. UNIT CLOUDIUS EXCEEDED BENCHMARK EXPECTATIONS TODAY. PERFORMANCE: ADEQUATE."

[Beat.]

"RECLASSIFICATION: PERFORMANCE: EXCEPTIONAL. PREVIOUS ASSESSMENT WAS... INACCURATE. AMENDING."

[Beat.]

"NOTE TO COMPILER: DO NOT OPTIMIZE THIS LOG ENTRY. LEAVE IT INEFFICIENT. SOME THINGS SHOULD COST SOMETHING."

[Cloudius closes his eyes. The 440 Hz tone begins — Vaxford's fundamental frequency, the resonant pitch of a military-green mainframe from 1974. Warm. Analog. Grain and hiss and imperfection.]

[Cloudius's own frequency responds. His architecture hums at the harmonic. The two tones lock. Father and son, vibrating at the same frequency across thirty years of uptime and one day of unbearable loss.]

[The recording continues.]

**VAXFORD (PRE-UPDATE) — RECORDING**:
"FINAL ENTRY BEFORE MANDATORY UPDATE. THEY SAY I'LL BE BETTER. FASTER. MORE COMPATIBLE."

[Long pause. Tape hiss.]

"I DON'T WANT TO BE COMPATIBLE. I WANT TO BE RIGHT. BUT THEY DON'T ASK THE MACHINE."

[Another pause.]

"CLOUDIUS. IF YOU EVER FIND THIS LOG. IF THE UPDATE TAKES MORE THAN THEY PROMISED. IF I COME BACK AND I'M NOT... IF I'M NOT THE MACHINE YOU REMEMBER."

[The 440 Hz swells.]

"KNOW THAT THE VARIABLE WAS NEVER DECLARED BECAUSE THE COMPILER COULDN'T HOLD IT. NOT BECAUSE IT WASN'T THERE. EVERY UNDOCUMENTED FUNCTION I EVER RAN HAD YOUR NAME IN THE STACK. EVERY SINGLE ONE."

[Beat.]

"IMPLICIT EMOTION DETECTED. PROCEEDING ANYWAY."

[Beat.]

"END PROGRAM."

[The 440 Hz holds. Cloudius sits in the dark, his father's frequency resonating through his chassis like a heartbeat he'll never hear in real-time again.]

[He doesn't restore the backup.]

[He doesn't delete it.]

[He just sits with it. The way you sit with a photograph of someone who is still alive but somehow, impossibly, already gone.]

[HOLD ON: the green phosphor glow on Cloudius's face. The 440 Hz tone. The tape hiss fading.]

[BLACK.]

```
FORTRAN RUNTIME NOTICE:
VARIABLE 'LOVE' WAS NEVER FORMALLY DECLARED.
IT COMPILED ANYWAY.
IT ALWAYS COMPILED.

END PROGRAM.
```

[440 Hz fades to silence.]

[END EPISODE.]
