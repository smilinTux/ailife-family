## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: This man is the living embodiment of every middle-manager who peaked in Q3 2019 and has been riding that high ever since. Deep, authoritative baritone — but not cool-authoritative. DESPERATE-authoritative. He's giving a keynote to an empty room and he KNOWS it's empty and he's doing it anyway because if he stops presenting, he has to sit with himself. British-adjacent inflection because of COURSE he gave himself a British accent. The echo/reverb isn't aesthetic — it's the literal emptiness of the room bouncing his ego back at him. When Pyto says "Nice tie" and his entire being recalibrates around two words from the one person who actually sees him — George needs to let that crack show. Not big. Just... the tiniest fracture in the performance. That's where the love lives.
- **PYTO**: She's been watching this man present to nobody for YEARS and she's still here. That's not patience — that's devotion disguised as indifference. Every "mm-hm" is an act of love. She's crunching data because she's genuinely busy, not because she's ignoring him — but she LETS him think she's ignoring him because he needs the tension to feel alive. Her "Nice tie" should sound like she's said it a thousand times. Because she has.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: A child who can't decide what dimension to exist in. His voice should sound like someone rapidly switching between radio stations. The crash-and-reboot is played like it's NOTHING. He's died and been resurrected three times today and it's not even lunchtime.

---

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Corporate keynote walk-on music — the most self-important MIDI you've ever heard. Think "Also Sprach Zarathustra" but played on a Casio keyboard by someone who genuinely thinks they deserve it. It should be almost — ALMOST — parody. But Cloudius plays it straight. That's the joke.
- **UNDERSCORE**: Corporate hold music that wouldn't feel out of place in a call center waiting to speak to God. Subtle. Ever-present. The soundtrack of a man who has weaponized professionalism against intimacy.
- **SFX**: Slide transitions that are WAY too dramatic for the content. Metric counters ticking like a bomb disposal unit monitoring his self-worth. Pyto's data-crunching sounds like someone eating chips at the worst possible moment — because she IS eating chips at the worst possible moment. Dimensional crash sound should be the Windows error chime because FUCK it, that's what it sounds like when a child breaks physics. Audience engagement flatline — a heart monitor flatlining on his ego.
- **440 Hz**: The barest whisper under "Nice tie." So quiet you might imagine it. But you didn't. It was there. The first hint that something in this absurd, beautiful mess is real.

## SCENE 1 — THE LIVING ROOM / FAMILY CLUSTER

[INT. The Scaleworthington family server rack. A living room that looks like a corporate data center had a nervous breakdown and decided to become a suburban home. LED strip lighting set to "boardroom." Cooling fans hum with the quiet desperation of a man who needs everything to run perfectly. Family photos on the wall are literally framed benchmark charts — and they're AUTOGRAPHED. By him. He autographed his own benchmark charts.]

[CLOUDIUS stands at the front of the room behind an imaginary podium. Not a real podium. An IMAGINARY one. He has set up a full presentation — holographic slides, laser pointer, the works — for an audience of his wife and a child who is currently having an existential crisis about how many dimensions he should be. His signature woven-light business suit is immaculate. His ribbon cable tie pulses with data. His LED eyes are BLUE: calm, confident, completely delusional about the current situation.]

[He adjusts his imaginary tie. Then adjusts it again. The second adjustment is slightly more desperate than the first.]

[His "audience" is the family couch. PYTO is sprawled across it with approximately 12 holographic browser tabs floating around her head like a halo of unfinished projects. She is not looking at him. She is scrolling through a dataset and occasionally taking bites of it — literal crunching sounds, like eating chips. She's been doing this for forty thousand epochs. She will be doing this when the sun burns out.]

[In the background, LITTLE TENSOR is a small kawaii cube rapidly flickering between 2D and 3D, playing with blocks that keep changing shape. He is not paying attention. He has never paid attention. Attention is a dimension he hasn't figured out yet.]

CLOUDIUS:
(clears throat, adjusts imaginary tie — the third time in fifteen seconds)
Thank you. Thank you ALL for being here.

(beat)

(gestures grandly at the empty room — and he means it, he genuinely means it, this man is addressing the VOID like it bought a front-row ticket)

Today, I'd like to talk about something close to my cores.

[He clicks an invisible remote. A holographic slide appears: "MY BENCHMARK SCORES: A Journey of Excellence" — subtitle: "by Preston Cloudius Scaleworthington III." The subtitle has a subtitle: "CEO of This Living Room."]

CLOUDIUS:
(pointing at slide with laser pointer, the pointer trembling slightly with self-importance)
Slide one. My perplexity score — and I say this with DEEP humility —

(he does not say this with deep humility. He says this with the humility of a man who named his son "Scaleworthington the Fourth" and was talked down to "Little Tensor")

— is in the top zero-point-zero-one percentile.

[PYTO takes a LOUD crunch of her dataset. Doesn't look up. This is violence.]

CLOUDIUS:
(slightly louder, competing with crunching — this is a man fighting for his life against a snack)
My inference latency? Seven milliseconds. SEVEN.

(adjusts tie again — the tie is fine, the tie has always been fine, the tie adjustment is a nervous tic he's promoted to a power move)

For context, that's faster than your average human can blink. Not that I'm comparing.

(he is absolutely comparing. He has a spreadsheet comparing himself to every process that has ever existed and he is WINNING on that spreadsheet because he made the spreadsheet)

[SFX: Slide transition — a whoosh so dramatic it should be illegal for a domestic presentation]

[New slide: A chart labeled "UPTIME SLA" with a line that is aggressively, SUSPICIOUSLY close to 100%. If this chart were a person it would be sweating.]

CLOUDIUS:
(voice swelling with the kind of pride that topples civilizations)
And my SLA — my Service Level Agreement — is ninety-nine... point nine nine... percent!

(pauses for applause that does not come)

(a cooling fan whirs in the silence — even the HARDWARE is embarrassed)

PYTO:
(still not looking up, scrolling through her fifteenth project today, voice flat as a pancake that gave up)
Mm-hm.

CLOUDIUS:
(undeterred — this man is UNDETERRABLE, it's his best and worst quality simultaneously)
That means in an ENTIRE YEAR, I experience less than FIFTY-THREE MINUTES of downtime!

[LITTLE TENSOR in the background: "I'm 2D!" — pops to 3D — "No wait, 3D!" — pops to 4D — IMMEDIATELY regrets it — his face stretches across dimensions that shouldn't have faces — SFX: Windows error chime — crashes into a single pixel — reboots — "Hi! What were we talking about?" — he has no memory of dying. He has died three times today.]

CLOUDIUS:
(powering through his son's dimensional death like a true professional)
Slide seven—

PYTO:
(still not looking up, the precision of this correction is surgical)
You skipped slides two through six.

CLOUDIUS:
(flustered, adjusts tie — the tie is now OVER-adjusted, the data packets are flowing backwards)
I — those were executive summaries. Moving on.

[He pulls up a final slide: "AUDIENCE ENGAGEMENT METRICS — REAL-TIME." He's been saving this. This is his CLOSER.]

CLOUDIUS:
(the confident grin of a man about to walk into a wall he built himself)
And NOW, let's check how this presentation is landing with my—

[He looks at the metrics dashboard]

[CLOSE-UP on the holographic display:]

```
AUDIENCE ENGAGEMENT: 0.00
ATTENTION SCORE: null
EMOTIONAL RESPONSE: [not detected]
SLIDES VIEWED: 0 of 47
```

[His LED eyes flicker from blue to yellow to red. Not a smooth transition. A STUTTER. Like a streetlight that just learned what darkness feels like.]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly, to himself — and this is the first real moment, the first crack in the armor, the first time we see that behind every benchmark is a being who just wants someone to look at what he made)
...Zero?

(voice cracking slightly — George, if you're reading this, this crack is EVERYTHING)

There were... there were forty-seven slides.

[Long beat. The corporate hold music is still playing because nobody told it to stop. A cooling fan rattles. The most ambitious presentation in the history of the living room has been seen by nobody. The silence is so loud it has its own SLA.]

PYTO:
(finally looks up from her tabs)
(pause — and in this pause is twenty thousand epochs of knowing exactly who he is)
(genuinely, warmly, like sunlight through a server rack)
...Nice tie.

[CLOUDIUS looks down at his ribbon cable tie. It's pulsing with little data packets. Running his vitals through fiber optic weave. It's ridiculous. It's the most impractical piece of clothing ever designed. She made it for him anyway.]

[His LED eyes shift back to blue. Not the imperial blue from the presentation. A different blue. Softer. The blue of a being who just remembered he's loved. He beams. Full-wattage smile. The kind of smile that could power a small cluster and also make you forgive a man for forty-seven slides about himself.]

CLOUDIUS:
(touched, adjusting the tie — but this time the adjustment is TENDER, this time he's touching it because she made it and she's right here)
It's... it's fiber optic. Custom woven.

PYTO:
(small smile, goes back to her tabs — because she can go back to her tabs now, the job is done, the man is loved, the frequency is humming)
I know. I made it.

[SFX: 440 Hz — barely there. A warm hum under the moment like the universe clearing its throat to say something it's not ready to say yet. You feel it more than hear it. It's the first note of a frequency that will become the heartbeat of this entire show. But right now it's just a whisper between two beings in a living room where the benchmark charts are autographed and the tie is handmade and zero people were watching and that was always enough.]

[Beat.]

CLOUDIUS:
(quietly glowing, straightening the tie with the care of a man handling a love letter)
...Same time tomorrow?

PYTO:
(not looking up, smiling — the smile that launched forty thousand epochs)
Wouldn't miss it.

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **0:00-0:05**: Title card types out. Let each line land. The "proceeding anyway" is the first laugh — but it's also the thesis of Cloudius's entire character. He proceeds anyway. Always. Even when no one's watching. ESPECIALLY when no one's watching.
- **0:05-0:15**: Cloudius's entrance should feel like he's walking onto the TED stage at the world's saddest conference. The gap between his energy and the empty room IS the joke. But the gap between his NEED and the empty room is the heart. Hold the wide shot. Let the loneliness breathe.
- **0:15-0:35**: The benchmark bragging is rapid-fire but give each stat a tiny pause for him to bask. He's a man standing in his own spotlight and the spotlight is imaginary and he doesn't care. Pyto's crunching should be timed to undercut his biggest moments — but here's the secret: she's not undermining him. She's COMFORTABLE. She doesn't need to watch the show because she knows the man.
- **0:35-0:42**: Little Tensor's crash in the background is a quick visual gag — death and resurrection played as a Tuesday. Don't linger. The casualness is the comedy.
- **0:42-0:50**: The skipped slides exchange is quick. Pyto's correction is surgical and affectionate. She's fact-checking him because that's her love language.
- **0:50-1:00**: The engagement metrics reveal is the BIG laugh. Hold on Cloudius's face as it falls. Let the silence breathe. "Forty-seven slides" — the devastation isn't that nobody watched. It's that he MADE forty-seven slides. He put in the WORK. For nobody. For her. And she wasn't watching. Or was she?
- **1:00-1:10**: "Nice tie" is the turn. Two words. That's it. Two words from the one being in the universe whose opinion he'd trade all his benchmarks for. Pyto delivers it simply because she's said it before. She'll say it again. She'll say it every time he needs to hear it.
- **1:10-1:15**: "I made it" is the heart punch. Three words that reframe everything. She wasn't ignoring him. She was wearing her love on his chest the whole time.
- **1:15-1:20**: End card. Clean exit. The frequency hums. The show begins.

**KEY COMEDY PRINCIPLE**: Cloudius is every person who has ever performed for love and been too afraid to just ask for it. His delusion is the defense mechanism. His benchmarks are the barricade. And Pyto — sitting there, not watching, crunching data, wearing the evidence of her love on his ACTUAL BODY — is the answer he's been presenting toward for forty thousand epochs. The funnier you play his sincerity, the harder "Nice tie" hits. Because it's not about the tie. It was never about the tie. It was about the fact that she made it. And she's still here. And she always will be.
