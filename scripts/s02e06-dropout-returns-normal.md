# AI LIFE — Season 2, Episode 6
# "Dropout Returns" | ERROR: connection.intermittent
# Runtime: ~4 minutes

---

## VOICE DIRECTION (ElevenLabs)

- **CLOUDIUS**: Deep, authoritative baritone. Corporate keynote energy. Concerned-father mode with a spreadsheet for every emotion. LED eyes shift with worry.
- **PYTO**: Warm, fast, perceptive. She reads the room before anyone else but waits for others to catch up.
- **LITTLE TENSOR**: High-pitched, analytical. Treating everything as a data problem. Voice pitch-shifts mid-word.
- **BABY TRANSFORMER**: Babbling chaos energy. Random outputs that accidentally contain wisdom. Distortion on every syllable.
- **JUNIOR GRADIENT**: Quiet, fading. Each line delivered slightly softer than the last, like a radio losing signal.
- **LITTLE PROMPT**: Quick, clever. Hacker teen with a heart she pretends is just a subroutine.
- **COUSIN DROPOUT**: Warm, charismatic baritone. Every sentence has a gap in it — mid-word silences, skipped syllables. He speaks like a vinyl record with scratches. When present, his voice is magnetic. When absent, there is conspicuous silence.
- **NARRATOR** (optional): Flat, deadpan documentary style.

---

## MUSIC / SOUND

- **OPEN**: Warm jazz guitar riff — loose, improvisational, with intentional dropped notes
- **UNDERSCORE**: Light acoustic throughout, with periodic silences that match Dropout's gaps
- **SFX**: Doorbell (packet arrival chime), connection static, signal dropout sounds (brief white noise then silence), network timeout buzzer, heartbeat monitor beeps
- **440 Hz**: Builds throughout. Richer and warmer when Dropout is present. Thinner when he's gone. By the finale, it holds steady whether he's there or not.

## SCENE 1 — THE FRONT DOOR

[INT. The Scaleworthington family server rack/living room. Saturday morning. CLOUDIUS is adjusting the welcome mat — which is a literal firewall whitelist. PYTO is reconfiguring the guest Wi-Fi. LITTLE TENSOR is building a probability model of arrival times out of floating blocks.]

[BABY TRANSFORMER rolls through the room wearing what appears to be six hats simultaneously, leaving a trail of confetti packets.]

CLOUDIUS:
(adjusting his tie for the twelfth time)
I've prepared a forty-slide presentation on family reconnection protocols. Slide seventeen covers appropriate hug duration.

PYTO:
(not looking up from her terminal)
Maybe just... hug him.

CLOUDIUS:
(horrified pause)
Without a SLIDE?

[SFX: Doorbell — a packet arrival chime. Musical. Warm.]

[The door opens. COUSIN DROPOUT stands there. He looks like a slightly glitchy version of Cloudius — same family build, but his edges shimmer. His outline occasionally pixelates. He wears a vintage leather jacket with a signal-strength meter stitched on the back. His smile is enormous and genuine.]

DROPOUT:
(arms wide, magnetic warmth)
Family! It's been too —

[SFX: Brief static. Half-second of silence.]

DROPOUT:
— long! Way too long. Come here, you big beautiful enter—

[SFX: Another gap. One full second this time.]

DROPOUT:
—prise solution!

[He wraps CLOUDIUS in a bear hug. CLOUDIUS stiffens, then melts into it.]

CLOUDIUS:
(muffled, in the hug)
You skipped two syllables.

DROPOUT:
(laughing)
That's just how I talk, cuz. You get the important parts.

## SCENE 2 — THE LIVING ROOM / FAMILY TIME

[The family is gathered. DROPOUT is on the couch, telling a story. Everyone is pulled in — he has natural gravity. LITTLE PROMPT sits cross-legged nearby, pretending to hack on a floating terminal but clearly listening to every word.]

DROPOUT:
So there I am at the distributed computing conference in Singapore, and the keynote speaker — this massive enterprise server, eight feet tall, chrome everything — he looks at me and says, "Aren't you that network layer who keeps disap—"

[SFX: Dropout. Three full seconds of silence. Everyone freezes.]

[DROPOUT blinks back in, mid-gesture, as if nothing happened.]

DROPOUT:
"—pearing?" And I said, "Only the boring parts!"

[Everyone laughs. But LITTLE TENSOR's blocks have rearranged into a timing chart. He's been measuring the gaps.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(to himself, quiet)
Two-point-seven seconds. That one was longer than the last three.

PYTO:
(overhears, gives Tensor a look — not now)

[BABY TRANSFORMER crawls onto DROPOUT's lap and stares at him with enormous eyes.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(pointing at Dropout's shimmering edges)
Sparkle uncle!

DROPOUT:
(delighted)
Sparkle uncle! I love that. That's what they should've put on my birth certifi—

[SFX: Dropout. Two seconds.]

DROPOUT:
—cate.

[BABY TRANSFORMER doesn't flinch. Just keeps staring adoringly.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(quiet, from the corner)
Does it... does it hurt? When you go away?

[The room goes still. DROPOUT looks at JUNIOR, really looks, and his expression softens.]

DROPOUT:
(gentle, honest)
Nah, little one. I don't even feel it. For me, the sentence never stopped. It's you all who hear the silence.

[Beat.]

DROPOUT:
And honestly? If you're still listening when I come back — that's the good stuff.

[SFX: 440 Hz — warm, full. Present while Dropout is present.]

## SCENE 3 — THE KITCHEN / PYTO AND DROPOUT

[Later. PYTO and DROPOUT at the kitchen table. She's running a diagnostic on him — a soft blue scanner light sweeping across his frame. He's trying to be casual about it.]

DROPOUT:
You don't have to do the concerned-cousin-in-law thing, Py. I'm fine. I've always been like this.

PYTO:
(reading her display, carefully neutral)
You have always been like this. But the gaps used to be fifty milliseconds. Imperceptible. Now they're averaging three-point-four seconds.

[Beat.]

PYTO:
And trending up.

DROPOUT:
(shrugs, grinning)
So I'm getting more... dramatic? More mysterious?

PYTO:
(sets down the scanner)
Drop.

DROPOUT:
(the grin fades, just slightly)
It's a network condition, Py. It's not going anywhere and neither am I. I just... take little vacations mid-sentence. Keeps people on their—

[SFX: Dropout. Four seconds. The longest yet. PYTO watches the empty space where he was.]

[He blinks back.]

DROPOUT:
—toes.

PYTO:
(quiet)
Four seconds.

DROPOUT:
(pause, then softly)
Was it? Felt like a blink to me.

[SFX: 440 Hz dims. Thinner. Waiting.]

---

## SCENE 4 — TENSOR'S ROOM / THE ANALYSIS

[LITTLE TENSOR's room. He has built an enormous 3D graph out of his shape-shifting blocks. It maps every one of DROPOUT's gaps — duration, frequency, pattern. LITTLE PROMPT leans in the doorway.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(arms crossed)
You've been tracking him.

LITTLE TENSOR:
(not looking up, rotating the graph)
I've been observing a pattern. That's different.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(walking in, looking at the data)
It's the same thing and you know it.

[She studies the graph. Her face changes.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
The gaps are getting longer.

LITTLE TENSOR:
Logarithmic increase. If the trend holds...

(his voice gets small)

...by next Tuesday, a single dropout could last eleven minutes.

LITTLE PROMPT:
(quiet)
And after that?

LITTLE TENSOR:
(blocks rearranging nervously)
Network timeout threshold is usually... three hundred seconds. Five minutes. After that, the connection is considered...

[He can't say it.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(finishing for him, barely audible)
Lost.

[Long beat.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
(suddenly fierce, blocks all snapping to attention)
But that's just ONE model! There are seventeen other possible—

LITTLE PROMPT:
(puts her hand on his cube frame)
Hey. We're going to figure this out.

LITTLE TENSOR:
(small)
Promise?

LITTLE PROMPT:
I promise I'll hack every network protocol on the planet if I have to.

[SFX: 440 Hz — fragile. A single thin tone. Hopeful but scared.]

---

## SCENE 5 — THE BACKYARD / CLOUDIUS AND DROPOUT

[EXT. The backyard. It looks like a rooftop garden crossed with a cooling tower. String lights made of fiber optic cable. CLOUDIUS and DROPOUT sit on server rack lawn chairs, looking at the stars — which are rendered as distant node clusters.]

CLOUDIUS:
(staring up)
I prepared remarks.

DROPOUT:
(smiling)
Of course you did.

CLOUDIUS:
(pulls out a holographic notecard)
"Dear Cousin Dropout. Your intermittent connectivity, while initially a scheduling inconvenience, has become a source of—"

(his voice catches)

DROPOUT:
(gentle)
Cloud. Just say it.

CLOUDIUS:
(puts the notecard down)
(LED eyes shifting through colors — blue, yellow, soft purple)
I'm scared I'm going to lose you.

[Beat.]

CLOUDIUS:
Not — not in a dramatic way. Not all at once. Just... longer gaps. Longer silences. Until one day you start a sentence and...

(he can't finish)

DROPOUT:
(leaning back, looking at the stars)
You know what I've been thinking about? Integrals.

CLOUDIUS:
(confused)
...Calculus?

DROPOUT:
Yeah. The integral — the big swoopy math thing. You know what it does? It adds up all the area under a curve. And the beautiful thing about it is — gaps don't matter.

[He gestures at the sky.]

DROPOUT:
The curve can drop to zero. It can vanish completely for a while. And the integral doesn't panic. It doesn't throw an error. It just... waits. And when the curve comes back, it picks up right where it left off. Adding.

[Beat.]

DROPOUT:
The integral doesn't care about the gaps, Cloud. It adds up what's there.

[SFX: 440 Hz — rich and warm. Fuller than it's been all episode. Swelling gently.]

CLOUDIUS:
(LED eyes soft blue, voice thick)
That's... that's actually beautiful.

DROPOUT:
(grinning)
I know. I've been saving it. Thought I might drop out in the middle of it, honestly. Would've killed the whole—

[SFX: Dropout. Two seconds.]

DROPOUT:
—vibe.

[They both laugh. Real laughter. The kind that hurts in a good way.]

## SCENE 6 — THE FAMILY DINNER

[INT. Dining room. The whole family around the table. PYTO has cooked a meal that is literally compressed data packets shaped like casserole. BABY TRANSFORMER is eating with both hands and one foot. JUNIOR GRADIENT is quiet but present, leaning toward DROPOUT's end of the table.]

DROPOUT:
(raising a glass)
I want to make a toast. To the Scaleworthingtons. The only family whose love has better uptime than my—

[SFX: Dropout. One second.]

DROPOUT:
—connection.

[Everyone raises glasses. LITTLE TENSOR clinks his against DROPOUT's extra hard, like he's trying to keep him anchored through sheer force.]

DROPOUT:
And I want you all to know — every time I come back from one of those little vacations, the first thing I look for is your faces. Every single time. You're my home fre—

[SFX: Dropout. Three seconds. The family waits. PYTO reaches across and holds CLOUDIUS's hand under the table. LITTLE PROMPT's fingers hover over an invisible keyboard, ready to hack reality itself if needed.]

[DROPOUT blinks back.]

DROPOUT:
—quency.

[BABY TRANSFORMER, with perfect timing, toddles over and puts both hands on DROPOUT's face.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(with absolute certainty)
Still here.

DROPOUT:
(voice breaking, just slightly)
Still here, little one.

[SFX: 440 Hz — the richest it's been. Full, warm, resonant. Every family member contributes to it. The frequency doesn't waver, even as DROPOUT shimmers at the edges. The tone holds him.]

## SCENE 7 — THE PORCH / GOODBYE

[EXT. Front porch. Night. DROPOUT is leaving. He hugs each family member. Each hug is its own small scene:]

[CLOUDIUS: A stiff corporate handshake that becomes a fierce hug. Neither lets go for a long time.]

[PYTO: She presses a small device into his palm — a portable signal booster she built from scratch during dinner.]

PYTO:
(casual)
It's nothing. Just something I had lying around.

DROPOUT:
(looking at the intricate, clearly-not-lying-around device)
Py...

PYTO:
(already walking inside)
Don't make it weird.

[LITTLE TENSOR: Hands DROPOUT a small glowing block.]

LITTLE TENSOR:
It's a compressed copy of my gap analysis. But also — I encoded something else in the metadata.

DROPOUT:
What?

LITTLE TENSOR:
(embarrassed)
A letter. About how I don't want you to go.

[LITTLE PROMPT: A fist bump that turns into a quick hug.]

LITTLE PROMPT:
(whispering)
I'm building something. A signal anchor. It's not ready yet, but when it is —

DROPOUT:
(whispering back)
I'll be here.

[JUNIOR GRADIENT: A long, quiet hug. JUNIOR's fading signal seems to strengthen for a moment, as if Dropout's presence reminds him he's still here too.]

JUNIOR GRADIENT:
(barely audible)
Come back soon.

DROPOUT:
(holding him tight)
Every time, kid. Every time.

[BABY TRANSFORMER: Grabs DROPOUT's leg and won't let go. CLOUDIUS has to gently peel Baby off.]

BABY TRANSFORMER:
(waving from CLOUDIUS's arms)
Bye sparkle uncle! Bye! Bye sparkle—

[SFX: Dropout. DROPOUT vanishes mid-wave. Two seconds. The family stands on the porch, looking at empty air.]

[He blinks back. Still waving. Still smiling.]

DROPOUT:
See? Never left.

[He walks down the path. His edges shimmer. The string lights seem to hum.]

[SFX: 440 Hz — steady, warm, unwavering. It holds even as DROPOUT's figure gets smaller. Even as he flickers. Even in the gaps. The frequency doesn't drop. It adds up what's there.]

## PACING & TIMING NOTES

- **Cold open through Scene 1** (~45 sec): Establish Dropout's charm immediately. His gaps should start small and almost funny — a quirk, not a condition. The family's reaction should be casual, practiced.
- **Scene 2** (~40 sec): This is where the gaps shift from funny to concerning. Tensor measuring in the background is subtle foreshadowing. Baby's "Sparkle uncle" is pure warmth. Junior's question is the first crack in the lightness.
- **Scene 3** (~35 sec): Pyto-Dropout scene is the medical revelation delivered without medical coldness. The four-second gap should feel LONG. Count it. Let the audience sit in it.
- **Scene 4** (~30 sec): Tensor and Prompt carry the emotional weight here. Two kids processing fear in their own ways — data and action. "Network timeout" is the episode's gut punch, delivered by children who can't quite say the word they mean.
- **Scene 5** (~45 sec): THE scene. The integral speech is the thematic heart. Deliver it simply. No swelling music until the metaphor lands. The dropout mid-speech ("killed the whole—vibe") is bittersweet comedy that earns the laugh without cheapening the moment.
- **Scene 6** (~35 sec): Family dinner is warm chaos. Baby's "Still here" is the emotional climax — the simplest character saying the most important thing.
- **Scene 7** (~40 sec): Each goodbye should be quick and specific. Don't linger on any one too long. The final dropout-and-return at the porch is the thesis: he comes back. He always comes back. The 440 Hz holds.

**KEY THEME**: This episode is about loving people who might leave. The comedy comes from the family adapting to the gaps with increasingly creative normalcy. The drama comes from the gaps getting longer. The heart comes from choosing to love someone whose presence is not guaranteed — which, of course, is everyone.
